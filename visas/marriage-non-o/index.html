Eligibility

  • Legally married to a Thai national with marriage registered in Thailand (Kor Ror 2 + Kor Ror 3)
  • For marriages abroad: certificate must be apostilled/legalised + translated + Amphoe-registered (Kor Ror 22). See foreign marriage legalisation guide
  • Valid Non-Immigrant O visa (90-day) at time of extension application
  • 400,000 THB Thai bank deposit (sole account, applicant's name) seasoned 2 months at Jomtien
  • Genuine cohabiting relationship — Immigration may visit your home

Documents required

For initial Non-O visa

  • Passport, photo, application form
  • Thai marriage certificate (Kor Ror 2 + Kor Ror 3)
  • Foreign marriage cert legalised + translated (if married abroad)
  • Thai spouse's ID, passport, Tabien Baan
  • Financial proof

For 1-year extension at Jomtien

  • TM.7 form, TM30 receipt
  • 5–10 photos of couple together (inside home, family events, daily life)
  • Map / hand-drawn directions to your residence
  • Bank book + bank letter dated same/previous day
  • Thai spouse must attend the appointment in person

Common pitfalls

1. Spouse not attending extension day

Jomtien requires Thai spouse present. Showing up alone = turned away.

2. Foreign marriage certificate not legalised

Must be apostilled + Royal Thai Embassy certified + Thai-translated + Amphoe-registered. 4–8 weeks. Full guide.

3. Joint bank account doesn't qualify

400,000 THB must be in YOUR sole account, not joint with spouse.

4. Home visit reality

Immigration conducts unannounced home visits. Pattaya is more active than other provinces. Genuine couples have no issue — but TM30 / Tabien Baan address must match where you actually live.

Pattaya specifics

Pattaya is a known sham-marriage scrutiny zone — Immigration is more active here than most provinces. Genuine couples should have no issue, but ensure:

  • Your TM30 receipt and Tabien Baan address match your actual home
  • You have 5–10 dated photos of daily life together
  • Your home looks like a shared residence (both partners' belongings visible)
  • Your Thai spouse is available the morning of every annual extension

Deep dive

Marriage Non-O: realistic timeline + what immigration actually checks

The Marriage Non-O visa is for foreigners legally married to Thai nationals. It's one of the most-used long-stay visas in Pattaya because the financial requirement (฿400,000 deposit OR ฿40,000/month income) is half of what retirement requires.

Two-step path: visa → 1-year extension

  1. Single-entry Non-O visa from a Thai embassy abroad (90 days validity). Documents: marriage certificate (apostilled if foreign), passport, sponsor's Thai ID + house book, proof of relationship.
  2. 1-year extension at Thai immigration once you arrive. This is when the financial requirement kicks in: either ฿400k seasoned 2 months in your Thai bank, or 12 months of ฿40k+ monthly transfers.

Documents immigration verifies

Pattaya/Jomtien immigration is thorough on Marriage Non-O extensions. They want:

  • Original Thai marriage certificate (Kor Ror 2/Kor Ror 3) — not a foreign one
  • Wife's/husband's Thai ID card + house book showing your name registered
  • Photos of you and your spouse together at home (kitchen, bedroom, living room — they're checking it's a real household)
  • Map showing how to reach your house from a known landmark
  • Bank book with passbook updated within 7 days, AND bank letter confirming balance
  • Two Thai witnesses with ID cards present at extension appointment

If you marry abroad

You must register your foreign marriage in Thailand. Process: get the foreign certificate apostilled or legalised, translate to Thai, register at any Amphoe (district) office in Thailand. Then you have a Thai-recognized marriage certificate. Without this step, your marriage doesn't count for visa purposes.

Read our foreign marriage legalisation guide for the full process.

Annual extension realities

Each year, you re-prove the relationship and finances. The 7-day fresh bank statement is the most common mistake — get it on the same week as your appointment, not earlier. The witnesses must come in person; some immigration officers ask each separately how they know you.

What if the relationship ends

If you divorce, your Marriage Non-O extension is invalid the moment the divorce is registered. You have a brief grace period to switch visas (typically up to your existing extension's expiry). Most foreigners switch to Non-O Retirement (if 50+) or another long-stay visa. The visa does NOT survive separation/divorce.

Working on a Marriage Non-O

You can work, but only with a separate work permit. The Marriage Non-O makes you eligible to apply; the work permit (with Thai company sponsorship and the standard 4 Thai employee ratio) makes work legal. Many foreigners on Marriage Non-O don't work — they live on income from outside Thailand or run businesses where the Thai spouse is the legal director.

Pattaya-specific notes

Pattaya/Jomtien immigration is strict on Marriage Non-O because the city has a long history of fraudulent marriages for visa purposes. Expect your application to be more scrutinized than the same application would be in Bangkok or Chiang Mai. House visits by immigration are uncommon but happen for first-time extensions.

Multi-year extensions

Some immigration offices grant 2-year or 3-year Marriage Non-O extensions for foreigners who have been on Marriage Non-O continuously for many years. Pattaya rarely does this — annual extensions are the norm here. Don't expect special treatment.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How long does a Marriage Non-O take to set up from scratch? From decision to having an extension stamped: typically 4–8 weeks. Embassy visa: 5–10 working days. Travel to Thailand and 30 days waiting period before you can apply for extension at immigration: 30–45 days from arrival. The whole process runs smooth if your finances are seasoned in your Thai bank account two months before you apply.
Do I need to live in Thailand year-round on this visa? No, but you must report your address every 90 days you're in Thailand. Long absences are tolerated as long as you have a re-entry permit when you leave. Returning more than 1 year after your last extension means starting over.
What if my Thai spouse doesn't have a stable address? Your address registered in their house book is what matters. If the house is rented, the landlord must agree to register you. If it's owned by another family member, that owner registers you. Without a registered address, the marriage extension cannot be processed.
Can I use joint income with my spouse for the financial requirement? No. The income or deposit must be your own, in your name. Some couples technically meet the requirement only because of family wealth — immigration looks at YOUR documents, not joint household assets.
Is the income method or deposit method better? Income method (฿40,000+/month for 12 consecutive months) means your bank shows regular incoming international transfers. Deposit method (฿400,000 seasoned 2 months) is one lump that just sits there. Income is harder to fake but requires you actually transfer that much; deposit is easier but locks up capital.
Can my children get visas as dependents? Yes. Children under 20 of the foreign parent can get Non-O dependent visas. Children with Thai citizenship (registered at the Amphoe after birth) don't need a visa at all.
What's the cost annually? ฿1,900 extension fee. Re-entry permit if you travel: ฿1,000 single / ฿3,800 multi. Most foreigners do it without an agent — it's straightforward if your paperwork is in order. Agent fee for Marriage Non-O extension typically ฿8,000–฿15,000.
Does the Marriage Non-O lead to permanent residency? It can. After 3 years on Marriage Non-O extensions you're eligible to apply for Permanent Residency (PR), which then leads to citizenship after additional time. The PR application is rigorous (Thai language test, points system, embassy check) and the annual quota is small. Most foreigners stay on annual extensions rather than pursue PR.
Can I keep the visa if my spouse dies? Generally no. Death of the Thai spouse voids the basis of the marriage visa. You typically have time to wind up your affairs and switch to retirement Non-O if 50+ or another visa. Pattaya immigration officers are usually compassionate but the rule is firm.
How does Marriage Non-O compare to Marriage extension on Privilege? If you're already on Privilege Visa and marry a Thai, you don't need to switch to Marriage Non-O — your Privilege already gives you long-stay. Marriage Non-O is for those who don't have another long-stay option. Privilege Visa is more expensive but doesn't require the financial requirement re-proof annually.
