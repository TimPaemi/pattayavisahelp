Eligibility

  • Legally married to a Thai national with marriage registered in Thailand (Kor Ror 2 + Kor Ror 3)
  • For marriages abroad: certificate must be apostilled/legalised + translated + Amphoe-registered (Kor Ror 22). See foreign marriage legalisation guide
  • Valid Non-Immigrant O visa (90-day) at time of extension application
  • 400,000 THB Thai bank deposit (sole account, applicant's name) seasoned 2 months at Jomtien
  • Genuine cohabiting relationship — Immigration may visit your home

Documents required

For initial Non-O visa

  • Passport, photo, application form
  • Thai marriage certificate (Kor Ror 2 + Kor Ror 3)
  • Foreign marriage cert legalised + translated (if married abroad)
  • Thai spouse's ID, passport, Tabien Baan
  • Financial proof

For 1-year extension at Jomtien

  • TM.7 form, TM30 receipt
  • 5–10 photos of couple together (inside home, family events, daily life)
  • Map / hand-drawn directions to your residence
  • Bank book + bank letter dated same/previous day
  • Thai spouse must attend the appointment in person

Common pitfalls

1. Spouse not attending extension day

Jomtien requires Thai spouse present. Showing up alone = turned away.

2. Foreign marriage certificate not legalised

Must be apostilled + Royal Thai Embassy certified + Thai-translated + Amphoe-registered. 4–8 weeks. Full guide.

3. Joint bank account doesn't qualify

400,000 THB must be in YOUR sole account, not joint with spouse.

4. Home visit reality

Immigration conducts unannounced home visits. Pattaya is more active than other provinces. Genuine couples have no issue — but TM30 / Tabien Baan address must match where you actually live.

Pattaya specifics

Pattaya is a known sham-marriage scrutiny zone — Immigration is more active here than most provinces. Genuine couples should have no issue, but ensure:

  • Your TM30 receipt and Tabien Baan address match your actual home
  • You have 5–10 dated photos of daily life together
  • Your home looks like a shared residence (both partners' belongings visible)
  • Your Thai spouse is available the morning of every annual extension

Frequently asked questions

Does my Thai wife need to attend my extension?
At Jomtien — yes, almost always. Confirm before each appointment as rules can shift.
Can I work on a marriage visa?
Yes — once you have the 1-year extension based on marriage, you're eligible to apply for a work permit. Major advantage over retirement visa.
What if my marriage ends in divorce?
Visa basis ceases. You must switch to another visa or depart. No automatic grace period — consult an immigration lawyer immediately.
Married in UK / US / Australia — is my certificate valid?
Only after full legalisation chain: home country apostille → Royal Thai Embassy certification → Thai translation → Amphoe registration → Kor Ror 22. Country-by-country guide.
