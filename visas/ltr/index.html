The killer feature: tax exemption

For LTR-W, LTR-P, and LTR-T holders, foreign-source income remitted to Thailand is exempt from Thai personal income tax under Royal Decree 743.

The math vs Privilege: A $150k/year remitter staying 180+ days/year saves approximately $25,000/year in Thai tax on LTR vs Privilege. Over 10 years: $250,000 savings. This single fact is why LTR is the smart-money choice for high-income residents who qualify.

Universal eligibility (all categories)

  • Valid passport (6+ months remaining)
  • Health insurance covering at least USD 50,000 per year (must clearly state $50k in English on certificate), OR Thai social security, OR USD 100,000 deposit
  • Criminal background check from home country
  • Bank statement / financial certificate proving thresholds
  • All names spelled identically across every document (mismatches = delay)

Key benefits across all categories

  • 10 years validity (5 + 5 renewable)
  • Annual reporting only — no quarterly TM47
  • No re-entry permits needed (multiple-entry by design)
  • Digital work permit for LTR-T and LTR-H
  • Airport fast-track at Suvarnabhumi, Don Mueang, Phuket, Chiang Mai
  • Spouse + dependent children under 20 can join as LTR-O

Application process

  1. Determine the right category (most-common rejection cause is wrong category)
  2. Register on BOI LTR portal: ltr.boi.go.th
  3. Upload documents (consistent name spelling critical)
  4. BOI completeness check; document requests have hard deadlines
  5. BOI forwards to government agencies for qualification review
  6. Receive Letter of Qualification Endorsement (4–8 weeks typical)
  7. Pay 50,000 THB visa fee
  8. Visa issuance at TIESC (Bangkok) or Royal Thai Embassy abroad
  9. Digital Work Permit issued simultaneously where applicable

Total typical: 8–16 weeks (criminal background check is often the bottleneck).

Pattaya / EEC angle

The Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC), which includes Chonburi (Pattaya), Rayong, and Chachoengsao, is a key zone for LTR-H Highly-Skilled Professionals. Multinational employers in the EEC's targeted industries — automotive, smart electronics, food, robotics, aviation/logistics — increasingly use LTR-H instead of standard Non-B for senior foreign hires.

Pattaya-based LTR-W / LTR-P retirees benefit from the same 10-year continuity but visa issuance happens at TIESC in Bangkok (no Pattaya office).

Common pitfalls

1. Choosing the wrong category

#1 rejection cause. BOI evaluates technically — mismatched applications are rejected, not redirected.

2. LTR-T employer revenue test

Your foreign employer needs $150M+ revenue over 3 years. Most freelancers and small-company employees fail this — they should consider DTV instead.

3. Health insurance not stated in English

Policy must clearly state "USD 50,000 coverage" in English. THB-only or vague language gets rejected.

4. Inconsistent name spellings

Even minor differences ("Jonathan" vs "Jon") trigger clarification requests and weeks of delay. Match passport exactly.

Frequently asked questions

Which category gets the 17% flat tax rate?
Only LTR-H. Other categories (W, P, T) get foreign-source income exemption — different but also valuable.
Is my foreign pension really tax-exempt?
For LTR-P (Wealthy Pensioner) holders: yes. Foreign-source income including pension income is exempt from Thai personal income tax under Royal Decree 743 regardless of remittance amount.
Can I freelance for multiple clients on LTR?
LTR-T's digital work permit covers the qualifying foreign employer declared in your application. Multi-client freelance is a grey area — consult a BOI-experienced specialist.
What if I change jobs after getting LTR-H?
You must terminate the LTR and reapply with the new employer's documentation. Working for a new employer without reapplying is a violation.
Is LTR worth it vs annual Non-O-A retirement?
For retirees with $80k+/year income — almost certainly yes. LTR-P eliminates 800k seasoning, eliminates insurance struggles, exempts foreign income from Thai tax, removes 90-day reporting. The 50,000 THB fee is negligible vs decades of administrative relief and tax savings.

Deep dive

LTR Long-Term Resident: the four categories and who fits each

The Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa is Thailand's premium long-stay program, launched in 2022 by the BOI. It offers 10 years of stay (one 5-year visa, renewable for another 5), tax exemption on foreign-source income (under Royal Decree 743), and a faster track than any other Thai visa. There are four categories with different requirements.

The four LTR categories

1. Wealthy Global Citizen

  • Personal assets ≥ $1,000,000
  • Personal income ≥ $80,000/year for the past 2 years
  • Investment in Thailand ≥ $500,000 (Thai government bonds, FDI, or property)

2. Wealthy Pensioner

  • Aged 50+
  • Annual passive income ≥ $80,000 (or $40,000 with $250k Thai investment)
  • Health insurance with $50,000+ coverage OR $100k Thai bank deposit as healthcare reserve

3. Work-from-Thailand Professional

  • Employed by reputable foreign company (public, listed, or revenue ≥ $150M)
  • Annual income ≥ $80,000/year (or $40,000 with masters degree or 5+ years experience)
  • Health insurance with $50,000+ coverage OR $100k Thai bank deposit

4. Highly-Skilled Professional

  • Working in BOI-promoted target industries (similar list to SMART)
  • Annual income ≥ $80,000/year (or $40,000 with masters degree)
  • Health insurance with $50,000+ coverage OR $100k Thai bank deposit
  • Eligible for Digital Work Permit (free, easier than standard work permit)

What every LTR holder gets

  • 10-year visa total (5 + 5 renewal)
  • Tax exemption on foreign-source income (Royal Decree 743) — this is the headline benefit
  • Annual report to immigration replaces 90-day reporting
  • Multiple-entry, no re-entry permit needed
  • Spouse and up to 4 dependents on the same LTR
  • Fast-track service at Suvarnabhumi airport
  • Digital Work Permit (free) for relevant categories

The tax exemption in plain English

Under Royal Decree 743, LTR Wealthy Pensioner, Wealthy Global Citizen, and Highly-Skilled Professional categories are exempt from Thai personal income tax on income earned outside Thailand and remitted to Thailand. This is significant because Thailand's regular tax residency rule (post-2024 update) makes ALL remitted foreign income taxable for residents staying 180+ days/year. LTR holders sidestep this entirely.

The exemption does NOT cover Thai-source income. If you work in Thailand for a Thai employer or run a Thai business, that income is still taxable.

Who genuinely qualifies for LTR

LTR has higher requirements than most Thai visas. Realistic profiles:

  • $80k/year+ remote tech worker (Work-from-Thailand Professional)
  • Pensioner with >$80,000/year combined pensions/investment income (Wealthy Pensioner)
  • Senior executive at multinational with $1M+ net worth (Wealthy Global Citizen)
  • BOI-sector professional with masters or 5+ years experience and $40k+ salary (Highly-Skilled, $40k path)

If you don't qualify yet

The DTV at ฿10,000 is the natural fallback for remote workers. Privilege Visa fits if you have lump capital but irregular income. Non-O Retirement is the entry-level retirement path if you're 50+. Read our DTV vs LTR comparison if you're choosing between those two.

Application path

  1. Apply via the BOI LTR portal (ltr.boi.go.th)
  2. Upload qualification documents — financial proof, employer info, qualifications
  3. BOI review (typically 4–8 weeks, sometimes 12+)
  4. Approval letter issued; pay ฿50,000 visa fee
  5. Visa stamped at Thai embassy or one-stop service center in Bangkok
  6. Family members and Digital Work Permit added after primary approval

Why LTR is underused despite being a great visa

Most foreigners don't realize they qualify, especially Wealthy Pensioner candidates and remote workers under Work-from-Thailand Professional. The application is online, the BOI is responsive, and the cost (฿50,000) is dramatically less than Privilege Visa for similar long-stay outcomes. If you can hit the income or asset thresholds, this is almost always the right visa.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How does LTR's tax exemption actually work?
Royal Decree 743 (issued 2022) exempts LTR Wealthy Pensioner, Wealthy Global Citizen, and Highly-Skilled Professional from Thai personal income tax on foreign-source income remitted to Thailand. You still file Thai tax (showing zero or minimal taxable income), and you still pay Thai tax on Thai-source income. The exemption is the entire reason LTR is the best visa for remote workers and pensioners with high foreign income.
Is the $80k income calculated before or after tax?
Before tax (gross). The BOI verifies through tax returns from your home country, employer letters, or pension statements. Two consecutive years above $80k is the standard.
Can I count my spouse's income toward the $80k requirement?
No. The income must be in your name. However, you and your spouse can each apply separately under your own LTR if both qualify.
What investments count toward the $250k threshold for Wealthy Pensioner?
Thai government bonds, Thai stocks (SET-listed), Thai REITs, Thai mutual funds, Thai property purchase. The investment must be made and verified before LTR application or within 1 year of LTR approval.
How does the Digital Work Permit differ from a regular work permit?
Digital Work Permit is a simplified, free work authorization issued to LTR Highly-Skilled Professional and Work-from-Thailand Professional holders. No employer sponsorship needed (you can be employed by a foreign company). Issued same-day at one-stop service centers.
Can I work for any employer on LTR Work-from-Thailand?
Your work-from-Thailand LTR is tied to a specific qualifying employer (public-listed or revenue $150M+). Switching employers within qualifying-employer set: notify BOI but generally OK. Switching to a non-qualifying employer: visa basis becomes invalid; you'd switch to a different visa category.
Is the health insurance requirement strict?
Yes — minimum $50,000 coverage from a recognized insurer. Or you can put $100,000 in a Thai bank as a healthcare reserve. The insurance reserve must be maintained throughout the visa term.
How does LTR Wealthy Pensioner compare to Non-O Retirement?
LTR is dramatically better for high-income pensioners ($80k+/year). 10 years vs annual extensions, tax exemption vs full taxation of remittances, family included on one visa, no annual paperwork. Non-O is better for pensioners closer to the ฿65k/month threshold who don't hit $80k/yr.
Can I switch from Non-O to LTR mid-stay?
Yes. Apply for LTR via the BOI portal while on your Non-O. Once LTR is approved, you switch to the LTR visa at a one-stop service center or by leaving and re-entering on the LTR. The two visas don't conflict during application.
How long does LTR approval really take?
BOI publishes 4-week target. Reality: 4–12 weeks depending on documentation complexity and BOI workload. Wealthy Pensioner with clean documents: ~6 weeks. Highly-Skilled in BOI sectors: ~4 weeks. Wealthy Global Citizen with complex assets: 8–12 weeks. Plan accordingly.
