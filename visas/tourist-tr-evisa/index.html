Critical 2026 rules

  • TDAC mandatory since 2024: Complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card at tdac.immigration.go.th within 72 hours before flight. Free, but airlines increasingly check at the gate.
  • Land border visa-exempt entries: 2 per calendar year max (effective Nov 2025). Air arrivals: 6 per year.
  • 20,000 THB cash check: Random border cash checks at major airports. Carry cash or proof of funds.
  • 60-day → 30-day visa exemption cut: APPROVED by Cabinet, effective April 2026. The 30-day extension at Thai Immigration remains, so most visitors can still reach 60 days total in-country (30 + 30). Why now: officials note ~90% of tourists already leave within 30 days, and the longer window had been abused by long-term residents on rolling stamps. Want a clean 60-day stamp on arrival? Apply for the TR e-Visa before flying.

Want to stay longer than 90 days?

The visa-exempt + extension cycle maxes out at 90 days. For longer:

  • Remote workers / digital nomads: DTV (5 years, 180 days per stay)
  • Over 50: Non-O retirement
  • Married to Thai: Marriage Visa
  • Want zero hassle: Privilege Visa or LTR

Deep dive

Tourist TR e-Visa: when it makes sense and when to skip it

The TR (Tourist) e-Visa is Thailand's online-applied tourist visa for stays up to 60 days. It's distinct from visa-exempt entry (now 30 days from April 2026) and from the Visa on Arrival category. The TR e-Visa is the smart choice for travelers wanting longer than 30 days in Thailand without applying for a long-stay visa.

Single-entry vs multiple-entry

  • TR Single-entry: 60 days per visit, valid 3 months from issue. Cost: ~฿1,200–฿2,000 depending on embassy.
  • TR Multiple-entry (METV): 60 days per visit, multiple entries within 6 months validity. Cost: ~฿5,000. Useful for SE Asia trippers using Thailand as a base.

How to apply (e-Visa portal)

  1. Apply online at thaievisa.go.th (the official portal)
  2. Upload passport scan, photo, flight booking, hotel booking, financial proof (฿20,000 cash equivalent)
  3. Pay fee online by credit card
  4. Receive e-Visa PDF in 5–10 working days
  5. Print the PDF and present at Thailand immigration on arrival

30-day extension at immigration

Once in Thailand, you can extend the TR e-Visa by 30 days at any immigration office for ฿1,900. This gives you up to 90 days on a single TR. Some travelers chain entries: enter on TR, use 60 days, extend 30 days, exit, re-enter on visa-exempt for another 30 days = 4 months total before having to leave properly.

What changed in 2026

The big change isn't to TR itself but to the visa-exempt comparison. Visa-exempt was cut from 60 days to 30 days in April 2026. This makes the TR e-Visa relatively more attractive — if you want 30+ days, applying for the TR online before flying is now noticeably easier than landing on visa-exempt and trying to extend.

Working on a TR — strictly no

The TR is a tourist visa. No work, no remote work for foreign clients (per recent strict interpretations), no volunteering for paid causes. If you're working remotely while in Thailand on a TR, you're technically violating visa terms. The DTV was created precisely to legitimize this category for digital nomads.

Common refusal reasons

  • Insufficient financial proof — submit a bank statement showing ฿40,000+ for safety
  • One-way ticket — embassies want a return or onward ticket within 60 days
  • Hotel booking too short — show coverage for at least most of the visa period
  • Recent overstays in Thailand on prior visits
  • Photo or document quality issues — upload high-resolution clear scans

From TR to long-stay: the conversion path

You can convert a TR e-Visa to a long-stay visa from inside Thailand if your reason qualifies (marriage to Thai, ฿800k retirement deposit at 50+, business sponsorship, education enrollment). The conversion is done at Chaeng Wattana (Bangkok) or some regional immigration offices. Most converters do this in their last 30 days on TR before the visa expires.

Pattaya as a TR base

Pattaya is a popular destination for TR e-Visa holders — many Russian, Chinese, and Indian visitors use TR for 60+30 stays in Pattaya before deciding whether to commit to a longer-stay visa. The 90-day reporting requirement doesn't apply to tourists; you just stay within your stamp.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How long can I stay on a TR e-Visa total? 60 days initial + 30 days extension at immigration = 90 days max on a single-entry TR. Multiple-entry METV: 60 days per entry, multiple entries over 6 months, with 30-day extensions possible per entry for total of 9 months in country if used optimally.
Can I work remotely on a TR e-Visa? Strictly no. Thailand has tightened interpretation of remote work on tourist visas. The DTV (5 years × 180 days, ฿10,000) was created specifically for remote work and is the appropriate visa for digital nomads.
What financial proof do I need? ฿20,000 minimum equivalent for single applicant, ฿40,000 for family. Bank statement (last 3 months), credit card statement, or stamped letter from bank are all accepted. Show more than minimum for safety — ฿50,000+ rarely refused.
Can I get a TR e-Visa if I previously overstayed? Depends on overstay length and date. Self-reported overstays under 90 days don't trigger automatic refusal but may flag your application for extra review. Caught overstays or 90+ days definitely complicate future applications.
How long does the e-Visa take to process? 5–10 working days standard. Some embassies offer 1–3 day expedited service for an extra fee. Apply 2–3 weeks before travel for buffer.
Can I extend a TR e-Visa beyond 30 days? Officially no — only one 30-day extension is allowed. In practice, after extension you can sometimes do a border bounce on visa-exempt to add another 30 days, but the new land border 2/year cap (Nov 2025) limits this strategy.
Is the TR e-Visa accepted by all airlines? Yes — print the PDF and show it at check-in. Airlines have integrated electronic visa verification, so the e-Visa is recognized as proof of right-to-enter Thailand.
What's the difference between TR and Visa on Arrival? TR is applied for online before traveling; Visa on Arrival is applied at the airport on landing. VOA is for short stays (15 days) and only available to specific nationalities. TR offers 60 days and is available to most nationalities. Most travelers from non-VOA countries default to TR.
Can I convert TR to retirement visa inside Thailand? Yes, if you're 50+. Open a Thai bank account, deposit ฿800,000, season for 2 months while on TR + extension, then apply for in-country change to Non-O Retirement at Chaeng Wattana. Read our retirement Non-O guide for the full process.
Are family members listed on one e-Visa? No, each family member needs their own e-Visa application. Children under a certain age can be on parent's passport in some countries — in those cases, children are listed on the parent's visa application.
