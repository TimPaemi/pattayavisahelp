Two paths: standard Non-B vs BOI fast-track

Standard Non-B: Thai company sponsors you. Must have 2M THB paid capital per foreign work permit, plus 4 Thai employees per 1 foreigner (4:1 ratio). Visa + work permit takes 4–8 weeks.

BOI-promoted: Company has BOI status (technology, EEC industries, etc.). Exempt from 4:1 ratio, faster processing at OSOS in Bangkok (1–3 days for visa + work permit), reduced 90-day reporting to annual. Major upgrade if eligible.

Eligibility

  • Confirmed job offer or employment contract from Thai company
  • Sponsoring company meets capital + Thai-staff requirements (or BOI-promoted)
  • Your role NOT on Thailand's 39 reserved-occupations list
  • Educational degree or experience matching the role
  • Personal funds: 20,000 THB / 40,000 THB family

Process — 6 steps

  1. Employer obtains WP.32 pre-approval letter from Department of Employment (7–15 working days)
  2. You apply for Non-B visa at Royal Thai Embassy in your home country (single-entry 90-day, 2,000 THB)
  3. Enter Thailand on Non-B
  4. Apply for work permit within 15 days of arrival (7–10 days Bangkok; 2 weeks Pattaya/Chonburi)
  5. Apply for 1-year extension at Jomtien (or OSOS for BOI) — 1,900 THB
  6. Renew annually with updated employer documents

Common pitfalls

1. The 4:1 ratio trap for small businesses

Solo entrepreneur setting up a Thai company to hire yourself = needs 4 Thai employees first. Most fail. BOI promotion or marriage to Thai (2:1) are the workarounds.

2. Working before the work permit is issued

Visa alone does NOT authorise work. Wait for the physical work permit booklet. Working in the meantime is a criminal offence.

3. Expired work permit kills the visa

Work permit and visa renewals are linked. If WP expires by even 1 day, your visa extension basis evaporates.

Pattaya / EEC angle

The Eastern Economic Corridor (Chonburi/Pattaya, Rayong, Chachoengsao) is a major BOI-promoted zone for automotive, electronics, food, robotics, aviation/logistics, and digital industries. Foreign senior hires increasingly use BOI-Non-B or LTR-H instead of standard Non-B for 4:1-exempt fast-track. See LTR for high-income alternative.

Deep dive

Non-B visa: how it actually works in practice

The Non-Immigrant B (Non-B) visa is the gateway to legal employment in Thailand for foreigners. It is not a work permit by itself — it's the visa category that makes you eligible to apply for one. The visa and the work permit together form the legal framework for employment in Thailand.

Sponsorship requirements for the employer

A Thai company sponsoring a Non-B visa must meet ratios that have been the standard for two decades:

  • Registered capital of at least ฿2,000,000 per foreign employee
  • Four Thai employees per one foreign employee (BOI-promoted companies are exempt from this ratio)
  • The foreign employee's salary must meet the minimum by nationality (US/UK/EU/Australia: ฿50,000/month; some Asian countries: ฿35,000/month)
  • The company must be registered for VAT and have filed at least one set of social-security contributions

The two-step application

Applying for a Non-B is a two-step process that catches first-time applicants out:

  1. Visa from a Thai embassy outside Thailand. The employer issues an invitation letter and supporting documents (registration, financials, employment contract). You apply at any Thai embassy — most popular: Vientiane, Phnom Penh, Penang, Savannakhet. Single-entry Non-B is typically valid 90 days.
  2. Work permit at the Department of Employment in Thailand. Once you arrive, the employer's HR or a labour agent files your work permit application. This takes 7–10 working days. You cannot legally work until the work permit blue book is issued.

Annual extensions inside Thailand

Once you're in Thailand on a Non-B with a work permit, you extend your stay annually at Immigration. The extension is based on your employment, not the visa expiry. Required documents: work permit, employer letter, payslips for 3 months, BIS social security contributions, tax filing PND91, and your passport.

Common reasons Non-B applications fail

  • Company hasn't filed VAT or hasn't paid social security for 4 Thai employees
  • Salary on contract is below minimum for your nationality
  • Company registered capital fully subscribed but not paid up
  • Wrong job title — must match what's allowed for foreigners (no list-of-39 restricted jobs)
  • Embassy refusal because supporting docs are stale (older than 3 months)

Pattaya-specific hiring landscape

Pattaya's hospitality, real estate, and service sectors are major Non-B sponsors. Watch for "phantom employer" arrangements — agents charging ฿80,000–฿120,000/year to "place" you with a paper employer that doesn't really need you. These are illegal and the immigration crackdowns of 2024–2025 caught hundreds of foreigners on phantom employments. Real employment, real work, real Non-B is the only safe path.

BOI-promoted alternative

If your employer is BOI-promoted (Board of Investment), the rules change dramatically: no Thai employee ratio, fast-track work permit at the One-Stop Service Center in Bangkok (1-day issue), longer visa validity. If you're choosing between two job offers, BOI-promoted wins on speed and paperwork every time.

Switching employers

Your Non-B and work permit are tied to a specific employer. Changing jobs requires: cancellation of the existing work permit, brief gap (max 7 days to leave Thailand or apply for change), new visa from the new employer. Some labour offices process the change without requiring an exit, but the conservative path is exit + re-enter on the new Non-B.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Can I work on a tourist visa or visa-exempt entry? No. Working on any non-work visa is illegal regardless of whether you're paid in Thailand. This includes remote work for foreign employers in some interpretations — though the DTV was created specifically to permit that. For employment by a Thai entity, you need a Non-B and a work permit.
How much does a Non-B visa cost? Visa fee at embassy: ฿2,000 single-entry / ฿5,000 multiple-entry. Work permit fee: ฿3,000 first-year, ฿3,000/year renewal. Annual visa extension: ฿1,900. Most foreigners pay an additional ฿15,000–฿30,000 in agent fees for the work permit application unless their employer's HR handles it directly.
Can my spouse get a Non-O dependent visa? Yes. Once your Non-B is approved, your legal spouse and children under 20 can apply for Non-O dependent visas. They cannot work on the dependent visa but can study and live in Thailand. The dependent visa renews annually alongside yours.
What's the salary minimum for Pattaya jobs? Same as anywhere in Thailand: ฿50,000/month for US/UK/EU/Australia/Canada/Japan citizens, ฿45,000/month for South Korea, ฿35,000/month for India/Indonesia/Philippines/Vietnam, lower for some other nationalities. The salary on your contract must match — paying you less than this in practice is a violation.
Can I open a Thai company and sponsor my own Non-B? Yes — this is a common path. You set up a Thai Limited company, hire 4 Thai employees, register for VAT, and the company sponsors your Non-B. Read our setting-up Thai company guide for the realistic costs and timeline. Note: simply being a director doesn't qualify; you need real employment with real salary and contributions.
Does the work permit allow me to work anywhere? No. Your work permit lists the specific job title, employer, and work address. Doing work outside this scope (different employer, different role, different location) is illegal. If your role evolves, the work permit must be amended at the labour office.
How long can I stay outside Thailand on a Non-B? If you have an active Non-B with a re-entry permit, you can leave for any length of time before the visa expires. Without a re-entry permit, leaving voids your visa instantly. Most labour officers also expect you to be in Thailand for at least 2/3 of the year — extended absences may complicate annual extension.
Can I change to a different visa from a Non-B? Yes. Common switches: Non-B → LTR (if you become eligible), Non-B → Marriage Non-O (if you marry a Thai), Non-B → Privilege Visa. Some require leaving and re-entering on the new visa; others allow change-of-status inside Thailand at Chaeng Wattana (Bangkok).
Is it legal to work remotely for a foreign company on a Non-B? Strictly speaking, work performed in Thailand requires a work permit even for foreign employers. The DTV was created to legalize this. If you're on a Non-B for a Thai employer but moonlight for a foreign company online, you're in a grey area that immigration has historically tolerated but technically violates work permit terms.
How does Non-B differ from SMART or LTR? Non-B requires annual extension and is tied to one employer. SMART (only S-track since Feb 2025) and LTR offer 3–10 year visas with simpler reporting and no Thai employee ratio. If you qualify for SMART or LTR, those are dramatically better options.
