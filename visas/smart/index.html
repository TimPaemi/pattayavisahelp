SMART. The tech-talent + startup visa.

The SMART (Skilled Migrant Acceleration through Reformed Talent-attraction) Visa is Thailand's BOI-administered visa for highly-skilled tech talent, startup founders, executives, investors, and their dependents. 4-year validity. Work permit waived. Available across 13 targeted industries. The right visa for tech professionals working with BOI-promoted Thai companies.

// TL;DR

If you're a senior tech professional, startup founder, executive, or investor in one of Thailand's 13 BOI-targeted industries — SMART is a strong fit. 4-year multi-entry visa, no separate work permit required, 1-year reporting (not 90-day), spouse + kids included. Five subcategories (T/I/E/S/O). Application via BOI online portal, 30-day endorsement process. Compared to LTR: smaller financial threshold, narrower industry fit. Compared to Non-B: no annual renewals, no employer-tied permit.

What SMART actually is

The SMART Visa was launched by Thailand's Board of Investment (BOI) in 2018 as part of the Thailand 4.0 economic strategy — an effort to attract foreign tech talent, investment, and startup activity to BOI-promoted industries. It's separate from the LTR (also BOI) and serves a narrower professional/industry niche.

Key differences from a regular Non-B + work permit:

  • 4-year visa instead of 1-year renewals
  • No separate work permit required — the SMART visa itself authorises work in your endorsed role
  • 1-year reporting at BOI instead of 90-day TM47s at immigration
  • Family included — spouse + children get matching 4-year visas, spouse can work without separate permit
  • BOI One-Stop Service Center handles renewals and operations, not regular immigration

The 13 targeted industries

SMART is only available if your work / company / investment is in one of these BOI-targeted "S-Curve" industries:

  1. Next-generation automotive
  2. Smart electronics
  3. Affluent, medical and wellness tourism
  4. Agriculture and biotechnology
  5. Food for the future
  6. Automation and robotics
  7. Aviation and logistics
  8. Biofuels and biochemicals
  9. Digital (incl. software, AI, big data, cybersecurity)
  10. Medical hub
  11. Defence industry
  12. Human resources development
  13. Alternative dispute resolution / international arbitration

If your work doesn't fit one of these — SMART won't work for you. Consider Non-B + work permit, LTR Work-from-Thailand, or DTV instead.

Application — step by step

  1. Confirm endorsement. You need a Thai employer / company / investment / startup that BOI considers eligible. Get a tentative letter of endorsement before applying.
  2. Apply via BOI online portal at smart-visa.boi.go.th. Upload contracts, salary proof, experience proof, company endorsement, insurance.
  3. BOI review (30 days). They consult relevant agencies (DEPA for digital, NIA for startups, etc.).
  4. Receive endorsement letter if approved. Valid 60 days.
  5. Collect SMART visa at a Thai embassy or at BOI One-Stop Service Center in Bangkok.
  6. Enter Thailand on the SMART visa. Begin work immediately.
  7. Annual report at BOI One-Stop, not at immigration.

4 common Pattaya pitfalls

  1. Assuming any tech job qualifies. The 13 targeted industries are narrow — generic software dev for a non-BOI company often doesn't qualify. Confirm BOI-eligibility before applying.
  2. Mixing SMART roles. The visa is tied to your endorsed role. If you switch employers / roles mid-visa, you need a re-endorsement. Notify BOI immediately.
  3. Forgetting the insurance. $50K+ health coverage is required. Missing this delays approval.
  4. Family work confusion. SMART-O (dependent) spouse can work without a separate permit — but the work itself must be reported to BOI. Don't just start working unannounced.

Common questions

Can I get SMART if I work remote for a foreign company?

No — SMART requires endorsement from a Thai entity (BOI-promoted employer, investee, or accelerator). For remote work from foreign companies, look at DTV or LTR Work-from-Thailand instead.

What if my industry isn't on the 13 list?

Then SMART isn't for you. Most regular tech jobs (general software, IT support, marketing) fall outside. You'd need Non-B + work permit, or LTR if you qualify income-wise.

Can I renew SMART after 4 years?

Yes — apply for a new SMART. Same eligibility criteria. Most renewals are smoother than initial applications because BOI already has your file.

Is SMART available in Pattaya or Bangkok-only?

BOI One-Stop service center is in Bangkok (Chamchuri Square). For routine SMART work, online portal + occasional Bangkok visits are sufficient. You can live and work in Pattaya — just travel to Bangkok for the 1-year report.

Does SMART grant a path to PR or citizenship?

No direct PR path under SMART itself. However, time on SMART counts toward general residency timelines if you later transition to a PR-eligible visa (Non-B with work permit for 3+ years, or Marriage).
