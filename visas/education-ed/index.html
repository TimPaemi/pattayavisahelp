2025 crackdown context: The Thai Ministry of Higher Education revoked nearly 10,000 student visas linked to fake schools. The ED visa is fully legitimate — but only at properly MOE-accredited institutions with real classes and attendance reporting. Verify any school before paying.

Eligibility

  • Valid passport (6+ months remaining)
  • Genuine enrolment at MOE-accredited school with real classes, real attendance tracking
  • Payment of 50%+ of course tuition
  • For some nationalities (UK, US): criminal background check required
  • No income / bank balance requirement (unlike DTV)
  • No employment of any kind permitted (including remote)

Approved activities

  • Thai language schools (typical: 12–14 months, 30k–80k THB)
  • Muay Thai training at MOE-licensed camps (typical: 12 months all-in 120k–135k THB)
  • English / Japanese / Chinese language at MOE-licensed institutes
  • University degree programmes
  • Vocational / technical training
  • Approved internships

Application process (Pattaya in-country path)

  1. Arrive on 60-day tourist visa or visa-exempt entry
  2. Enrol at chosen MOE-accredited Pattaya school within first week
  3. School submits to Ministry of Education for approval (2–4 weeks)
  4. MOE issues official approval letter
  5. Submit application at Immigration (school typically accompanies you)
  6. Processing ~16 working days
  7. Receive 90-day Non-ED visa stamp
  8. Renew every 90 days at Jomtien (1,900 THB)

Critical: verify your school before paying

The 10,000 visa revocations in 2025 hit students who paid "ghost schools" — institutions with MOE letterhead but no real classes. Before paying any school, verify:

  • Show me your MOE registration certificate (legitimate schools display proudly)
  • Can I visit the school in person and sit in a class?
  • How do you track attendance, and how often do you report to MOE?
  • Avoid: schools that "guarantee" approval, online-only courses (no longer accepted), schools advertising only on visa-tourism sites

Deep dive

Education ED visa: how to choose a school that won't get you in trouble

The Non-Immigrant ED (Education) visa is for foreign students enrolled in schools, universities, language courses, or vocational training in Thailand. It's the most-abused visa category in the country, which means immigration scrutiny is high.

Who actually qualifies for an ED visa

  • Students enrolled at MOE-accredited language schools (Thai, English, Chinese)
  • University students at accredited institutions (bachelor's, master's, PhD)
  • International school students (under 20, K-12)
  • Vocational training programs (Muay Thai, cooking, massage) at accredited schools
  • Religious study programs at officially registered temples or institutions

How to verify a school is real

Any school issuing ED visa support letters must be MOE-accredited (Ministry of Education). Read our how to verify MOE accreditation guide — bookmark it before paying any agent or school. The single most common scam is a school that issues paperwork but isn't actually accredited; immigration discovers this at extension time and your visa is cancelled.

Realistic costs by program type

  • Thai language school: ฿18,000–฿35,000 per year for 4 hours/week, ฿35,000–฿60,000 for 12 hours/week
  • Muay Thai school (with ED visa support): ฿30,000–฿60,000 per year
  • University: ฿80,000–฿250,000 per year for international programs
  • International school K-12: ฿300,000–฿900,000 per year

Attendance is monitored

This is the part agents don't tell you. Schools must record your attendance and report it. If you skip more than 30% of classes, the school can cancel your enrollment, which voids your visa. Some schools do this loosely; the strict ones will absolutely cut you. Don't buy an ED visa thinking it's a long-stay solution where you never show up — those days are over.

Extension process

Initial ED visa is single-entry, 90 days. After arrival you extend at Immigration in 9-month or 1-year increments depending on the school's accreditation and your program length. Extensions require: school letter, attendance record, payment receipts, passport copies. Currently ฿1,900 per extension.

Pattaya ED visa schools

Pattaya has dozens of language and Muay Thai schools that issue ED visas. The reputable ones are well-known and verifiable. If a "school" operates from a small office in Soi Buakhao with no visible students, it is almost certainly a paper school being used for visa purposes only.

Working on an ED visa

You cannot work on an ED visa. This includes online remote work, freelance gigs, or English teaching. If you want to work, you need a Non-B and work permit (or a DTV for remote freelance). Universities sometimes allow part-time campus jobs but only with explicit written permission from the program.

Switching from ED to other visas

Common transitions: ED → Non-B (if you finish school and get a job offer), ED → Marriage Non-O (if you marry a Thai), ED → DTV (if you start a remote-work portfolio). All require fresh visa applications, typically from a Thai embassy outside Thailand.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Can I really not work at all on an ED visa? Correct. The ED visa permits study only. Any paid work — teaching English under the table, online freelancing, anything that produces income — violates your visa terms. Universities sometimes have limited exceptions for on-campus paid research. Pattaya immigration treats ED visa misuse seriously and has cancelled visas for foreigners caught working.
How long can I extend an ED visa? Most language and Muay Thai ED visas allow up to 4 years total (in 9-month or 1-year extensions). University programs allow extensions for the duration of the program plus a grace period. After max extensions, you need to apply for a fresh ED visa from a Thai embassy abroad.
What if I miss too many classes? The school will warn you, then cancel your enrollment if attendance stays below 70%. Cancelled enrollment means your visa is voided. You then have 7 days to leave Thailand or transfer to another visa-compatible status. Don't gamble on this — schools are required to report attendance honestly.
Can I bring my family on a dependent visa? Limited. ED visa dependents are typically allowed for university students. For language and Muay Thai programs, family members usually need their own visa pathway (tourist, marriage, etc.). Your school can advise on what's possible for your specific program.
Is the ED visa good value for a Thai language student? Yes, if you actually want to learn Thai. ฿20,000–฿35,000/year for serious language study is reasonable, and you get a long-stay visa as a side benefit. If you don't actually plan to study, the ED visa is poor value compared to DTV (฿10k one-time, 5 years) for the same long-stay outcome.
Do I need health insurance for an ED visa? Mandatory for international school children under 20. Not mandatory for adult language school or university ED visa holders. Highly recommended regardless — Thai healthcare costs add up fast for the uninsured.
Can I switch schools mid-visa? Yes, but it's a hassle. Your visa is tied to one school. Switching requires the new school to issue support docs, you exit Thailand, get a fresh ED visa from an embassy, then re-enter. Some students manage in-country transfers but it's case-by-case at the labour office.
What about Muay Thai schools — are they all legit? No. Some Muay Thai schools are training-first and ED-visa-as-side-effect; others are visa mills with a heavy bag in the corner. The legit ones have visible daily training schedules, real Thai trainers with credentials, and real students who train hard. The mills have empty gyms and a desk for paperwork.
Can I do an online Thai course for ED visa? No. Online-only courses don't qualify for ED visa. You need in-person attendance at a physical school. Hybrid programs may work if the in-person component meets minimum hours per week.
How does the ED visa compare to the DTV for Thai language study? DTV explicitly lists Muay Thai and Thai cooking among qualifying activities for the soft-power category. For pure Thai language, ED is the standard path with school accreditation built in. DTV gives 5 years × 180 days, ED gives consecutive 9–12 month stays. Choose based on whether you want school-attached structure or independent flexibility.
