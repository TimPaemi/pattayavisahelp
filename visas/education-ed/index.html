2025 crackdown context: The Thai Ministry of Higher Education revoked nearly 10,000 student visas linked to fake schools. The ED visa is fully legitimate — but only at properly MOE-accredited institutions with real classes and attendance reporting. Verify any school before paying.

Eligibility

  • Valid passport (6+ months remaining)
  • Genuine enrolment at MOE-accredited school with real classes, real attendance tracking
  • Payment of 50%+ of course tuition
  • For some nationalities (UK, US): criminal background check required
  • No income / bank balance requirement (unlike DTV)
  • No employment of any kind permitted (including remote)

Approved activities

  • Thai language schools (typical: 12–14 months, 30k–80k THB)
  • Muay Thai training at MOE-licensed camps (typical: 12 months all-in 120k–135k THB)
  • English / Japanese / Chinese language at MOE-licensed institutes
  • University degree programmes
  • Vocational / technical training
  • Approved internships

Application process (Pattaya in-country path)

  1. Arrive on 60-day tourist visa or visa-exempt entry
  2. Enrol at chosen MOE-accredited Pattaya school within first week
  3. School submits to Ministry of Education for approval (2–4 weeks)
  4. MOE issues official approval letter
  5. Submit application at Immigration (school typically accompanies you)
  6. Processing ~16 working days
  7. Receive 90-day Non-ED visa stamp
  8. Renew every 90 days at Jomtien (1,900 THB)

Critical: verify your school before paying

The 10,000 visa revocations in 2025 hit students who paid "ghost schools" — institutions with MOE letterhead but no real classes. Before paying any school, verify:

  • Show me your MOE registration certificate (legitimate schools display proudly)
  • Can I visit the school in person and sit in a class?
  • How do you track attendance, and how often do you report to MOE?
  • Avoid: schools that "guarantee" approval, online-only courses (no longer accepted), schools advertising only on visa-tourism sites

Pattaya specifics

Pattaya is one of Thailand's most active centres for ED visa schools — particularly Thai language and Muay Thai. Only a subset of Muay Thai gyms hold valid MOE licenses — most are sports facilities, not educational institutions. We refer only to verified MOE-current Pattaya schools.

Frequently asked questions

Can I do Muay Thai on an ED visa?
Yes — at MOE-licensed Muay Thai camps with real attendance tracking. Most Pattaya gyms aren't licensed; verify before paying.
Can I work remotely on an ED visa?
No — all employment prohibited, including remote work for foreign clients. The DTV is the right visa for remote workers.
What's the attendance requirement?
80% minimum. Schools report monthly to MOE/Immigration. Below 80% = visa extension refused. Officers also test language students at extensions ("can you say a few Thai words after 3 months?").
How do I verify a school is MOE-accredited?
Ask for MOE registration certificate. Visit physically. Sit in a class. Cross-check with PCEC or our network. Full verification guide.
