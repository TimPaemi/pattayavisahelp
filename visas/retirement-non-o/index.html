Is Non-O retirement right for you?

  • ✅ You're 50+ with 800,000 THB available (or 65,000 THB/month income)
  • ✅ You want the cheapest, simplest retirement path with no mandatory insurance
  • ✅ You're already in Thailand or arriving on tourist visa / visa-exempt
  • ❌ Not for: under-50s (try DTV), or if you want embassy-issued 1-year visa upfront (try O-A)

Why this is the most popular Pattaya retirement path

The Non-O retirement path (in-country) is arguably the most popular retirement visa route in all of Thailand. The defining feature: critical paperwork is done inside Thailand at Jomtien Immigration, not at a Thai embassy abroad — which most retirees find simpler and more forgiving than the Non-O-A's embassy-first requirement.

The single biggest advantage over Non-O-A: no mandatory health insurance. For older retirees (65+) facing 80,000–150,000 THB/year insurance premiums, this saves substantial money each year.

Eligibility

  • Aged 50 or older on the day of application
  • Hold a valid 90-day Non-Immigrant O visa (the prerequisite)
  • Meet financial requirements (see below)
  • Have a verifiable Thai address (TM30 filed)
  • No criminal background check required for in-country extension
  • No medical certificate required
  • No mandatory health insurance (unlike Non-O-A)
  • Employment of any kind strictly prohibited

Critical Jomtien rule — the 2-month seasoning trap: The 800,000 THB must be in your Thai bank account for at least 2 months before applying. This catches almost every first-time applicant. Plan ahead — the clock starts the day you deposit.

The standard Pattaya path — step by step

  1. Arrive in Thailand on tourist visa, visa-exempt, or initial 90-day Non-O from embassy abroad
  2. Open a Thai bank account (Bangkok Bank or Kasikorn) and deposit 800,000 THB. See bank account guide
  3. If on tourist/exempt: visit Jomtien Immigration to file TM.86 / TM.87 to convert to 90-day Non-O
  4. Receive 90-day Non-Immigrant O stamp (~15 working days)
  5. Wait for the 2-month seasoning period to complete
  6. In the last 30 days of your 90-day Non-O, return to Jomtien for the 1-year extension
  7. Submit TM.7 form, passport, bank book, bank letter, TM30, photos
  8. Pay 1,900 THB cash; receive 1-year Extension of Stay stamp same day
  9. Renew annually at Jomtien with same documents + updated bank book

Common pitfalls

1. The 2-month seasoning trap

Depositing 800k THB the week before extension = turned away. Wait the full 2 months.

2. UK/US/AU embassy income letters don't exist anymore

These embassies stopped issuing income letters. Use the 800k bank deposit method.

3. Exiting Thailand without re-entry permit

Cancels your 1-year extension permanently. Buy single (1,000 THB) or multiple (3,800 THB) at Jomtien before any trip. See re-entry permit guide.

4. TM30 not filed by landlord

Jomtien requires TM30 receipt at every extension. Verify your landlord has filed. See TM30 guide.

Pattaya / Jomtien specifics

This visa is the most-processed retirement path at Jomtien Immigration. Thousands of Pattaya retirees go through annually. See our Jomtien practical guide.

Bank letter same-day requirement: Many Jomtien officers in 2026 insist on a bank letter dated the same day or previous day of your appointment. Get the letter at your bank branch the morning of your appointment.

Local agents: Pattaya retirement visa agents typically charge 5,000–15,000 THB to handle the full extension annually — many retirees use them to skip queues and avoid documentation errors.

Frequently asked questions

Do I really not need health insurance?Correct — as of April 2026, the Non-O in-country extension does not legally require health insurance. Only the Non-O-A path (embassy-issued) mandates it. Private insurance is still strongly recommended given Pattaya hospital costs.
How long does the 800k need to be in my Thai bank?At Jomtien, 2 months of seasoning before application date. National rule doesn't always specify this, but Jomtien enforces it consistently.
Can I use a UK / US / Australian income letter?No — these embassies stopped issuing income letters years ago. UK, US, and Australian retirees must use the 800,000 THB bank deposit method.
How many times can I renew?Indefinitely. Annual renewals at Jomtien for as long as you meet age and financial requirements. No maximum.
What if I'm 50+ AND married to a Thai national?You can choose either retirement (800k THB) or marriage visa (400k THB). Marriage saves you 400k in seasoning but adds spouse-attendance and home-visit requirements. See comparison.
How does this differ from Non-O-A?Non-O-A: applied at embassy abroad, 1-year visa upfront, mandatory insurance + police clearance + medical certificate. Non-O (this path): in-country, simpler, no mandatory insurance. Full comparison here.

Deep dive

Non-O Retirement: how it actually plays out year after year

The Non-O Retirement extension is Thailand's most-used long-stay visa for foreigners 50+. The headline numbers (฿800,000 deposit OR ฿65,000/month income) are well-known, but the lived experience of doing this every year is where things get specific.

The two paths into Non-O Retirement

  1. From abroad: Apply for a Non-O visa at a Thai embassy in your home country or a regional embassy. Single-entry Non-O is 90 days; you arrive, complete extension paperwork inside Thailand for the 1-year retirement extension.
  2. From inside Thailand: Enter on visa-exempt or TR e-Visa, open a Thai bank account, deposit ฿800,000, season for 2 months, apply for in-country change to Non-O at Chaeng Wattana (Bangkok). This path used to be common; recent tightening means it's now harder but still possible.

Year-by-year reality of the financial requirement

The financial requirement isn't just at extension time — it has to be maintained:

  • ฿800,000 must be in your Thai bank for 2 months before first extension, 3 months before subsequent extensions
  • After extension, you must keep ฿400,000+ in the account for 12 months continuously, only dipping to ฿800k threshold for the seasoning period
  • OR ฿65,000/month income transferred to Thailand (12 consecutive months of incoming international transfers)
  • OR a combination totalling ฿800,000 over 12 months

The annual extension routine

Each year, you visit Pattaya/Jomtien Immigration with:

  1. Updated bank book showing seasoning
  2. Bank letter confirming balance (must be dated within 7 days)
  3. TM7 application form
  4. 4 passport photos
  5. Photocopies of every page of your passport
  6. TM30 (address registration) confirmation
  7. Map showing your residence
  8. ฿1,900 extension fee

Without an agent, the extension takes 1.5–3 hours at Jomtien on a busy day. With an agent (~฿5,000–฿15,000), they handle queue and paperwork — your visit is 15–30 minutes for biometrics + signature.

Things that catch people out

  • Stale bank letter: immigration wants it within 7 days; people get caught with 14-day-old letters
  • Bank book not updated: the passbook must show recent transactions; print at the bank within a few days
  • Insufficient income transfers: ฿65k/month means ฿65,000 each month, not ฿700,000 total over 12 months. A ฿200k deposit one month and skinny months elsewhere doesn't qualify
  • Bank account joint with Thai spouse: the financial requirement must be in YOUR name only, not joint
  • FCD (foreign currency) accounts: some immigration officers accept these, others don't. Convert to THB savings for safety

The "no income letter" landscape

Through 2018, you could get an income letter from your home country's embassy in Thailand attesting to your foreign income — this counted toward the ฿65k/month requirement. The US, UK, Australia, and Canada embassies all stopped issuing these in 2018–2019. Now you must show actual transfers to your Thai bank or the deposit method.

Health insurance — not required, strongly recommended

Non-O Retirement has NO mandatory insurance. This is one of its main advantages over O-A. But Thai healthcare costs add up fast — a basic illness can cost ฿100,000+ at private hospitals. Most Pattaya retirees on Non-O carry insurance voluntarily. Read our health insurance guide for what to look for.

What you can and can't do on Non-O Retirement

  • Live in Thailand year-round
  • Travel with re-entry permit
  • Volunteer (unpaid)
  • Buy property (though land ownership has separate restrictions)
  • Own a Thai bank account, get a Thai driving licence, get a Thai phone
  • Work for a Thai employer (need Non-B + work permit)
  • Run a Thai company actively (you can be a passive shareholder)
  • Receive paid income from work performed in Thailand

The path beyond Non-O annual extensions

Many Pattaya retirees stay on Non-O for 10+ years successfully. Some upgrade after 3+ years to Permanent Residency (rigorous application, small annual quota — Pattaya rarely sees PR-track retirees). Others switch to Privilege Visa after 5–10 years on Non-O to escape the annual paperwork. Read our Non-O vs O-A comparison for the upgrade decision tree.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Can I get a Non-O Retirement if I'm 49 turning 50 this year? No. Age 50+ at the time of application is required. Some applicants apply 1-2 weeks before their 50th birthday hoping the immigration officer won't notice — this is risky and refused often. Wait until after your 50th.
What's the realistic total cost in year 1? Embassy visa: ฿2,000–฿5,000. Translation/courier: $50–$200. Re-entry permit if traveling: ฿1,000–฿3,800. Extension fee: ฿1,900. Health insurance (voluntary): ฿15k–฿100k+. Agent fee (optional): ฿5k–฿15k. Total realistic year 1: ฿20,000–฿130,000 depending on insurance choice and agent use.
Can I do the application without an agent? Yes, easily. The Non-O Retirement extension is one of the simpler immigration processes if you have your finances in order. Most retirees do it themselves after the first year. Year 1 with an agent helps you understand the process; subsequent years you can self-do confidently.
Does the ฿800k earn interest? Yes — standard Thai savings rate is 0.5–1.5%/year on regular savings, 1.5–3% on fixed deposits. Some retirees split: ฿400k in savings (immediate access), ฿400k in 6-month fixed deposit (better rate). The deposit must be redeemable on demand for immigration purposes — fixed-deposit terms shouldn't lock past your extension date.
What if my balance dips below ฿400k between extensions? Technically a violation. Practically, immigration only checks at extension time. If you let it dip and restored before the next extension, you usually get away with it. But it's risky — random checks happen.
Can I pre-fund the ฿800k requirement before turning 50? Yes. Many soon-to-be 50-year-olds open a Thai bank account during a tourist visit, deposit ฿800k, and let it season. By the time they hit 50 and apply for the visa, the deposit is already 12+ months seasoned. This is the smoothest path.
Do I have to be in Thailand for my extension appointment? Yes. The extension is processed in Thailand; you must be physically present at Pattaya/Jomtien (or other) immigration office. You can't extend from outside Thailand.
Can I leave Thailand and re-enter without a re-entry permit? No — leaving Thailand on an active Non-O visa without a re-entry permit voids the visa instantly. Always get a re-entry permit (single ฿1,000 / multi ฿3,800) before leaving. Get it at the airport on departure if needed.
How does Pattaya/Jomtien immigration compare to other offices? Jomtien is famous for being thorough but professional. Queue numbers issued at 6:00am for popular days. Online queue available via the Thai Immigration mobile app. Read our Jomtien Immigration practical guide for the full experience.
Can my non-Thai spouse get a dependent Non-O visa? Yes. Spouse can be on Non-O dependent visa with the same term as you. Spouse doesn't need to be 50+ — they're a dependent of you, not a separate retiree. Children under 20 can be dependents too. Dependent extension fee: also ฿1,900.
