Jomtien family beach district

5km of beachfront, more relaxed than central Pattaya, popular with retirees and families. Where it sits, what it costs, and who it suits.

Updated 10 May 2026·Pattaya Visa Help·Independent · No commissions
Pattaya neighborhood 4 min read·Updated April 2026

Where Jomtien is

Jomtien Beach runs 5km south of central Pattaya, separated by Pratumnak Hill. The northern end (Jomtien Soi 1–10) is closest to Pattaya and busiest. The southern end (Soi 25+, toward Najomtien and Bang Saray) is quieter. Beach Road runs parallel to the coast; Second Road runs inland.

Vibe

Jomtien is family-friendly beach. The beach is wider and cleaner than central Pattaya. Russian, Chinese, and Western families predominate during high season. Restaurants and shops cater to long-stay residents rather than 3-day tourists. Less nightlife than central Pattaya — bars exist but no Walking Street equivalent.

Who Jomtien suits

  • Retired couples wanting beach access without central Pattaya chaos
  • Families with school-age children
  • Russian/Chinese expat families
  • Cost-conscious solo retirees (older buildings offer best value in Pattaya)
  • Long-stay DTV holders working remotely

What's nearby

  • Schools: Pattaya International School (PIS), Garden International, Tara Pattaya — within 15 min
  • Hospitals: Bangkok Hospital Pattaya (15 min), Pattaya International Hospital (10 min)
  • Shopping: Tukcom (electronics), Outlet Mall, Tops Market, BigC, Lotus's
  • Beach activities: Sailing, jet ski (rentals controversial), beach restaurants
  • Daily food: Jomtien Beach Soi 5 night market, Thepprasit Market (Tue/Sat/Sun)

Best for / avoid

Best for: 60% of Pattaya retirees pick Jomtien for the value-to-quality ratio. Beach access, family-friendly, English-speaking community, established expat infrastructure, multiple schools, condos at all price points.

Avoid if: You want premium-only (go Wongamat), rural quiet (Bang Saray), or the central Pattaya nightlife/walking-distance-to-everything (Central or Pratumnak).

Visa-logistics from Jomtien

Jomtien Immigration Office serves all of Pattaya — it's in Jomtien itself, on Soi Chaiyaphruek 2. Walking distance from Soi 5–10. This is one of Jomtien's practical advantages — for retirement and marriage visa holders doing annual extensions and 90-day reports, the immigration office is on your doorstep. Daily life details

What life looks like in Jomtien

Mornings

Beach walk on the wide promenade, mango sticky rice from the morning vendors, coffee at one of dozens of cafes (Common Ground, Cafe Amazon, Volume). Most retirees do their daily routine before noon when temperatures spike.

Afternoons

Shopping at Tukcom (electronics), BigC, Tops Market, or Outlet Mall. Lunch at Thai street stalls (฿80-฿120) or beachfront restaurants. Many retirees use this time for visa-related visits to Jomtien Immigration Office (5-10 min walking from Soi 5-10).

Evenings

Dinner at one of hundreds of restaurants — Thai, German, Russian, Indian, Italian widely available. Beach Road comes alive after sunset with night markets and walking traffic. Soi 5 night market and Thepprasit Market (Tue/Sat/Sun) are major draws.

Sports + activities

Beach: swimming, jet ski rental, sailing, kitesurfing (north end). Tennis: Tiff\'s Tennis Centre. Gym: Tiff\'s Fitness, Jetts. Yoga and Pilates studios in mid-tier condos. Golf: 15+ courses within 30 min.

Things to know about Jomtien specifically

  • The "Jomtien Beach" is technically two beaches: Jomtien Beach (north, busier) and Najomtien Beach (south, quieter)
  • Soi 5-10 are the most popular expat sois
  • Soi 13-15 has the highest concentration of Russian businesses
  • Soi 20+ approaches Najomtien — quieter, lower density
  • Beach Road runs the entire length; Second Road is parallel inland with most shops
  • Songthaew (baht bus) runs frequently between Jomtien and central Pattaya: ฿10-฿20
