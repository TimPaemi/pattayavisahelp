Russia to Thailand: visa options

Best visa pathways for Russian citizens moving to Pattaya or anywhere in Thailand. Updated for 2026 rules including the visa-exempt cut and tax changes.

Updated 10 May 2026·Pattaya Visa Help·Independent · No commissions
Russian citizens · 2026 Thailand visa guide 7 min read·Updated April 2026 Quick take for Russian citizens

Largest single-nationality influx since 2022. Most stayed on tourist + visa-exempt rotations until DTV launched — that became the default in 2024.

Visa-exempt entry: what changed in 2026

Russian passport holders get 30 days visa-exempt on arrival. Cut from 60 to 30 days April 2026.

If you plan to stay longer than 30 days, plan ahead — get a Tourist e-Visa (60 days) or apply for a long-stay visa from your home country before flying.

Land border cap (Nov 2025) Visa-exempt entries by land border are now capped at 2 per calendar year. Air arrivals are unlimited but each is still 30 days max from April 2026.

Best visa pathways for Russian citizens

Based on what works in practice for Russian clients in Pattaya:

DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) → 5-year, 180 days/entry, ฿10,000 fee Privilege (Elite) Visa → 5–20 years, 4 tiers, ฿650k–฿2.5M Tourist TR e-Visa → Up to 60 days online Education ED → Thai language or accredited study Marriage Non-O → Specifically for Russian citizens

Russia expats in Thailand: the deeper context

The Russian community in Pattaya exploded after February 2022, with estimated 50,000+ long-term Russian residents in greater Pattaya by 2026, plus tens of thousands more in Phuket. The community is the youngest of any major foreign cohort — many are remote workers, IT professionals, entrepreneurs in their 20s and 30s rather than retirees.

Visa pathways since 2022

Initial 2022–2023 wave used tourist visas and visa-exempt rotations. The DTV launch in 2024 became the dominant pathway: ฿10,000 one-time, 5 years, 180 days per entry, and explicitly remote-work-friendly. Many of the most settled Russians are now on DTV, Privilege Visa, or LTR Work-from-Thailand Professional.

Banking under sanctions

Russian credit cards (Visa/Mastercard issued by Russian banks) stopped working in Thailand in March 2022. Russian-issued Mir cards are accepted at limited Thai merchants. Most Russians in Thailand operate on:

  • Cards from third-country banks (UAE, Kazakhstan, Georgia, Armenia)
  • Crypto (USDT/USDC) ramps to Thai bank or cash via local exchanges
  • Foreign-issued cards from family in Europe (relative-issued)
  • Wise, Revolut, and other fintechs accessed via European phone numbers

Opening a Thai bank account as a Russian

This is harder than for Western passport holders. Most Thai banks now require:

  • Active long-stay visa (Non-O, DTV, Privilege, LTR — not tourist or visa-exempt)
  • Work permit or income proof in some cases
  • Letter from a Thai sponsor or employer for some banks

Bangkok Bank and Kasikorn (KBank) are the most Russian-friendly. SCB and Krungthai are stricter. Read our Thai bank account guide for the full process.

Russian Embassy in Bangkok

Located on Sathorn Road. Notarial services for Russian documents, replacement passport (10–14 days), tax residency certificates. Russian honorary consul in Phuket; Pattaya has no consular presence.

Marriage and family

Russian-Thai marriages are increasing rapidly post-2022. Russian-Russian families settling long-term in Pattaya are now common — Pattaya International Hospital reports significant maternity volume in the Russian-speaking community.

Schools and language

Russian-language schools have multiplied in Pattaya since 2022: Pattaya Russian School, Russky Pattaya Academy, and several private tutoring operations. Russian-speaking pediatricians, dentists, and lawyers are now widely available — a marked change from pre-2022 when most service providers required Thai or English.

FAQ

Russian citizen FAQs

Можно ли получить тайскую визу с российским паспортом? Да. Россияне получают визы на тех же условиях, что и большинство других иностранцев — DTV, Privilege, LTR, Non-O retirement (50+), Marriage Non-O. Виза-эксемпт сокращён до 30 дней с апреля 2026.
Can I work for a Russian company while on DTV in Thailand? DTV explicitly permits remote work for non-Thai employers. Working for a Russian company remotely is permitted. Receiving payment in roubles is the practical complication — most use crypto or third-country accounts to convert to THB.
Is Mir card accepted in Thailand? Limited acceptance. Some merchants in Pattaya/Phuket accept Mir, especially Russian-owned businesses. Major retail (BigC, Tops, Lotus's), most hotels, and most restaurants don't accept Mir. Plan on cash, crypto-converted, or third-country card.
Are Russian credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) usable in Thailand? Russian-bank-issued Visa/Mastercard stopped working internationally in March 2022 due to sanctions. Cards issued by foreign banks but held by Russian residents continue to work normally.
Can I receive transfers from Russia to a Thai bank? Direct SWIFT transfers from sanctioned Russian banks are blocked. Workarounds: third-country bank intermediation (UAE, Kazakhstan, Armenia), crypto, in-person cash via couriers (legal but cumbersome). Many Russian residents maintain accounts in non-sanctioned countries for transit.
How do I get a Russian police clearance for Thai visa applications? Via the Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs (MVD) at a regional office, or via the Russian Embassy in Bangkok. Embassy issuance: 30–45 days, certificate apostilled. Required for O-A retirement and some long-stay categories.
Can I open a Thai bank account as a Russian on tourist visa? Generally no, since 2024 banks require long-stay visa (Non-O, DTV, Privilege, LTR). Some banks accept tourist + 1-year apartment lease + sponsor letter. Bangkok Bank and KBank are the most flexible for Russian customers.
Is Russian recognized as an official language for Thai government documents? No. Documents must be in Thai or English. Russian-language documents (birth certificates, marriage certificates, police clearance) need certified translation to Thai, plus apostille from Russia.
Are Russian medical insurance policies accepted for O-A retirement? Few. Russian-domestic policies typically aren't on the Thai General Insurance Association approved list. Most Russian retirees buy a Thai-domestic insurance (Pacific Cross, Bupa Thailand, AXA Thailand) for O-A purposes.
How do I find Russian-speaking immigration help in Pattaya? Russkaya Pattaya Telegram channels, Pattaya-based Russian-speaking visa agents (verify they're actual Pattaya-registered businesses), Russian honorary consul service in Phuket. Verify any agent before paying — visa scams targeting Russian newcomers are widespread.
