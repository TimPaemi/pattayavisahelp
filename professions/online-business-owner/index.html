Thailand visa for online business owners

You run a Shopify store, an agency, a SaaS, an e-commerce empire, an Amazon FBA business, a course business, or a coaching practice. Your customers and team are global. Thailand is a tax-efficient, high-quality-of-life base. Here is the visa map.

Where you stand legally

If your business entity is incorporated outside Thailand (LLC in the US, GmbH in Germany, Pte Ltd in Singapore, sarl in France), and your Thai presence is "remote work for the foreign company you own", the DTV explicitly permits this. You do not need a Thai company, a Thai work permit, or Thai employees. You operate your foreign business from Thailand on a 5-year visa.

If you want to invoice Thai customers, hire Thai staff in Thailand, or claim Thai expenses against profit — you need a Thai company structure. That changes the visa to Non-B + work permit (or LTR Highly-Skilled if you qualify).

Pathway 1 — DTV (default for most)

Foreign business, foreign income, $80k+ or 500k THB liquid proof. 5 years multi-entry, 180 days per stay. The lowest-friction option for online business owners. Bangkok Bank accepts DTV for personal accounts; some banks need ฿200k cushion in the account.

Pathway 2 — LTR Work-from-Thailand Professional / Wealthy Global Citizen

Two LTR sub-types fit online business owners:

Work-from-Thailand Professional — you must be an employee (not owner) of a foreign company with $150M+ revenue, $80k+ personal income. Suits founders who structure as employees of their Cayman holding company.

Wealthy Global Citizen — $1M+ in liquid + investment assets, $80k+ annual income, $500k+ Thai investment (real estate or government bonds). Suits successful exited founders or established 7-figure operators. Royal Decree 743 tax exemption on foreign income.

Pathway 3 — Set up a Thai company + Non-B (for Thai-market expansion)

If you want to sell to Thai customers, hire Thai staff, or expense Thai operations, form a Thai limited company. ฿2M paid-up capital, 4 Thai employees minimum to sponsor your work permit, BOI promotion possible if your business sector qualifies (digital services, advanced manufacturing, food tech, etc.). Realistic incorporation cost: ฿300-600k all-in including 1-year bookkeeping retainer.

Pathway 4 — Privilege (the "do not want to optimise tax / just want to be left alone" option)

Thailand Privilege Membership (formerly Elite) at ฿900k for 5 years gives you visa stability, airport Fast Track, and zero work permit. Tax: same as DTV (no LTR-style exemption). Suits owners who do not want LTR's investment requirement and prefer pay-once-forget convenience.

Tax engineering — read carefully

Thailand's foreign-income taxation changed in January 2024. Money earned outside Thailand and remitted into Thailand is taxable at progressive rates 5-35%. There are three legal optimisation paths for online business owners:

  1. Spend overseas. Income held in your foreign account, used to pay foreign suppliers / family / international travel, is not Thai-taxable. Many DTV-holders run hybrid spending.
  2. LTR exemption. Royal Decree 743 makes foreign-source income earned and remitted to Thailand by Wealthy Pensioner / Wealthy Global Citizen LTR holders tax-exempt — provided certain conditions (income earned in prior tax year, etc.) are met.
  3. Days under 180. If your physical Thailand presence is under 180 days/year, you are not Thai tax-resident. Many DTV-holders use a 5-month-Thailand / 7-month-elsewhere rotation to stay non-resident.

For 7-figure-revenue businesses, the difference between LTR ($0 Thai tax on foreign-remitted income) and DTV (up to 35% on remitted income) can be hundreds of thousands of dollars per year. Get an actual Thai tax advisor before optimising.
