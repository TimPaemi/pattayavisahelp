## The basics

In Thailand, foreign drivers need a **Thai driving licence** if they intend to drive for more than 60 days. Short-term tourists can drive on an **International Driving Permit (IDP)** for up to 60 days; after that, conversion or fresh application is required.

**Pattaya residents** typically get their licences at the **Banglamung Department of Land Transport (DLT)** office, which serves the Pattaya/Chonburi area.

## Two main paths

### Path 1: Convert your foreign licence

If you have a valid driving licence from your home country, you can convert it to a Thai licence **without taking the practical driving test**.

**Eligibility:**
- Valid foreign driving licence (in original or with certified translation)
- Hold a long-stay visa or extension (or short-stay with sufficient documentation)
- Be in Thailand legally

**Resulting licence:** **5-year Thai driving licence** issued directly (skipping the 2-year temporary stage that fresh applicants get).

### Path 2: Fresh application (no foreign licence)

If you don't have a foreign licence (or it's expired beyond a certain age), you must apply fresh:

- Theory test (50 multiple-choice questions, English available)
- Aptitude tests (colour vision, depth perception, peripheral vision, brake reaction)
- Practical driving test (in DLT car or your own)
- Initial issuance: **2-year temporary licence** (renewable to 5-year after first 2-year period)

## Documents required (conversion path)

1. **Foreign driving licence** — original + certified translation (if not in English)
2. **Passport** — with valid visa/extension stamp
3. **Visa/extension page copy**
4. **TM30 receipt** (proof of address)
5. **Certificate of Residence** OR rental agreement (proof of Thai address)
6. **Medical certificate** — from any clinic, generally same-day, ~150–500 THB
7. **2 passport photos** (4×6 cm, recent)

**For the certificate of residence:** Issued by Jomtien Immigration (~500 THB, same-day or 1–2 days). See [G5 Jomtien Immigration practical guide](./G5-jomtien-immigration.md).

## Step-by-step process (conversion at Banglamung DLT)

### 1. Book appointment via DLT Smart Queue app

As of 2025, most DLT offices including Banglamung **require advance appointment booking** through the official DLT Smart Queue mobile app. Walk-ins are limited.

### 2. Get medical certificate

Visit any clinic on the morning of your appointment. Most Pattaya clinics do this routinely for 150–300 THB. Takes 10–15 minutes.

### 3. Bring all documents to DLT

Banglamung DLT, Sukhumvit Road, Banglamung district.

### 4. Document review

Officer checks all documents, verifies foreign licence, and reviews translations.

### 5. Aptitude tests (~10–15 minutes)

- **Colour vision** — identify red/yellow/green
- **Depth perception** — judge distance to moving objects
- **Peripheral vision** — identify lights at periphery
- **Brake reaction time** — press brake when light changes

### 6. Theory test

50 multiple-choice questions on Thai driving rules. Available in English, Chinese, Japanese, and Thai. Pass mark: **45/50**.

Sample questions cover road signs, right-of-way rules, speed limits, alcohol limits, accident procedures.

### 7. Practical test? — NO if converting

Conversion path **skips the practical driving test entirely**. You go straight from theory test to licence issuance.

### 8. Pay fees

- 5-year car licence: 505 THB
- 5-year motorbike licence: 305 THB

### 9. Receive licence (same day)

Photographed at DLT, licence printed and issued same-day.

## Using an agent

Pattaya has multiple driving licence service agents who handle the entire process:

- **Service fee:** 3,000–8,000 THB (in addition to government fees)
- **What they do:** Handle paperwork, translations, accompany to DLT, fast-track queues
- **Worth it for:** Non-English speakers, busy schedules, language barrier issues
- **Total cost with agent:** 6,000–13,000 THB

## Validity and renewal

| Licence type | Validity | Renewal |
|---|---|---|
| First-time licence (no foreign licence) | 2 years | Renewable to 5 years after first 2 years |
| Converted from foreign | 5 years | Renewable for 5 years |
| Subsequent renewals | 5 years | 5 years each |

**Renewal process:**

- Same documents as initial application
- New medical certificate
- New aptitude tests (no theory test on renewal)
- Fee: same as initial

**Renewal can be done up to 60 days before expiry** at any DLT office in Thailand (not just where you originally applied).

## Motorbike licence — separate from car

Thailand requires **separate licences for motorbikes vs cars**. Most Pattaya expats need both.

**Conversion option:** If your foreign motorbike licence allows full motorbike (not just moped), you can convert to Thai motorbike licence the same way — no practical test.

**Practical reality:** Many Pattaya expats ride motorbikes daily without proper licences. Police checks are common; **fines: 500–2,000 THB per stop**. Insurance won't cover accidents if you're unlicensed.

## International Driving Permit (IDP)

For short-term stays:

- IDP from your home country valid for up to **1 year in Thailand**
- Must be carried with original licence
- Acceptable for vehicle rental and police checks
- After 1 year residence, conversion or fresh application required

## Banglamung DLT — practical info

**Address:** Sukhumvit Road, Banglamung, Chonburi (the regional DLT for Pattaya area)

**Hours:** Mon–Fri, 08:30–16:30 (lunch break 12:00–13:00); Saturday limited service some weeks

**Smart Queue app:** Required for most appointments since 2025. Download "DLT Smart Queue" from app store.

**Languages:** Some staff English-capable; agents make life easier for non-English speakers

**Parking:** Available on site

## Insurance implications

Thai driving licence is required for:
- Standard motor insurance to cover accidents
- Vehicle rental from reputable agencies (some accept IDP)
- Some condo parking arrangements

**Driving without proper licence:** insurance void in case of accident — even if accident isn't your fault.

## 10 FAQ questions

**1. How long does the conversion take?**
Typically same-day at Banglamung DLT, assuming all documents ready and appointment booked. Plan for ~3–4 hours total at the office.

**2. Do I need to take the practical driving test?**
**No, if converting from a valid foreign licence.** Yes, if applying fresh (no foreign licence).

**3. What if my foreign licence is expired?**
Most DLT offices accept recently expired (within 2 years) for conversion. Severely expired licences may require fresh application path.

**4. Is the theory test really 50 questions?**
Yes — and you need 45/50 (90%) to pass. Available in English. Most expats pass first time after 30–60 minutes' study.

**5. Can I drive on my home licence in Thailand?**
For tourists: yes with IDP for up to 1 year. For long-term residents: should convert after 60 days.

**6. What about driving in other ASEAN countries?**
Thai licence is recognised in some ASEAN countries via mutual recognition agreements. Outside ASEAN, an IDP issued by Thailand is needed.

**7. Where do I get my foreign licence translated?**
Multiple Pattaya translation services charge 500–1,500 THB per document. DLT may also accept embassy-issued translations.

**8. What's the difference between car and motorbike licence?**
Two separate licences with separate tests/fees. Most expats get both. 1+B (motorcycle) or 1+B+C (truck) categories possible.

**9. Can I drive a small scooter on a car licence?**
**No** — separate motorbike licence required for any motorbike including 50cc scooters. Common mistake with serious legal/insurance consequences.

**10. Renewal abroad — possible?**
No — Thai driving licences must be renewed in Thailand at any DLT office. Can be done up to 60 days before expiry.
