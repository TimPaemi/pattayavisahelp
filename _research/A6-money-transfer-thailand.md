## Why this matters in 2026

Long-stay foreigners in Thailand routinely need to transfer significant amounts:

- **Retirement visa seasoning:** 800k–3M THB transfers (one-time)
- **Property purchase:** 3M–15M THB (typically requires FET form)
- **Monthly living expenses:** $2k–$10k/month
- **Visa fees and major expenses:** ad-hoc
- **Annual health insurance:** $1k–$5k/year

The cost of getting transfers wrong (poor exchange rates, fees, FET form missing) can run into thousands of dollars per year. Getting it right means the difference between a stressful retirement and a comfortable one.

## Major change for 2026: Wise launches Thai-licensed entity (May 2026)

**Effective on or after 19 May 2026**, Wise (formerly TransferWise) operates through its locally-incorporated Thai entity, fully licensed by the Bank of Thailand.

**What changed:**

### New capabilities for Thai users

- Thai users can send THB abroad **directly from a local Thai bank account** or from THB Wise balance
- Mid-market exchange rates with no hidden margin
- Fully regulated under Bank of Thailand (improved trust, dispute resolution)

### Important restrictions from May 2026

- **No more "transit transfers"** — moving money between two overseas accounts (e.g., US to Singapore) is no longer supported from Thai-based Wise accounts
- **No direct foreign currency to overseas:** Cannot send USD, GBP, EUR balances directly to overseas bank account; must convert to THB first, then send THB internationally
- These restrictions apply to **Thai-based accounts only**; non-Thai users are unaffected

**Practical implication:** If you maintain Wise primarily for in-Thailand use, the new local entity is great. If you use Wise as a cross-border treasury system, plan workarounds before May 2026.

### Wise (TransferWise) — the workhorse

**Pros:**

- Mid-market exchange rates with low transparent fees
- Free virtual debit card; Wise card available physically
- Multi-currency balance (50+ currencies)
- App-based, well-designed interface
- Now Bank of Thailand regulated (May 2026)

**Cons (post-May 2026 for Thai-based accounts):**

- No transit transfers
- Foreign currency must convert to THB before sending internationally
- 5,000 THB daily ATM limit on card

**Best for:** Most regular expat transfers. The default choice for most readers.

### Revolut — premium alternative

**Pros:**

- Strong multi-currency support
- Good app, free version available
- Useful for frequent travellers
- Premium tier offers higher limits and benefits

**Cons:**

- Not yet Bank of Thailand regulated (as of April 2026)
- Some Thai banks reject Revolut transfers for retirement deposit purposes
- Pricing less transparent than Wise

**Best for:** Frequent international travellers, premium users wanting cards in multiple currencies.

### SWIFT direct from home bank

**Pros:**

- Reliable, well-established
- High limits suitable for property purchases
- **Generates FET form automatically** (critical for property/retirement)

**Cons:**

- Higher fees (intermediary banks add ~$15–40 each)
- Worse exchange rates than Wise/Revolut
- Slower (2–5 business days)

**Best for:** **Large one-off transfers requiring FET form** (property purchase, retirement seasoning), high-amount transactions.

### Forex shops in Pattaya

Several Pattaya forex shops (especially on Beach Road, Soi Buakhao, Jomtien Beach Road) offer cash exchange:

- Used for: receiving cash from friends, visitors, exchanging Western Union, etc.
- Rates: typically 0.5–1.5% margin from mid-market
- **Not suitable** for retirement deposit (no FET form generated for cash exchange)

## The Foreign Exchange Transaction Form (FET)

**Critical for retirement visa and property purchase:**

- Issued by your **Thai bank** when foreign currency arrives
- Documents: amount in original currency, conversion to THB, sender/recipient details, purpose
- **Required for:** condominium purchase registration, retirement visa 800k seasoning verification (in some cases)
- **Free to issue** — Thai bank provides on request
- **Available for any amount** since 2018 (was $50,000+ minimum previously)

**How to ensure your transfer generates a usable FET:**

1. Send via **Bank wire (SWIFT)** to your Thai bank in your home currency
2. Thai bank receives, converts to THB, deposits in your Thai account
3. Request FET form from bank (some auto-generate, others require asking)
4. Keep original FET — Land Department / Immigration require physical document

**Wise / Revolut FET status:**

- **Wise (post-May 2026 Thai entity):** Thai bank may issue FET if Wise transfers in foreign currency. Verify with your bank before relying on this.
- **Revolut:** Less reliable for FET issuance; often treated as domestic transfer if THB
- **Best practice for property/retirement seasoning:** Use SWIFT direct or Wise pre-confirmed for FET, not standard fintech transfers

## Best practices for the 800k retirement seasoning

This is the highest-stakes transfer most retirees do. **Get it right or restart the 2-month seasoning clock.**

### Recommended approach

1. **Open Thai bank account first** (Bangkok Bank or Kasikorn — see G6)
2. **Transfer 800,000 THB minimum (~$22,500) via SWIFT** from home bank to Thai bank
3. **Send in foreign currency** (USD, GBP, EUR, etc.) — Thai bank converts on arrival
4. **Request FET form** at Thai bank when transfer received
5. **Don't touch the funds** for 2 months minimum (Jomtien seasoning rule)
6. **Get bank letter** dated same-day or previous day before extension appointment
7. **Keep balance always above 800k** during seasoning

### Common mistakes

- Transferring via Wise → bank doesn't issue FET → may complicate Jomtien
- Sending THB directly → no foreign currency conversion = no FET
- Multiple small transfers → harder to document; single large transfer cleaner
- Transferring just before appointment → fails 2-month seasoning

## Property purchase transfer

For 3M+ THB condo purchases, transfer flow:

1. SWIFT wire from home bank in foreign currency (USD, GBP, EUR)
2. Receive at Thai bank, request FET form
3. **FET amount must match purchase price** — coordinate carefully
4. Multiple transfers can be combined but each needs FET
5. Submit FET to Land Department with title transfer

**Critical:** Without FET, title cannot be registered in foreign name. Plan transfers 4–8 weeks before closing date.

## Crypto withdrawal to Thai bank

**Increasingly scrutinised in 2025–2026.** Thai banks now flag suspicious crypto inflows. Practical considerations:

- Use only **regulated Thai exchanges** (Bitkub, Satang, BX Thailand) for THB withdrawal
- Smaller amounts (<500k THB) typically pass without issue
- Larger amounts may trigger AML review and account freezes
- **Crypto is NOT a substitute for SWIFT for retirement seasoning** — won't generate FET, banks may reject

## Currency considerations

**Where to hold currency:**

- **GBP, USD, EUR, AUD:** Hold home country if rates moving against THB; convert to THB only as needed
- **THB:** Hold in Thai bank for daily expenses
- **Multi-currency balances:** Wise / Revolut allow holding multiple currencies; convert on favorable rates

**THB exchange rate trends (April 2026):**

- Roughly 35–37 THB per USD
- Roughly 44–46 THB per GBP
- Roughly 38–40 THB per EUR

Rates fluctuate daily; Wise app shows mid-market for reference.

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. What's the cheapest way to transfer money to Thailand?**
**Wise** for routine transfers. **SWIFT** for large transfers requiring FET. Avoid Western Union for non-urgent transfers (high fees).

**2. Will Wise still work in Thailand after May 2026?**
**Yes** — Wise operates through its Thai-licensed entity. Some restrictions for Thai-based accounts (no transit transfers, must convert to THB before sending internationally), but core functionality intact.

**3. Do I need an FET for the 800k retirement deposit?**
Not strictly required by Jomtien for the deposit (bank passbook + bank letter are typically enough), but **strongly recommended** for documentation. SWIFT transfers automatically generate FET.

**4. How long does a SWIFT transfer take?**
Typically 2–5 business days. Direct correspondent banking can be faster (1–2 days).

**5. Can I avoid bank fees by using Wise to transfer to my Thai account?**
Often yes — Wise typically beats SWIFT on fees and exchange rate. **However**, for property purchase or retirement seasoning, SWIFT may be preferred for FET generation.

**6. What if my home bank charges high SWIFT fees?**
Compare with Wise. Often Wise is cheaper even with home bank's reduced SWIFT fee. Some home banks offer "premium" accounts with reduced or waived SWIFT fees for international clients.

**7. Are there limits on how much I can transfer to Thailand?**
**Generally no upper limit** for legitimate purposes. Banks may flag unusual patterns for AML review. Transfers over $10k USD typically trigger reporting (standard, not blocking).

**8. Should I hold THB or my home currency?**
For daily expenses: hold THB in Thai bank. For larger sums: home currency in home country, convert as needed. Multi-currency Wise/Revolut balance offers flexibility.
