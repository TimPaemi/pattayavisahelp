## At a glance

- **Visa-exempt entry:** 60 days for Indian passport holders (extended from earlier 30-day rule)
- **Indians in Pattaya:** Growing community; popular with retirees and businesspeople
- **Most popular long-stay visa:** Non-O retirement (50+); Non-B (business); DTV (remote workers)
- **Critical India-specific issue:** Document legalisation is multi-step (no apostille); banking via UPI/forex requires planning

## India-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance certificate

For Non-O-A, Non-O-X, LTR applications:

- **Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)** — issued by Regional Passport Office (RPO) in India
- Apply via Passport Seva (passportindia.gov.in)
- Cost: ₹500–₹2,000 depending on processing
- Time: 1–3 weeks

### Document legalisation chain — India is NOT a Hague signatory

India uses direct consular legalisation, not apostille:

1. **Notary attestation** in India
2. **Home Department / Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM)** attestation
3. **Ministry of External Affairs (MEA)** authentication
4. **Royal Thai Embassy New Delhi** certification
5. **Thai translation** in Pattaya (Hindi/English→Thai depending on document language)
6. **Thai MFA legalisation** in Bangkok
7. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Indian chain:** 6–10 weeks (multi-step Indian authentication is the bottleneck), ~$100–200.

## Indian financial considerations

### LRS (Liberalised Remittance Scheme)

Indian residents can remit up to **USD 250,000 per financial year** under LRS for permitted purposes including:

- Education abroad
- Maintenance of relatives abroad
- Investment abroad
- Property purchase abroad

For Pattaya retirement: 800,000 THB ≈ USD 22,500 — well within annual LRS limit.

### Tax compliance

- **India-Thailand DTA** exists
- **Indian tax residency:** Complex tests; living abroad >182 days typically reduces Indian tax obligation
- **Thai tax residency:** 180+ days/year triggers Thai tax on remitted foreign income
- **TCS (Tax Collected at Source):** 20% TCS may apply on certain LRS remittances above threshold

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Is the visa-exempt period 60 days for Indians?**
**Yes** — Thailand extended visa-free entry from 30 to 60 days for Indian passport holders. Confirmed for 2026 (subject to proposed 30-day reduction).

**2. How do Indians fund the 800,000 THB retirement deposit?**
Via LRS — annual USD 250,000 limit covers this comfortably. SWIFT transfer from major Indian bank (HDFC, ICICI, SBI, Axis) to Thai bank generates FET form.

**3. What about OCI cardholders?**
OCI (Overseas Citizen of India) holders generally apply on their non-Indian passport for Thai visas. OCI status is a separate document from Indian passport.

**4. Can I run my Indian business remotely from Pattaya?**
Possible via DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) if you earn foreign-source income from your Indian business. Direct work for Thai entities requires Non-B + work permit.

**5. Is the Indian community large in Pattaya?**
Growing — particularly among retirees and businesspeople. Smaller than UK or German communities but established. Indian restaurants, cultural events present in Pattaya.

**6. Can I send money home from Thailand?**
Yes — Wise, SWIFT from Thai bank to Indian bank work. Thai 2024 tax rule may affect future remittances of Indian income brought back to India (consult tax specialist).

**7. Driving licence conversion?**
Indian driving licence can be converted to Thai. **India is NOT a Hague signatory**, so additional authentication may be required. Translation needed.

**8. Healthcare access?**
Bangkok Hospital Pattaya has English-speaking and increasingly Indian-language-aware staff. Cost similar or slightly less than premium Indian private hospitals; international standards.
