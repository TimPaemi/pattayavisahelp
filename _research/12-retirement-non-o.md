## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant O retirement path is **the most commonly used retirement visa route in Pattaya** — arguably the most popular route among all of Thailand's retirement options.

It is technically not a standalone "retirement visa" — it is a **1-year Extension of Stay based on retirement**, issued to foreigners already in Thailand on an initial 90-day Non-Immigrant O visa. The defining characteristic of this path is that the critical paperwork is done **inside Thailand at the local Immigration office** — not at a Thai embassy abroad — which many retirees find simpler and more forgiving than the Non-O-A's embassy-first requirement.

It is the preferred path for retirees who arrive in Thailand first, open a bank account, season their funds, and then apply entirely through **Jomtien Immigration** — with no embassy visit and, critically, **no mandatory health insurance requirement**. (siam-legal+2, youtube, casapattaya+2)

## How it differs from Non-O-A (the key distinction)

This page must make this difference crystal clear — it is the most-searched question among Pattaya retirees: (youtube)

| | Non-O-A | Non-O (In-country path) |
|---|---|---|
| Where applied | Thai Embassy/Consulate abroad | Jomtien Immigration (in Thailand) |
| First step | Embassy issues 1-year visa | Embassy or visa-exempt entry → 90-day Non-O stamp |
| **Health insurance** | **Mandatory** (40k OPD / 400k IPD minimum) | **Not required by law for in-country extension** (thaiembassy+1) |
| Criminal background check | Required at embassy stage | Not required in-country |
| Medical certificate | Often required | Not required |
| Financial requirement | Same: 800k THB or 65k/month | Same: 800k THB or 65k/month |
| Who prefers it | Those who want 1-year visa before arriving | Those who arrive first, then apply in-country |

**The health insurance difference is the single biggest practical reason many retirees choose the in-country Non-O path** — particularly older retirees who struggle to find qualifying insurance at affordable premiums. (thaiembassy+2)

## Eligibility (full list)

- Must be **aged 50 years or older** on the day of application (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- Must hold a valid Non-Immigrant O visa (90-day) at the time of applying for the 1-year extension — this is the prerequisite (pcec+1)
- Not prohibited from entering Thailand under the Immigration Act B.E. 2522 (image.mfa)
- Must meet one of the three financial qualifications (see below) (pcec)
- **Employment of any kind strictly prohibited** throughout stay (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- Must have a verifiable Thai address (rental agreement, utility bills, TM30 receipt) (thaiembassy+1)
- **No criminal background check required** for in-country extension (contrast with O-A) (pcec)
- **No medical certificate required** for in-country extension (pcec)
- **No mandatory health insurance** for the Non-O in-country path (contrast with O-A) (issacompass+1)

## Required documents

### Stage 1 — Obtaining the initial 90-day Non-Immigrant O visa

The initial Non-O can be obtained in three ways:

**Route A — From embassy/consulate abroad** (most common for first-timers):

1. Valid passport (≥6 months) (image.mfa)
2. Completed visa application form (image.mfa)
3. Passport photo (image.mfa)
4. Bank statement showing at least 800,000 THB (or income evidence of 65,000 THB/month) (pcec+1)
5. Return flight booking or itinerary (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)
6. Hotel/accommodation booking (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)

**Route B — In-country conversion from tourist visa or visa-exempt entry (TM.86/TM.87):**

1. Passport (original + copies of all relevant pages) (pcec)
2. **TM.86 form** (if converting from tourist TR visa) or **TM.87 form** (if converting from visa-exempt entry) (pcec)
3. Passport photo (pcec)
4. Thai bank book showing 800,000 THB on deposit for at least **2 months prior to application** — the 2-month seasoning rule applies at Jomtien (pcec+1)
5. TM30 accommodation notification receipt (facebook+1)
6. Fee: 2,000 THB (in-country status change) **[verify]**

### Stage 2 — Applying for the 1-year Extension of Stay at Jomtien

*Applied within the last 30 days of the 90-day Non-O period — or after at least 60 days in Thailand*

1. **TM.7 Extension of Stay application form** (facebook+1)
2. Passport — original + full copies (biodata page, current Non-O visa stamp, entry stamp, all recent stamps) (facebook+1)
3. Passport photo (facebook+1)
4. **Thai bank book (passbook)** showing balance of at least 800,000 THB for **2 months prior to application date** (pcec+1)
   - Jomtien's specific policy: 2-month seasoning required even though the official national rule does not always specify this (pcec)
5. **Thai bank letter/certificate** confirming current balance ≥800,000 THB (obtained from your Thai bank branch, usually same-day) (casapattaya+1)
6. TM30 accommodation receipt (proof of address) (facebook+1)
7. Rental agreement or utility bills showing Thai address (thaiembassy+1)
8. Extension fee: 1,900 THB cash (pcec)

**Income method (if using 65,000 THB/month instead of bank deposit):**

1. Embassy income letter (from your home country's embassy in Bangkok)
   - **Note: Australian, UK, and US embassies no longer issue income letters; their nationals must use the 800,000 THB bank deposit route** (pcec)

## Financial requirements (THB and USD, current 2026 figures)

Three methods — only one needs to be met:

| Method | Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Bank deposit | 800,000 THB in Thai bank (≈$22,500) maintained for at least 2 months prior to extension (pcec+1) | Most common — Jomtien enforces the 2-month seasoning |
| Monthly income | 65,000 THB/month via embassy income letter (pcec) | **Not available to UK, US, Australian passport holders** — their embassies stopped issuing income letters |
| Combined | Bank balance + annual income totalling 800,000 THB/year (thaiembassy) | Less commonly used in practice |

**Seasoning specifics at Jomtien:** The national Immigration rule doesn't always specify a seasoning period, but Jomtien Immigration's local practice is to require the 800,000 THB to have been on deposit for at least 2 months before application — **this is a Pattaya-specific enforcement point that catches many first-time applicants.** (pcec)

## Application process — step by step

### The standard Pattaya path — most common route

1. **Arrive in Thailand** — on a tourist visa (TR), visa-exempt entry, or an initial 90-day Non-O from a Thai embassy abroad (siam-legal+1)
2. **Open a Thai bank account** and deposit at least 800,000 THB — allow funds to season for 2 months before applying at Jomtien (casapattaya+1)
3. **If on tourist/exempt entry:** Visit Jomtien Immigration at least 15 days before your stay expires to file TM.86 or TM.87 to convert to a 90-day Non-Immigrant O status — submit financial documents, TM30, accommodation proof (casapattaya+1)
4. Receive 90-day Non-Immigrant O stamp (processing: ~15 working days for in-country conversion; applications go to Bangkok HQ) (pcec)
5. Within the **last 30 days of the 90-day Non-O** (or after a minimum of 60 days in Thailand), visit Jomtien Immigration again to apply for the 1-year Extension of Stay (thaiembassy+1)
6. Submit TM.7, passport, bank book, bank letter, TM30, address proof, photo (facebook+1)
7. Pay **1,900 THB** (pcec)
8. Receive 1-year Extension of Stay stamp — valid from the day of issue (siam-legal+1)
9. **Renew annually at Jomtien:** submit the same documents with an updated bank book, updated bank letter, and updated TM30 (siam-legal)

### From embassy Non-O (already have 90-day Non-O from abroad)

Skip steps 2–4 above. Arrive in Thailand, wait 60 days, then go directly to Jomtien in the last 30 days of the 90-day stay to apply for the 1-year extension (step 5 onwards). (banyangroupresidences+2)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Initial 90-day Non-O:**

- At a Thai Embassy/Consulate abroad OR
- In-country conversion from tourist/exempt status at Jomtien Immigration (TM.86/TM.87) (pcec)

**1-year Extension of Stay:**

- **Entirely in Thailand — at Jomtien Immigration Office for Pattaya/Chonburi residents** (pcec+1)

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial Non-O visa (embassy):** 90-day single-entry permission to stay (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- **1-year Extension of Stay:** 365 days from date of issue (casapattaya+1)
- **Renewals: Unlimited** — every 12 months at Jomtien indefinitely, as long as financial and age criteria are met (siam-legal+1)
- **There is no total maximum stay** on the Non-O retirement path — retirees renew every year for as long as they wish to remain (siam-legal)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — standard 90-day TM47 reporting required.** Every 90 days, Non-O extension holders must file a TM47 address report:

- Online via immigration.go.th (preferred by many Pattaya retirees)
- In person at Jomtien (queue required)
- By post (available but unreliable)

**Pattaya-specific:** Many Non-O retirees align their annual renewal visit with their 90-day report to minimise Jomtien trips — if the timing works out, both can be handled at the same appointment. (pcec)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required if leaving Thailand:**

- Single re-entry: 1,000 THB
- Multiple re-entry: 3,800 THB

The re-entry permit must be purchased **before exiting Thailand**. Leaving without one cancels the 1-year extension — a common and painful mistake. Available at Jomtien Immigration or at the airport on departure (airport purchase is riskier — see pitfalls). (visa.thainexus)

## Common pitfalls

1. **The 2-month seasoning trap at Jomtien:** Jomtien Immigration requires the 800,000 THB to have been sitting in the Thai bank account for a full 2 months before they will accept the application — even though this is not always stated as a national rule. Retirees who deposit 800,000 THB one week before applying are turned away. **Plan ahead: the clock starts the day you deposit, not the day you decide to apply.** (pcec)
2. **UK/US/Australian income letter — it doesn't exist anymore:** Many retirees from the UK, US, and Australia arrive expecting to use an Embassy income letter to prove 65,000 THB/month rather than the 800,000 THB deposit. **Their embassies stopped issuing these letters years ago.** UK, US, and Australian nationals must use the bank deposit method — no exceptions. (pcec)
3. **Exiting Thailand without a re-entry permit:** This is the most frequent costly mistake for Non-O extension holders. A retiree travels to Singapore for a medical appointment or flies home for a family event without purchasing a re-entry permit first. They return to Thailand to find their 1-year extension has been cancelled and they must restart the entire process — including reseasoning 800,000 THB for 2 months. (visa.thainexus)
4. **Bank balance dipping below 800,000 THB mid-year:** Living expenses, medical costs, or simply forgetting to monitor the account can cause the balance to drop below threshold. If an officer checks the bank book at renewal and sees the balance dipped even briefly, it can cause problems. **Many experienced Pattaya retirees keep a separate "visa account" holding exactly 800,000 THB and draw living expenses from a separate account.** (casapattaya+1)
5. **Confusing the visa type with the extension stamp:** Once on a 1-year extension, you are technically not "on a visa" — you are on an Extension of Stay. This matters when re-entering Thailand: the passport stamp says "Extension of Stay," not "Non-O Visa." Border officers and some airlines misread this. **Always carry a copy of your full extension stamp and a current re-entry permit.** (asq)
6. **TM30 not filed by landlord — Jomtien turns you away:** Jomtien Immigration requires the TM30 accommodation notification receipt at every extension. If your landlord (condo juristic office, house owner) has not filed the TM30, or filed it with an error, you will be turned away and must fix the TM30 before reapplying. **In Pattaya, many older condo buildings or private landlords handle TM30 inconsistently — verify yours before your appointment.** (facebook)
7. **Applying too early:** Extensions must be applied for within the **last 30 days of the current permission to stay**. Arriving at Jomtien 45 days early will result in the officer telling you to come back. Time the appointment for day 60–75 of the 90-day Non-O or within the last 30 days of the annual extension. (thaiembassy+1)

## Pattaya/Jomtien-specific process detail

**The Non-O retirement in-country path is the single most Pattaya-relevant visa process on your entire site.** Thousands of Pattaya retirees go through Jomtien Immigration every year for their annual extension.

**Standard Jomtien Non-O Extension day:**

1. Arrive by 07:00–08:00 — take a queue number at the front (pcec)
2. At your counter: officer checks all documents, bank book, bank letter, TM30, passport copies
3. Officer may ask you to bring the bank letter if it's more than 1 day old — some Jomtien officers insist on a bank letter dated the same day or the day before. **Get your bank letter on the morning of your appointment.**
4. Pay 1,900 THB cash — bring exact change or close to it (pcec)
5. Extension stamp issued — same day, typically within 1–2 hours (casapattaya+1)

**Jomtien-specific enforcement notes:**

- 2-month seasoning enforced locally even if not always national policy (pcec)
- **Dress code: no shorts or flip-flops — formal attire required** (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)
- Officers typically fluent enough in English for retirement extension appointments (pcec)
- The 30-day application window is strictly enforced — come too early and you'll be turned away (thaiembassy)
- Pattaya City Expats Club (PCEC) maintains an updated guide to Jomtien Non-O procedures that is widely used and respected by the community (pcec+1)

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. What's the difference between Non-O-A and Non-O retirement visa?**
Non-O-A is applied for at a Thai embassy abroad, issues a 1-year visa, and requires health insurance. Non-O (in-country path) starts with a 90-day entry, then extends at Jomtien — same financial requirements, but **no mandatory health insurance and less embassy paperwork**. Both end with the same practical result: annual 1-year extensions. (youtube, thaiembassy)

**2. Do I need health insurance for the Non-O in-country retirement extension?**
**No** — health insurance is not required by law for the Non-O in-country extension, unlike the Non-O-A which mandates it. However, private health insurance is strongly recommended given the cost of private hospitals in Pattaya. (thaiembassy+1)

**3. How long does the 800,000 THB need to be in the Thai bank before I apply?**
At Jomtien Immigration, the local practice is **2 months (approximately 60 days) of seasoning** before the application date. This is a Jomtien-specific enforcement point — book your extension appointment no earlier than 2 months after depositing. (pcec)

**4. Can UK, US, or Australian retirees use the income letter method?**
**No** — their embassies no longer issue income letters for Thai retirement visa purposes. British, American, and Australian nationals must use the 800,000 THB Thai bank deposit method. (pcec)

**5. Can I apply for the Non-O retirement extension if I arrived on a tourist visa?**
Yes — you first need to convert from tourist (TR) status to Non-Immigrant O status at Jomtien using TM.86. Once you have the 90-day Non-O stamp, you wait 60 days and then apply for the 1-year extension in the final 30 days of that 90-day period. (casapattaya+1)

**6. What if I can't make it to Jomtien before my extension expires?**
You can apply for a 30-day extension of stay (1,900 THB) to buy yourself time before submitting the retirement extension application — but only if you apply **before** your current permission expires, not after. (pcec)

**7. How many times can I renew the Non-O retirement extension?**
**Indefinitely** — there is no maximum number of annual renewals. You can renew every year as long as you continue to meet the age and financial requirements. (siam-legal)

**8. Can I leave Thailand on the Non-O extension?**
Yes — but you must purchase a re-entry permit at Jomtien (or at the airport) before leaving. **Leaving without one cancels your extension permanently.** (visa.thainexus)

**9. What bank is best for the 800,000 THB retirement deposit in Pattaya?**
Bangkok Bank and Kasikorn Bank (KBank) are the most popular among Pattaya retirees for retirement fund accounts. Both issue same-day balance certificates that Jomtien Immigration accepts. Branches near Jomtien or on Pattaya Klang are most convenient — verify current opening hours before your extension appointment. **[verify specific branches]**

**10. Is the Non-O retirement extension the same as the Non-O-A?**
No — they are the same financial requirement (800,000 THB) and same end result (1-year stay), but they are technically different. The Non-O-A is a visa with an annual extension. The Non-O in-country path results in an Extension of Stay stamp — not a visa. **This distinction matters for re-entry and some third-country visa applications.** (asq)

**11. Can I work part-time on the Non-O retirement extension?**
No — any employment, part-time, remote, or casual, is strictly prohibited. This includes teaching English for cash, selling products online, or freelancing. (image.mfa+1)

**12. If I get married to a Thai national while on Non-O retirement, what happens?**
You can switch the basis of your extension from "retirement" to "marriage to Thai national" at the next renewal — which **reduces the financial requirement from 800,000 THB to 400,000 THB**. This is a common strategy for Pattaya retirees who marry their Thai partner. **[verify Jomtien switching procedure]**

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **No structural changes to Non-O retirement requirements:** The 800,000 THB deposit, 65,000 THB/month income option, age 50+ requirement, and 1,900 THB annual fee remain unchanged (wochenblitzenglish, youtube)
- **Health insurance remains NOT required for in-country Non-O extensions:** Despite periodic rumours of changes, as of April 2026 the in-country Non-O extension path **does not legally mandate health insurance** — only the O-A path does (isanrealestate+2)
- **In-country conversion streamlined:** The TM.86/87 in-country conversion process (tourist to Non-O) is better understood by Jomtien staff in 2026 vs the confusion of earlier years (casapattaya)
- **TDAC replaces TM6:** Returning from overseas trips now requires pre-travel TDAC digital arrival card completion — affects all retirees with re-entry permits (thailandinsiderguide)
- **Jomtien bank letter same-day enforcement:** Anecdotally, Jomtien officers in 2025–2026 more frequently insist on the bank letter being dated the same day or previous day — get the letter the morning of your appointment, not the day before **[verify current Jomtien practice]**
- **Thai tax residency now a concern:** Retirees spending 180+ days per year in Thailand triggered by the 2024 foreign income remittance tax change should now consider whether their pension or investment income imported into Thailand to meet the 800,000 THB deposit requirement is taxable — a new consideration for 2026 that intersects visa and tax planning (mbmg-group)
