## Russia-specific visa context

### Visa-free entry confirmed for 2026

- **60 days visa-exempt** for Russian passport holders
- 30-day extension available at Immigration (1,900 THB)
- **2-per-year land border cap and 6-per-year air cap** apply
- Subject to proposed reduction to 30 days (under review as of April 2026)

### Document legalisation chain

Russia uses both apostille and consular legalisation:

1. **Russian authentication** — Apostille from Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs OR regional Russian authority (depends on document type)
2. **Royal Thai Embassy Moscow** certification
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya (Russian→Thai specialist; 1,500–3,000 THB)
4. **Thai MFA legalisation** in Bangkok
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Russian chain:** 5–9 weeks, equivalent of $150–300.

### Police clearance

Russian Ministry of Internal Affairs (МВД) issues background certificate. Cost typically RUB 2,500. Time 2–4 weeks. Apply at local police station or online via gosuslugi.ru.

## Russia-specific challenges in 2026

### Banking sanctions and remittance

Following 2022 sanctions, Russian banking links to international SWIFT system are restricted:

- **Major Russian banks** (Sberbank, VTB, etc.) — mostly disconnected from SWIFT
- **Some Russian banks** still operate limited international transfers via correspondent banking
- **Practical workarounds:**
  - **CIS bank intermediaries** (banks in Kazakhstan, Armenia, Kyrgyzstan)
  - **Crypto** (USDT, BTC) — popular but Thai bank acceptance variable
  - **Cash transfer via couriered bank notes** (up to USD 10k declared)
  - **UAE / Turkey banking hubs** for Russians who have those relationships

### Currency considerations

- **RUB exchange:** weak vs THB; large fluctuations
- Holding USD/EUR/AED is common practice for Russians abroad
- THB conversion via crypto-to-bank is widespread but increasingly scrutinised

### Tax considerations

- **Russia-Thailand DTA** exists
- **Thai tax residency** at 180+ days/year applies
- **Russian tax residency:** complex; living abroad doesn't automatically end Russian tax obligation
- Many Russian expats use professional advice for cross-border tax planning

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Is Thailand still visa-free for Russian citizens in 2026?**
**Yes** — 60 days visa-exempt confirmed for 2026. Subject to the proposed 30-day reduction under review.

**2. How do Russians transfer money to Thailand under sanctions?**
Common workarounds: CIS bank intermediaries (Kazakhstan, Armenia), crypto-to-Thai bank (with caution), UAE/Turkey banking hubs, courier of declared cash. **Many Russians use a Pattaya-based money transfer specialist** familiar with Russian-Thai remittance.

**3. Can I open a Thai bank account as a Russian?**
**Yes** with a long-stay visa (Non-O retirement, DTV, etc.). Tourist/visa-exempt entry: rejected. See [G6 Bank account guide](./G6-thai-bank-account.md).

**4. Where do Russians live in Pattaya?**
Concentrated heavily in Jomtien (especially View Talay buildings), parts of Pratumnak, some North Pattaya. Specific buildings have 50%+ Russian residents.

**5. Is the Privilege (Elite) Visa popular among Russians?**
**Yes** — Privilege is among the top long-stay choices for affluent Russian expats due to no income proof requirements (helpful given banking complexity) and full multi-year stays.

**6. Can my children attend Russian-language school in Pattaya?**
**Yes** — Russian-language schools and tutoring services exist in Pattaya. International schools (Regents, ISE) also have Russian-speaking student communities.

**7. What about Russian driving licence?**
Hague Convention member; conversion to Thai licence at Banglamung DLT possible (skip practical test). Russian→Thai translation needed (~1,500 THB).

**8. What's the most common visa choice for Russian working-age expats?**
**DTV** for those with foreign-source remote income. **Non-O Marriage** for those married to Thai. **LTR** if income/assets meet thresholds. **Non-B** if working for Thai/foreign-licensed employer in Thailand.
