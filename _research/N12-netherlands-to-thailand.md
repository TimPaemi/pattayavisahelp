## At a glance

- **Visa-exempt entry:** 60 days for Dutch passport holders
- **Dutch in Pattaya:** Established community
- **Most popular long-stay visa:** Non-O retirement, DTV, LTR
- **Netherlands eligible** for Non-O-X (14-country list)

## Netherlands-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance

- **Verklaring Omtrent het Gedrag (VOG)** — Certificate of Good Conduct
- Cost: ~€41
- Time: 4–6 weeks
- Apply via Dienst Justis

### Document legalisation

Netherlands is Hague Apostille Convention member:

1. **Apostille from designated court** (Rechtbank)
2. **Royal Thai Embassy The Hague** certification
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya
4. **Thai MFA legalisation**
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Dutch chain:** 5–8 weeks.

## Dutch tax considerations

### Tax residency

- **Dutch fiscal residency** based on facts (centre of life, dwelling, family ties)
- Box 1/2/3 system continues for Dutch tax residents
- 30% ruling for inbound expats doesn't apply outbound

### Pensions

- **AOW (state pension):** portable to Thailand; taxable in Thailand if remitted (Thai tax resident); Dutch tax credit
- **Pillar 2 pensions** (workplace, ABP, etc.): typically taxable in Thailand if remitted
- **Pillar 3 (private):** similar treatment

### Netherlands-Thailand DTA

Provides credits, source rules, tie-breaker.

## Dutch community in Pattaya

- Substantial community
- **Dutch Society Pattaya** — social organization
- Concentrated in Jomtien, Pratumnak
- Dutch-language services available (some restaurants, agents)

## Royal Thai Embassy The Hague

- **Address:** Buitenrustweg 1, 2517 KD The Hague
- Visa applications, document certification

## Dutch Embassy in Thailand

- **Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands Bangkok:** 15 Soi Tonson, Lumpini, Bangkok 10330
- **Honorary Consulate Pattaya:** routine matters

## 6 FAQ questions

**1. Non-O-X eligibility?** Yes, Netherlands on the 14-country list.

**2. AOW portability?** Yes; AOW state pension paid into Thai bank. Reduction may apply for short Dutch residency periods.

**3. Where do Dutch expats live?** Jomtien primarily.

**4. Healthcare?** Basisverzekering doesn't cover Thailand; many use Thai or international expat insurance.

**5. Driving licence?** Hague signatory; conversion at Banglamung DLT.

**6. Tax residency formalities?** Establishing Dutch non-residency requires careful documentation. Departure tax considerations (Box 3) may apply.
