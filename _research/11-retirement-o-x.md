## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant O-X is Thailand's 10-year retirement visa — the longest single-issuance retirement visa available. It was designed as a premium long-stay option for high-net-worth retirees who want maximum certainty and minimum annual bureaucracy. It operates on a **5+5 structure**: the initial visa is valid for 5 years with multiple entry, renewable for a further 5 years — giving a total potential stay of 10 years.

Despite its appeal on paper, the O-X **remains significantly less popular than the Non-O-A in practice**, primarily because it requires a Thai bank deposit of **3,000,000 THB (roughly $84,000)** — nearly four times the 800,000 THB required for the O-A — and is only available to nationals of **14 specific countries**.

The O-X suits high-net-worth retirees from those eligible countries who want to commit to long-term Thailand residency without annual bureaucracy and can absorb the large deposit requirement. (consular.mfa+3, youtube)

## Eligibility (full list)

- Must be **aged 50 years or older** on the day of application (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+2)
- Must hold a passport and nationality from one of **14 eligible countries only**:
  1. Japan
  2. Australia
  3. Denmark
  4. Finland
  5. France
  6. Germany
  7. Italy
  8. Netherlands
  9. Norway
  10. Sweden
  11. Switzerland
  12. United Kingdom
  13. Canada
  14. United States of America (conciergepattaya+1)
- Must not be prohibited from entering Thailand under the Immigration Act B.E. 2522 (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)
- Must not have a criminal record that would bar entry to Thailand (criminal background check required) (conciergepattaya)
- Must not have certain forbidden illnesses: leprosy, tuberculosis, elephantiasis, syphilis, drug addiction (conciergepattaya)
- **Employment of any kind is strictly prohibited throughout the stay** (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
- Must hold health insurance covering the entire 5-year visa period (specific coverage minimums: **[verify]** — commonly cited as **120,000 THB outpatient / 3,000,000 THB inpatient** — higher tier than O-A's 40,000 / 400,000 THB) (conciergepattaya)
- Must meet financial requirements (see below)

## Required documents

1. Valid passport — minimum 6 months' validity from date of entry, with copies of all relevant pages (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
2. Three completed visa application forms (conciergepattaya)
3. Three recent passport-sized photographs (4×6 cm, white background) (conciergepattaya)
4. **Thai bank certificate showing deposit of not less than 3,000,000 THB** (or 1,800,000 THB + qualifying income — see financial section) in a Thai bank account in the applicant's name (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
5. Bank passbook (if applicable) showing deposit history (conciergepattaya)
6. **Criminal background check** (FBI for US, ACRO for UK, equivalent for other eligible nationalities) (conciergepattaya)
7. Medical certificate from an accredited physician in country of residence — confirming no forbidden illnesses (conciergepattaya)
8. **Health insurance certificate covering the full 5-year period of stay** (conciergepattaya)
9. Evidence of address in Thailand (hotel booking, rental agreement, or host letter) (conciergepattaya)
10. Personal biography/CV — basic personal background (conciergepattaya)

**For the second 5-year extension (in-country renewal):**

## Financial requirements (THB and USD, current 2026 figures)

The O-X offers two financial routes:

| Route | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Full deposit | Thai bank deposit of **3,000,000 THB (~$84,000 / £67,000)** held in Thailand throughout the visa period (conciergepattaya+1) |
| Reduced deposit + income | Thai bank deposit of **1,800,000 THB (~$50,000) plus regular income** making up the balance to 3,000,000 THB per year (consular.mfa+1) |

**Key difference from O-A:** The O-A requires only 800,000 THB deposit (or 65,000 THB/month income). The O-X requires 3,000,000 THB — **this single factor is why most retirees choose O-A over O-X despite the 10-year duration on offer.**

**Health insurance cost estimate (for 5-year O-X policy):** Multi-year or annually renewable policies covering the higher O-X thresholds (**[verify]** 120,000 THB outpatient / 3,000,000 THB inpatient) are substantially more expensive than O-A insurance. Annual premiums for applicants aged 60–75 from approved Thai insurers typically range widely but are higher-tier products — **budget 50,000–120,000+ THB/year depending on age and provider** **[verify with current insurer pricing]**.

## Application process — step by step

The Non-O-X must be applied for at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate **abroad** — it cannot be applied for from inside Thailand:

1. **Open a Thai bank account** (or transfer to an existing Thai account) and deposit the required 3,000,000 THB (or 1,800,000 THB if using the income route) — the bank will issue a certificate (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
2. **Arrange health insurance** — obtain a policy (from a Thai-approved insurer) covering the first 5-year period (conciergepattaya)
3. **Obtain criminal background check** from your country's national authority — allow 4–8 weeks for most Western countries (conciergepattaya)
4. **Obtain medical certificate** from an accredited doctor in your country of residence (conciergepattaya)
5. Prepare full document set (see checklist above) (conciergepattaya)
6. **Apply in person at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate** in your home country (or country of legal residence) — not available via e-Visa portal due to document complexity (consular.mfa+1)
7. Attend interview/submission appointment; pay visa fee of 10,000 THB equivalent (consular.mfa)
8. Visa issued — **5 years' validity, multiple entry** (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
9. Enter Thailand and begin stay — **no further annual Immigration office visits required** during the 5-year period for extensions (unlike O-A which requires annual Jomtien visits) (youtube, ottawa.thaiembassy)
10. **90-day TM47 reports still required throughout** (youtube)
11. Prior to 5-year expiry, apply for the **second 5-year extension** — done at Immigration Bureau in Thailand (Jomtien for Pattaya residents) with updated financial and insurance documentation (ottawa.thaiembassy)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

- **Initial O-X visa:** At a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in an eligible country — **not available in-country or via e-Visa** (consular.mfa+1)
- **5-year extension** (after first 5-year period): At Thai Immigration Bureau — **Jomtien Immigration Office for Pattaya/Chonburi residents** (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- Must apply at the embassy of your eligible nationality country (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)

## Duration & extensions

- **Visa validity:** 5 years from date of issue, multiple entry (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- **Permission to stay per entry:** 5 years (holders do not need annual Immigration visits during the first 5-year period) (youtube)
- **Extension:** One further 5 years at Thai Immigration (Jomtien for Pattaya) — provided financial and insurance criteria are still met (consular.mfa+1)
- **Maximum total stay: 10 years** (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
- **After 10 years:** Must apply for a new O-X visa (or switch to O-A or other long-stay category) — there is no further automatic extension beyond the 10-year total (youtube)
- **Employment:** Strictly prohibited throughout (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — standard 90-day TM47 reporting applies throughout the O-X stay.** This is a notable practical disadvantage compared to the LTR Wealthy Pensioner (annual reporting only):

- TM47 reports every 90 days at Jomtien (Pattaya) or online (youtube)
- The O-X does **not** reduce reporting frequency — despite the 10-year visa, the quarterly reporting obligation remains identical to O-A holders (youtube)
- Many O-X holders use this as a comparison point when evaluating LTR-P, which offers annual reporting (youtube+1)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required** if leaving Thailand during the 5-year stay period:

- Single re-entry permit: 1,000 THB
- Multiple re-entry permit: 3,800 THB

**This is another contrast with the LTR Wealthy Pensioner**, which requires no re-entry permits. Frequent travellers on the O-X should purchase a multiple re-entry permit at Jomtien before every overseas trip.

## Common pitfalls

1. **Nationality exclusion shock:** Retirees from countries NOT on the 14-country list — including Ireland, New Zealand, South Africa, Russia, Ukraine, the Philippines, Israel, and most Asian and African nations — are completely ineligible for the O-X regardless of wealth. **This is a hard exclusion with no workarounds.** Non-eligible nationals must use O-A, LTR, or Privilege. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
2. **Conflating the 3,000,000 THB "deposit" with permanent loss of funds:** The Thai bank deposit requirement means parking 3M THB in a Thai account — the money remains yours and earns interest. However, it must stay above 3,000,000 THB throughout the visa period. Spending below that threshold violates the conditions and puts the visa at risk at the 5-year extension stage. (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)
3. **Health insurance policy coverage mismatch:** Multi-year insurance policies for the O-X must meet a higher coverage threshold than the standard O-A. Applying with an O-A-level policy (40,000/400,000 THB) when the O-X requires higher-tier coverage **[verify: 120,000/3,000,000 THB]** causes immediate rejection at the embassy. **[verify]**
4. **Assuming the O-X eliminates all immigration visits:** The O-X eliminates the need for annual renewal extensions during the 5-year period — but **90-day TM47 reporting still requires quarterly action** (online or in person at Jomtien). Some O-X holders discover this only after arriving in Thailand. (youtube)
5. **Most retirees still choose O-A despite O-X availability:** In practice, the vast majority of eligible retirees choose the Non-O-A over the O-X because:
   - O-A requires only 800,000 THB vs O-X's 3,000,000 THB
   - O-A is available to all nationalities; O-X is limited to 14
   - O-A annual visits to Jomtien are familiar and manageable
   - The 5-year O-X benefit is real but not compelling enough to justify locking up 3M THB
   - **The LTR-P is now a better 10-year option for those who qualify** (youtube+1)
6. **O-X vs LTR-P misunderstanding:** For eligible retirees with USD 80,000/year income (the LTR-P threshold), the LTR Wealthy Pensioner is objectively superior: 10-year visa, annual reporting only, no re-entry permit needed, foreign income tax exemption. **The O-X's advantage over LTR-P is that it does not require income proof — only a bank deposit — making it suitable for retirees with capital but not regular declared income.** (siam-legal, youtube)
7. **Second 5-year extension catching people off guard:** The initial O-X visa is issued at the embassy. The second 5-year extension must be applied for at Thai Immigration — Jomtien for Pattaya — with updated financial and insurance documentation. **Some holders believe the 10-year visa is unconditional; it is not.** The second 5 years are conditional on still meeting the criteria. (youtube, ottawa.thaiembassy)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**Jomtien for 5-year extension:** The O-X's 5-year extension is handled at Jomtien Immigration. Unlike the annual O-A renewal, the O-X extension appointment happens only once in 10 years — but it requires a full set of updated financial documents (Thai bank certificate at 3M THB threshold) and updated health insurance, so thorough preparation is essential. (ottawa.thaiembassy)

**Pattaya demographics:** O-X holders in Pattaya are typically high-net-worth retirees from Western Europe, the UK, the US, Canada, or Japan who have committed seriously to long-term Thailand residency. The 14-country eligibility list aligns well with Pattaya's largest expat nationalities (UK, US, Germany, Australia, France, Netherlands). (conciergepattaya)

**The Pattaya O-X vs LTR-P conversation:** For eligible wealthy retirees in Pattaya, the most frequent question is **O-X vs LTR-P**. Your comparison page (C-2 Elite vs LTR or C-3) should address this specifically: O-X suits capital-rich/income-uncertain retirees; LTR-P suits income-verified/tax-optimisation-focused retirees. (youtube+1)

**Lead routing (§15):** O-X applicants are high-net-worth individuals who need Thai bank account setup, insurance brokering, and legal document compilation. Refer to a Pattaya-based immigration law firm with experience in embassy submissions, plus a bank account liaison service (Bangkok Bank and Kasikorn are the most O-X-friendly banks for the 3M deposit certification) **[verify]**.

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. What countries can apply for the Non-O-X?**
Only 14: Japan, Australia, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom, Canada, and the United States. All other nationalities are ineligible regardless of financial capacity. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)

**2. What is the minimum deposit for the Non-O-X?**
Either 3,000,000 THB as a full Thai bank deposit, or a reduced deposit of 1,800,000 THB combined with qualifying annual income making up the difference. Both must be in or transferred through a Thai bank. (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)

**3. Do I need to visit Immigration every year on the O-X?**
No — the O-X eliminates annual renewal visits during the 5-year permission-to-stay period. However, **90-day TM47 address reports are still required quarterly.** At the 5-year mark, you must go to Jomtien Immigration for the second 5-year extension. (youtube)

**4. Can I work in Thailand on the O-X?**
Absolutely not. All forms of employment are strictly prohibited — this is the same as O-A and all retirement visas. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)

**5. Is the 3,000,000 THB deposit permanently locked?**
No — the money remains yours and typically earns Thai bank interest. However, it must stay above the 3,000,000 THB threshold throughout the visa period. Withdrawing below the threshold could jeopardise your extension. (conciergepattaya)

**6. Why don't more retirees use the O-X if it's 10 years?**
Three reasons: the 3M THB deposit requirement is 3.75× the O-A minimum; the 14-country nationality restriction excludes many retirees; and the LTR Wealthy Pensioner now offers a better 10-year option for income-verified retirees (with tax benefits and annual reporting vs O-X's 90-day reports). (youtube+1)

**7. How does the O-X compare to the LTR for wealthy retirees?**
**O-X:** capital-focused (3M THB deposit, no income proof), 14 countries only, 90-day reporting, re-entry permits needed, no tax benefits.
**LTR-P:** income-focused (USD 80k/year), open to all nationalities, annual reporting, no re-entry permits, foreign income tax exempt.
For retirees who qualify for LTR-P, **it is the stronger option.** (siam-legal, youtube+1)

**8. Can I apply for the O-X while in Thailand?**
No — the initial O-X must be applied for at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your home country or country of legal residence. It cannot be applied for from inside Thailand. (consular.mfa+1)

**9. What health insurance do I need for the O-X?**
Higher-tier insurance than O-A — the commonly cited O-X minimum is **120,000 THB outpatient / 3,000,000 THB inpatient** coverage **[verify at application embassy]**. This is significantly more comprehensive than the O-A's 40,000/400,000 THB, and must be maintained for the full visa period. **[verify]**

**10. What happens at the 5-year renewal?**
You must apply at Thai Immigration (Jomtien for Pattaya residents) for the second 5-year extension — bringing updated Thai bank certification (still at 3M THB+), updated health insurance, and supporting documents. **This is conditional, not automatic.** (youtube, ottawa.thaiembassy)

**11. After 10 years, what are my options?**
Apply for a brand new O-X visa, switch to Non-O-A (annual extension path), or — if you qualify — apply for the LTR Wealthy Pensioner. There is no "permanent residency" equivalent issued through the O-X. (youtube+1)

**12. Is the O-X available in Pattaya?**
It must be applied for at a Thai Embassy abroad, not in Pattaya. The 5-year extension is handled at Jomtien Immigration in Pattaya. The visa itself is well-suited to Pattaya-based retirees from the eligible 14 countries. (ottawa.thaiembassy+1)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **No structural changes to O-X core requirements:** The 3,000,000 THB deposit threshold, 14-country eligibility list, 5+5 structure, and visa fee of 10,000 THB have remained unchanged since the visa was introduced (consular.mfa+1)
- **LTR Wealthy Pensioner now established as a stronger alternative:** Since LTR-P became fully operational in 2022–2023 and gained real traction by 2025–2026, it has effectively syphoned the higher-end retirement market away from O-X — particularly for income-verified high-net-worth individuals (youtube+1)
- **Health insurance enforcement tightening generally:** Across all retirement visa types, insurance verification is more strictly enforced in 2025–2026 — presenting documentation that matches the specific coverage tier required is more important than ever **[verify with current embassy advice]**
- **TDAC replaces TM6:** O-X holders returning to Thailand after travel abroad now complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card before boarding (thailandinsiderguide)
- **O-X remains underused:** As of 2026, the O-X is used by a small fraction of Thailand retirees compared to O-A — the practical advantages are real but the higher financial bar limits uptake significantly (youtube)
