## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant ED visa is Thailand's student visa — issued to foreign nationals enrolling in government-recognised educational institutions for genuine study purposes. In 2026, the vast majority of ED visa holders are language students studying Thai, English, Japanese, or Chinese at Ministry of Education (MOE)-accredited language schools, though the visa also covers university degree programmes, vocational training, Muay Thai training at registered schools, cooking courses, and certified internships.

In Pattaya specifically, the ED visa is popular among people studying Thai language, Muay Thai, and English who want a legal, structured long-stay option. **Following a major 2025 crackdown in which nearly 10,000 student visas were revoked nationwide for abuse, this visa is under closer scrutiny than ever — it is only appropriate for those who intend to genuinely attend classes.** (abroadeduguides+2, youtube, huahintoday+1)

## Eligibility (full list)

- Valid passport with at least **6 months' remaining validity** (siam-legal+1)
- **Genuine enrolment** (or confirmed acceptance) at a Thailand Ministry of Education (MOE)-accredited school, language institute, university, or training organisation (siamvisapro+2)
- Payment of at least **50% of total course fees** before applying (some schools require 100% upfront) (siam-legal)
- Must be applying for one of the recognised ED visa activities:
  - Elementary to university-level academic study (washingtondc.thaiembassy)
  - Language courses (Thai, English, Japanese, Chinese, etc.) (abroadeduguides)
  - Muay Thai training at an MOE-registered school (asialifestylemagazine)
  - Vocational or technical training (washingtondc.thaiembassy)
  - Short courses organised by government agencies or international organisations (abroadeduguides)
  - Internship as a compulsory part of a registered academic programme (abroadeduguides)
- Some nationalities (UK, US, and others) must provide a **criminal background check** (FBI / ACPO equivalent) (abroadeduguides)
- No minimum age (parental consent required for under-18s) **[verify]**
- No income or bank balance requirement — though some schools require proof of funds to cover tuition and living expenses (siam-legal)
- **Employment of any kind is strictly prohibited** (siamvisapro+1)

## Required documents

**Core documents for all ED visa applications:**

1. Valid passport — minimum 6 months' validity, copy of biodata page and all relevant stamp/visa pages (siam-legal+1)
2. Completed Thai visa application form — via Thai e-Visa portal (online submission) or physical form at embassy (thaiembassy+1)
3. Two recent passport-sized photographs — 4×6 cm, white background (siam-legal+1)
4. **MOE approval letter** — issued by your enrolled institution after submission to Thailand's Ministry of Education; takes 2–4 weeks from enrolment (thailand.acclime+1)
5. Enrolment/acceptance letter from the school — on official letterhead, confirming your course, start date, and duration (siamvisapro+1)
6. Copy of school's registration certificate or MOE operating licence — provided by the school (abroadeduguides)
7. Proof of tuition payment — receipt showing minimum 50% paid (siam-legal)
8. TM30 — accommodation notification receipt (if applying in-country) (abroadeduguides)
9. Criminal background check — FBI (US), ACRO/DBS (UK), or equivalent — required for certain nationalities (abroadeduguides)

**For in-country extension (at Jomtien or Chaeng Watthana Immigration):**

1. TM.7 Extension of Stay form (thailand.acclime)
2. **School attendance record — minimum 70–80% attendance required** (varies by school) (geosthai+1)
3. Current student ID card (siam-legal)
4. Academic progress report or grade transcript (for university students) (thailand.acclime)
5. Renewal enrolment letter from the school confirming continued study (abroadeduguides)
6. Passport and copies (biodata, current visa, entry stamp, TM30) (abroadeduguides)

## Financial requirements (THB and USD, current 2026 figures)

There is **no official minimum bank balance** required for the ED visa itself — it is not a financial visa. However: (siam-legal+1)

- **Tuition fees** must be partially or fully paid before application — costs vary widely:
  - Thai language school (12 months): approximately **30,000–80,000 THB** **[verify current rates]**
  - Muay Thai ED package (12 months including visa support): approximately **120,000–135,000 THB** (muaythaivisathailand)
  - University degree programmes: highly variable
- Some embassies may ask for proof that you can support yourself financially during your stay — a bank statement showing 3–6 months of living expenses (~20,000–30,000 THB/month in Pattaya) is prudent to have ready **[verify]**
- The **extension fee at Immigration is 1,900 THB per extension** (abroadeduguides)

## Application process — step by step

### Route A: Applying in-country (most common for Pattaya / language school students)

1. Arrive in Thailand on a 60-day tourist visa or visa-exempt entry — enrol at your chosen MOE-accredited school within the first week to maximise processing time (geosthai+1)
2. Pay at least 50% of tuition fees; school issues enrolment letter (siam-legal)
3. School submits your documents to the Ministry of Education for approval — takes 2–4 weeks (abroadeduguides)
4. MOE issues official approval letter; school compiles full document package (abroadeduguides)
5. Submit application at the Immigration Bureau — in Bangkok at Chaeng Watthana; in Pattaya through a school representative or direct at Jomtien (conciergepattaya+1)
6. Attend the Immigration appointment — dress formally; bring all originals and copies (abroadeduguides)
7. Application processing: approximately **16 working days** in-country (abroadeduguides)
8. Receive Non-Immigrant ED visa stamp in passport (abroadeduguides)

### Route B: Applying at a Thai Embassy abroad

1. Contact your chosen MOE-accredited school; confirm enrolment and pay fees (abroadeduguides)
2. School initiates MOE approval process (2–4 weeks) (abroadeduguides)
3. Once MOE letter is ready, school sends full document package to you (abroadeduguides)
4. Apply at your Royal Thai Embassy or via Thai e-Visa portal (thaiembassy)
5. Processing: 1–3 working days at most embassies (longer if criminal background check required) (abroadeduguides)
6. Receive 90-day Non-Immigrant ED visa; travel to Thailand and begin studies (abroadeduguides)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

Both routes are valid. **In-country application is the most common path for language school students,** as most enrol after arriving in Thailand on a tourist entry. Embassy application abroad suits those who want the visa before travelling, or who have limited time remaining on a tourist entry. (conciergepattaya+2)

**Pattaya-specific:** Language schools and Muay Thai camps in Pattaya typically guide students through the in-country process, with school staff accompanying students to Jomtien Immigration Office for the initial application and extensions. (pattaya4all+1)

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial ED visa (embassy-issued):** 90 days from date of issue (pattaya4all+1)
- **Extension of stay:** 90 days per extension, at a fee of 1,900 THB per extension at Jomtien (pattaya4all+1)
- **Maximum total stay per enrolment:** Determined by the course length as approved by MOE:
  - English language courses: 12 months (geosthai)
  - Thai language courses: up to 14 months (geosthai)
  - Japanese language courses: up to 15 months (geosthai)
  - University degree programmes: duration of degree (washingtondc.thaiembassy)
- **Extensions are limited to 2 per calendar year** (per updated November 2025 rules) (geosthai)
- After completing a course, the student must leave Thailand, re-enrol in a new course, and restart the process — they cannot simply extend indefinitely without a new MOE-approved enrolment (geosthai)
- **Conversion:** ED visa can be converted to Non-B (if offered employment), Non-O, or other categories at Thai Immigration, though this is rarely straightforward **[verify]**

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — mandatory.** ED visa holders staying 90+ consecutive days must file TM47 reports every 90 days. Most schools include assistance with 90-day reporting as part of their student support service. Options: (pattaya4all+1)

- Online via immigration.go.th
- In person at Jomtien Immigration (Pattaya residents)
- Via the school's visa assistance service (most Pattaya language schools and Muay Thai camps assist with this)

Since ED visa extensions are also done every 90 days, many students combine their extension appointment with their 90-day report in a single Jomtien visit. (pattaya4all)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required.** Leaving Thailand without a re-entry permit cancels the active ED visa status. Purchase before any international travel:

- Single re-entry: 1,000 THB
- Multiple re-entry: 3,800 THB

**Important 2026 enforcement note:** ED visa holders returning from abroad are now being questioned by immigration officers about their course attendance — officers ask which school you attend, what you studied recently, and when your next class is. **If you cannot answer convincingly, you risk being denied re-entry.** (geosthai)

## Common pitfalls

1. **Fake schools and shell institutions — the most dangerous pitfall in 2026:** Thai Immigration revoked nearly **10,000 student visas in 2025** as part of a nationwide crackdown on schools that existed purely to issue ED visa letters with no actual education taking place. Students who paid a dodgy school and never attended a single class lost their visa status immediately — some were deported. **Only enrol at genuinely MOE-accredited institutions. Check the school's MOE registration before paying anything.** (youtube, huahintoday)
2. **Attendance below the minimum threshold:** Most schools require 70–80% attendance for visa extension applications. Immigration officers are increasingly verifying attendance records. Students who miss too many classes — even with valid reasons — face extension refusals. The school's attendance system is now cross-referenced with Immigration digital records at some offices. (thailawonline, youtube, geosthai+1)
3. **Immigration officers testing your language skills at extension:** This is now officially reported practice — officers ask students basic questions in the language they are supposedly studying. A "Thai language student" who cannot say a single Thai word after 3 months of "classes" will have their extension denied on the spot. (thailawonline)
4. **Running out of time on tourist entry before ED approval:** The in-country ED process takes 30–60 days. Students who arrive on a 30-day visa-exempt stamp (and no extension) frequently find their legal stay expires before the ED visa is approved. **Arrive on a 60-day tourist visa or extend your visa-exempt entry immediately — do not wait.** (geosthai+1)
5. **Returning from abroad without being able to discuss your course:** Border officers are stopping ED visa holders at entry and asking course-specific questions. If you cannot name your school, describe your classes, or say what you studied this week, you face denial of entry or, at minimum, being pulled aside for questioning. (geosthai)
6. **Assuming the ED visa allows business activity:** No commercial activity whatsoever is permitted on an ED visa — not freelancing, not running an online business, not selling on social media. Officers are cross-referencing social media presence with visa status as part of the 2025–2026 enforcement push. (youtube)
7. **Extension limit misunderstood:** The November 2025 rule change limits extensions to 2 per calendar year — meaning you cannot extend indefinitely within a single calendar year without re-enrolling in a new MOE-approved course. (geosthai)

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Can I do Muay Thai on an ED visa in Pattaya?**
Yes — but only at a Muay Thai camp that holds an official MOE school licence. Informal training at a regular gym cannot support an ED visa. The camp must issue an MOE-backed enrolment letter and attendance records. (asialifestylemagazine)

**2. Can I work remotely on an ED visa?**
No. All forms of employment — local or remote, for Thai or foreign employers — are prohibited. This includes freelancing and running an online business. The DTV is the correct visa for remote workers. (abroadeduguides)

**3. How often do I need to renew an ED visa extension?**
Every 90 days at Jomtien Immigration. Cost is 1,900 THB per extension. Most Pattaya schools assist with this. (pattaya4all)

**4. What happens if I fail to maintain 80% attendance?**
Your school will typically refuse to sign your extension paperwork. Without the school's support letter and attendance record, Jomtien Immigration will deny your extension. (geosthai+1)

**5. Can I study at multiple schools on one ED visa?**
No. The ED visa is tied to one specific enrolled institution. Switching schools requires a new MOE approval process and a new visa application. (abroadeduguides)

**6. Is there a limit on how many times I can renew?**
The maximum total stay is determined by your course length (12–15 months depending on the course). From November 2025, extensions are limited to 2 per calendar year. After completing a course, you must leave Thailand and re-enrol before starting a new cycle. (geosthai)

**7. How do I know if a school is genuinely MOE-accredited?**
Ask the school for its MOE registration certificate number. You can verify legitimacy directly with the Ministry of Education or ask your school to show the physical certificate. Avoid any school that cannot provide this immediately. (abroadeduguides)

**8. Can I switch from an ED visa to a different visa type?**
Yes — conversion is possible at Thai Immigration. The most common switch is to Non-B if you find employment. Consult an immigration lawyer before attempting a conversion. **[verify]**

**9. Do I need to leave Thailand when my course ends?**
Yes — unless you immediately re-enrol in a new MOE-approved course or switch visa types. There is no automatic grace period after your ED visa expires. (abroadeduguides)

**10. Will Immigration officers test me in the language I'm studying?**
Yes — this is confirmed practice in 2026 for extension appointments. Officers ask basic questions in the language of your stated course. After 3–6 months of classes, you should be able to answer simple questions. (thailawonline)

**11. Is the Muay Thai ED visa legitimate in 2026 despite the crackdowns?**
Yes — when done through a genuinely MOE-licensed school that provides real training, the Muay Thai ED visa is fully legal and widely used. The crackdowns targeted fake institutions, not legitimate training programmes. (youtube, asialifestylemagazine)

**12. Can I bring my family on an ED visa?**
No dependent visa is attached to the Non-Immigrant ED. Family members need their own visas. A spouse would likely need a Non-O (dependent on spouse's Non-B, if working) or their own qualifying visa. **[verify]**

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **Mass revocation of ~10,000 student visas (2025):** The largest crackdown on ED visa abuse in Thailand's history. Immigration and education officials cross-referenced school attendance data with visa records, revoking all ED visas where students were not genuinely studying. Schools that failed to comply with reporting requirements lost their right to sponsor visas entirely. (huahintoday, youtube)
- **Digital attendance monitoring introduced:** A new joint Immigration/Ministry of Education digital system now cross-references student attendance records with visa status in real time. Schools that don't send reports flag their students for investigation. (youtube)
- **Extension cap (November 2025):** 2 extensions per calendar year maximum — previously students could extend indefinitely within a single year on rolling 90-day increments. (geosthai)
- **Border re-entry questioning of ED visa holders:** Immigration officers now routinely ask returning ED visa holders course-specific questions at the border. (geosthai)
- **Visa exemption reduction proposals:** A proposal to cut visa-free stays from 60 to 30 days would directly impact the standard "arrive visa-free, enrol, convert to ED" pathway — if enacted, students would need to arrive on a pre-arranged tourist visa. Status: under review as of April 2026. (geosthai)
- **TDAC replaces TM6:** Digital arrival card now required pre-travel for all arrivals, including ED visa holders. (thailawonline)
- **Attendance scrutiny at extensions:** Officers now check attendance records at every 90-day extension, not just the initial application. (thailawonline+1)
