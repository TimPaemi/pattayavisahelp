## At a glance

- **Visa-exempt entry:** 60 days for SE/NO/FI/DK passport holders
- **Nordics in Pattaya:** Substantial Swedish community especially; smaller Finnish, Norwegian, Danish presences
- **Most popular long-stay visa:** Non-O retirement, DTV, LTR
- **Nordic-specific advantage:** All four eligible for Non-O-X (10-year) — among 14 eligible nationalities

## Nordic-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance

- **Sweden:** Belastningsregisterutdrag from Polismyndigheten
- **Norway:** Politiattest from Politiet
- **Finland:** Rikosrekisteriote from Oikeusrekisterikeskus
- **Denmark:** Straffeattest from Politiet
- All available online; cost ~€10–€20; processed in 1–2 weeks

### Document legalisation

All four are Hague Apostille Convention members:

1. **Apostille** from country's designated authority
2. **Royal Thai Embassy** in country's capital certification (or via Bangkok)
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya
4. **Thai MFA legalisation** in Bangkok
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Nordic chain:** 5–8 weeks.

## Nordic tax considerations

### Sweden

- **Skatteverket** complex residency rules; living abroad >5 years typically establishes non-residency
- **Swedish pension** (allmän pension): taxable in Thailand if remitted (Thai tax resident); Swedish tax credit may apply
- Sweden-Thailand DTA exists

### Norway

- **Norwegian tax residency** ends after 4 years abroad with proper documentation
- **Norwegian pension** (Folketrygd / NAV): portable to Thailand; taxable in Thailand if remitted
- Norway-Thailand DTA exists

### Finland

- **Finnish tax residency** ends after 6 years abroad
- **Finnish pension** (Kela): portable; taxable in Thailand if remitted
- Finland-Thailand DTA exists

### Denmark

- **Danish tax residency** complex; typically requires formal end of Danish residency
- **Danish pension** (Folkepension, ATP): portable; tax treatment varies
- Denmark-Thailand DTA exists

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Can Nordic citizens get the Non-O-X 10-year visa?**
**Yes — all four nationalities (SE, NO, FI, DK)** are on the 14-country eligibility list.

**2. What about my home-country pension?**
All four Nordic countries have DTAs with Thailand. Pensions are typically taxable in Thailand if remitted (and you're Thai tax resident); home-country tax credits available.

**3. Can I keep my home-country health insurance?**
**Public health systems** typically cease covering you when you move abroad permanently. Some private supplementary insurance maintains some coverage. Most Nordic retirees take Thai or international expat insurance.

**4. Where do Nordics live in Pattaya?**
Largely Jomtien (Swedish concentration), with Pratumnak and central Pattaya communities. Specific buildings have heavy Nordic presence.

**5. Driving licence conversion?**
All Nordic licences convertible to Thai (Hague members). Translation needed (~1,500 THB).

**6. Currency and transfer?**
SEK/NOK/DKK/EUR exchange typically via Wise or SWIFT. Routine transfers via Wise; large transfers (retirement seasoning) via SWIFT for FET.

**7. Tax residency formalities?**
Each Nordic country has specific rules for ending residency. Don't assume "I'm in Thailand 6 months" automatically ends home tax obligations. Consult home tax advisor before relocating.

**8. Are there Nordic-language services in Pattaya?**
Limited but growing — some Swedish-speaking medical staff at private hospitals; Swedish-speaking real estate agents; Nordic-speaking visa agents. Less infrastructure than UK/German communities.
