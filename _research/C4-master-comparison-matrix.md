## Decision tree by primary intent

### "I just want to visit Thailand for tourism (under 90 days)"

→ **Visa Exemption** (60 days + 30 ext) for eligible nationalities. Or **Tourist Visa (TR)** if your nationality requires one.

### "I want to live in Thailand long-term"

**By age:**

- **Under 50:** DTV (foreign income), LTR-T/H (qualifying employer), SMART (BOI sectors), ED (language/Muay Thai), Non-B (salaried work), Marriage (Thai spouse), Privilege (cost-no-object)
- **50+:** Non-O retirement (cheapest), Non-O-A (if insurance affordable), Non-O-X (if 14 nationalities + 3M THB), LTR-P (if income $80k+), Privilege (cost-no-object)

**By income/assets:**

- **<$80k/year, <800k THB:** ED visa, DTV (if foreign income), or Marriage if eligible
- **800k THB available, 50+:** Non-O retirement
- **$80k+/year, 180+ days/year stay:** LTR (tax benefits)
- **No income proof, willing to pay 650k+ THB upfront:** Privilege

**By work intent:**

- **No work needed:** Retirement, Marriage, DTV, Privilege, ED
- **Foreign-source remote work:** DTV, LTR-P, LTR-T
- **Local employment:** Non-B + work permit, LTR-T/H, SMART
- **Running Thai company:** Non-B + Thai company structure (or BOI promotion)
- **Investing in Thai company:** SMART-I

**By lifestyle:**

- **Frequent international travel:** Multi-entry visas (DTV, METV, LTR, SMART, Privilege) — avoid re-entry permit hassle
- **Continuous Thailand stay:** Privilege, LTR (no exit requirements)
- **Pattaya base + EEC work:** LTR-H or SMART (BOI industries)

### "I'm married to a Thai national"

→ **Non-O Marriage** is the cheapest (400k THB seasoning vs 800k for retirement). Provides work permit eligibility.

### "I'm a foreign correspondent / media professional"

→ **Non-M Media** if your media entity is registered 6+ months. Otherwise consider DTV for "talent" route.

## Hidden trade-off: 90-day reporting and re-entry permits

Visas that **require 90-day reporting + re-entry permits** add real annual administrative friction:

- 4 × TM47 reports/year + every-trip re-entry permit purchase
- ~6,000–15,000 THB/year in agent fees if outsourcing
- Several Jomtien visits/year

Visas that **eliminate this** (LTR, SMART, Privilege, DTV, METV) save substantial time and money over a multi-year stay. **For frequent travellers, this is a real factor in the cost-benefit comparison.**

## Strategic insights from the matrix

### The "no-brainer" picks

- **Pattaya retiree, 50+, 800k THB, healthy:** Non-O retirement (in-country)
- **Remote worker, foreign income $40–80k:** DTV
- **Senior pro at Fortune 500, $150k+/year:** LTR-T
- **Wealthy retiree, $100k+/year, will spend 9+ months/year in Thailand:** LTR-P
- **Foreign-Thai married couple:** Non-O Marriage

### The "hard cases"

- **Over-70 retiree with pre-existing condition:** Non-O in-country (no insurance) > O-A (insurance) and probably > Privilege (cheaper insurance threshold)
- **DTV-eligible but needs Thai bank account:** Privilege or DTV with workarounds (see G6)
- **Mid-income remote worker ($40–80k):** DTV is the default; LTR-T fails the employer revenue test for most

### The "wrong picks" people often make

- **Choosing Privilege when LTR-P would qualify and save $250k+ in tax over 10 years**
- **Choosing O-A and locking into expensive insurance when Non-O in-country is identical end-state**
- **Choosing SMART when LTR is broader (LTR has tax benefits SMART doesn't)**
- **Trying to live on rolling visa-exempt entries** — no longer viable in 2026 (see G4)
