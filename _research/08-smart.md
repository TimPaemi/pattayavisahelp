## Who it's for

Thailand's SMART Visa is a **BOI-managed programme** designed to attract highly-skilled professionals, investors, executives, and startup founders in Thailand's targeted industries, along with their families. It offers up to 4 years of stay, **exemption from standard work permit requirements**, and simplified immigration reporting — positioning it as a specialised alternative to the Non-B + work permit route for people working in BOI-targeted sectors.

The SMART-O category covers spouses and children of SMART-T/I/E/S holders, **with spouses allowed to work without a separate work permit in many cases.** (siam-legal+4)

## Eligibility (full list)

SMART Visa has five categories:

- **SMART-T (Talent)** — highly-skilled technical professionals
- **SMART-I (Investor)** — investors in targeted industries
- **SMART-E (Executive)** — senior management in targeted industries
- **SMART-S (Startup)** — startup founders/entrepreneurs
- **SMART-O (Other)** — spouse and dependents of SMART-T/I/E/S holders

Below are the core 2026 eligibility criteria (summarised — always cross-check on smart-visa.boi.go.th before publishing).

### SMART-T (Talent)

- Employment in one of Thailand's targeted industries (same as LTR-H list: next-gen automotive, smart electronics, digital, medical hub, etc.) (emerhub+1)
- Highly-skilled worker or field-specific expert — typically a bachelor's degree or higher in science/technology-related field and relevant work experience (thailand.acclime+1)
- **Minimum salary:**
  - Common thresholds reported: **THB 100,000–200,000/month** depending on degree and role (siam-legal+1)
- Employment contract with a company in a targeted industry (Thai or foreign BOI-promoted entity) (emerhub)
- Health insurance covering full stay in Thailand (thailand.acclime+1)

### SMART-I (Investor)

- **Minimum investment of THB 20 million** in one or more technology-based companies in targeted industries, or in venture capital funds that invest in those industries (thailand.acclime)
- Investment must be maintained throughout visa validity (thailand.acclime)
- Health insurance covering stay (thailand.acclime)

### SMART-E (Executive)

- Senior management position (e.g. CEO, MD, Director) in a company operating in a targeted industry (thailand.acclime)
- Minimum requirements often cited:
  - Bachelor's degree or higher
  - **10 years' work experience** in the relevant field
  - **Minimum monthly salary of THB 200,000** (thailand.acclime)
- Fixed deposit of THB 600,000 with maturity of at least 1 year and valid health insurance, or equivalent financial security (thailand.acclime)
- Employment contract with at least 1-year remaining term (thailand.acclime)

### SMART-S (Startup)

Two main durations:

**6-month SMART-S:** for people exploring a startup idea
- Participation in endorsed incubation/accelerator/startup camp programmes organised by NIA or BOI (siam-legal+1)
- Health insurance covering entire stay (siam-legal)

**2-year SMART-S:** for founders actually incorporating in Thailand
- **Fixed deposit of THB 600,000** (1+ year term) (thailand.acclime)
- Must set up a company in Thailand within 1 year with **at least 25% ownership or board membership** in a targeted-industry company (smart-visa.boi+1)
- Health insurance (thailand.acclime)

### SMART-O (Spouse and Dependents)

- Legal spouse or child of a SMART-T/I/E/S holder (thailand-e-visas+1)
- Valid passports and relationship documentation (marriage certificate, birth certificate, etc.) (tteo.thaiembassy+1)
- **Spouses granted permission to work in Thailand without a separate work permit** for most SMART categories (conciergepattaya+1)

## Required documents

**Universal documents (all SMART categories):**

1. Valid passport with at least 2–3 blank pages and 6+ months' validity (thailand-e-visas+1)
2. Completed SMART Visa online application form (via BOI portal) (belaws+1)
3. Passport-sized photo (white background) (siam-legal)
4. Health insurance covering the full intended stay (siam-legal+1)
5. **Letter of Qualification Endorsement** issued by BOI / SMART Visa unit once your credentials are approved (tteo.thaiembassy+1)

**SMART-T (Talent):**

1. Employment contract or offer letter from a company in a targeted industry (emerhub+1)
2. Evidence of qualifications — degree certificates, professional certifications (emerhub)
3. Proof of salary meeting the minimum threshold (payslips, employment letter) (siam-legal)
4. Evidence that the employer operates in a targeted industry (BOI promotion letter, business profile, MOI classification, etc.) (emerhub)

**SMART-I (Investor):**

1. Evidence of investment — share purchase agreements, bank transfer slips, company share register showing at least THB 20M investment in tech-based company in targeted industry (thailand.acclime)
2. Documentation confirming target company's status as tech-based and in targeted sector (BOI promotion, investment approval) (thailand.acclime)

**SMART-E (Executive):**

1. Employment contract in executive role (CEO, director, etc.) with company in targeted industry (thailand.acclime)
2. CV and reference letters demonstrating at least 10 years' relevant experience (thailand.acclime)
3. Degree certificate (minimum bachelor's) (thailand.acclime)
4. Proof of salary (≥ THB 200,000/month) (thailand.acclime)
5. Evidence of fixed deposit (THB 600,000) and health insurance (thailand.acclime)

**SMART-S (Startup):**

1. Proof of participation in endorsed incubation/accelerator/startup programmes (for 6-month SMART-S) (smart-visa.boi+1)
2. Evidence of business plan/technology-based startup concept (smart-visa.boi)
3. Bank certificate for THB 600,000 deposit (for longer-term SMART-S) (thailand.acclime)
4. Incorporation documents (when company set up), share register showing ≥25% ownership or board seat (smart-visa.boi+1)

**SMART-O (Other):**

1. Marriage certificate, birth certificates for dependents (thailand-e-visas)
2. Passport copies of primary SMART holder (tteo.thaiembassy+1)

## Application process — step by step

### Stage 1 — Qualification Endorsement (BOI)

1. Create an account at SMART Visa portal: https://smart-visa.boi.go.th (lexnovapartners+1)
2. Choose SMART category (T, I, E, S, O) and complete online qualification application (belaws)
3. Upload PDF copies of all required documents for your category (lexnovapartners+1)
4. Accept qualification endorsement criteria (online acknowledgement) (belaws)
5. BOI and relevant agencies (e.g. NIA, NSTDA) review your qualifications — typical timeframe up to 30 business days (thac.or+1)
6. If approved, you receive **Letter of Qualification Endorsement** (tteo.thaiembassy+1)

### Stage 2 — Visa Issuance (Immigration / Embassy)

1. With the endorsement letter, apply for SMART Visa at:
   - Thai Embassy/Consulate in your home country, or
   - Immigration Bureau One Stop Service Center (OSS) at Chamchuri Square, Bangkok, or
   - **EEC Labour Administration Center in Chonburi** (for EEC-based applicants) (thac.or+1)
2. Submit:
   - Endorsement letter
   - Passport
   - TM6 Arrival card (if already in Thailand)
   - Completed SMART Visa application form
   - STM.6 criteria acknowledgement form (siam-legal)
3. SMART visa issued — validity up to 4 years depending on contract or investment (thailand-e-visas+1)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Qualification endorsement:**

- Always via BOI online portal at smart-visa.boi.go.th — can be done from abroad or while in Thailand. (lexnovapartners+1)

**Visa issuance (after endorsement):**

- **Abroad:** Thai Embassy/Consulate – must apply within 60 days of endorsement issuance (thac.or+1)
- **In Thailand:**
  - **OSS (One Stop Service Center for Visa and Work Permits)** — Immigration Bureau office at Chamchuri Square, Bangkok (belaws+1)
  - **EEC Labour Administration Center (Chonburi)** — for SMART holders working in the Eastern Economic Corridor (includes Pattaya/Chonburi region) (thac.or+1)

Pattaya-based SMART applicants will usually use the EEC centre in Chonburi or travel to Bangkok OSS, depending on employer advice and BOI instructions. (thac.or)

## Realistic processing time

| Stage | Timeline |
|---|---|
| BOI qualification endorsement review | ≈30 business days (around 6 weeks) (thac.or+1) |
| Embassy/OSS visa issuance after endorsement | 3–10 business days (varies by post & workload) (thailand-e-visas) |
| Total (clean application) | 4–8 weeks (thailand-e-visas) |
| Total (with document clarification) | Up to 12 weeks |

As with LTR, delays are typically caused by missing documents or unclear proof that the employer or startup is in a targeted industry.

## Duration & extensions

- **SMART-T, SMART-I, SMART-S (invested stage):** Validity of up to 4 years — typically tied to employment contract or business/investment plan duration (thailand-e-visas+1)
- **SMART-E:** Some sources note up to 2 years; others up to 4 years depending on contract; official BOI materials clarify the period equals contract length but not more than 4 years (thailand-e-visas+1)
- **SMART-S (initial exploratory 6-month visa):** 6 months, renewable into longer-term SMART-S once company set-up conditions are met (smart-visa.boi+1)
- **All SMART categories are multiple entry** — no separate re-entry permit necessary (thailand-e-visas+2)
- **Extensions are possible** as long as criteria (salary, investment, sector) remain satisfied and BOI continues to endorse the status (thac.or+1)

## 90-day reporting applicability

SMART Visa holders benefit from a reduction in reporting frequency similar to LTR:

- **Reporting to Immigration is required once per year, instead of every 90 days.** (conciergepattaya+2)
- Reporting can be done at OSS (Bangkok) or the EEC Labour Administration Center (Chonburi). (thac.or)
- This annual reporting applies both to primary SMART holders and their SMART-O dependents. (conciergepattaya)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**No re-entry permit required.** SMART visas are explicitly defined as multiple-entry visas:

- Holders can travel in and out of Thailand freely during their SMART visa validity. (thailand-e-visas+2)
- No single or multiple re-entry permits (1,000 / 3,800 THB) are needed, unlike Non-B, O-A, ED, and Marriage visas.

## Common pitfalls

1. **Employer/industry not truly "targeted":** Many applicants assume any tech or digital company qualifies. The company must operate in BOI-defined targeted industries and in many cases be BOI-promoted or endorsed by relevant agencies. Applying with a general services company or small marketing agency is a common rejection scenario. (emerhub+1)
2. **Salary thresholds misinterpreted:** Prospective SMART-T or SMART-E applicants sometimes count equity or bonuses toward the monthly salary threshold. **BOI and Immigration typically assess fixed monthly salary only.** Falling even a few thousand baht under the threshold disqualifies the application. (siam-legal+1)
3. **Startup SMART-S never progresses:** Some founders obtain the 6-month SMART-S based on an incubator programme, then fail to set up the company or invest the required 600,000 THB within the specified timeframe. The visa is not extended, leaving them needing to exit or switch visa type. (smart-visa.boi+1)
4. **Insufficient documentation of expertise:** For SMART-T and SMART-E, the "expert" or "executive" claim must be backed by clear evidence — degrees, certifications, 10-year employment history. **A senior-sounding title alone is not enough**; missing documentation leads to rejection. (lexnovapartners+1)
5. **Confusion between SMART and LTR programmes:** Some candidates better suited to LTR (e.g. wealthy remote professionals with high foreign income but no targeted-industry employer) apply for SMART-T or SMART-E and are rejected because their employer doesn't meet the sector/scale requirements. **They should have applied for LTR-T instead.** (mbmg-group+1)
6. **Assuming SMART allows any work:** SMART-T/E holders can work without a traditional work permit only in the endorsed company and role. Work outside the endorsed scope, such as side consulting or freelance gigs, may still require additional permission or a different visa. (siam-legal+1)
7. **Family member employment assumptions:** While SMART-O spouses can work without a separate permit, they typically still need BOI endorsement and proper registration. Assuming an automatic right to work in any job without paperwork can cause compliance issues. (conciergepattaya+1)

## Pattaya/EEC-specific considerations

**EEC Labour Administration Center (Chonburi):** SMART Visa holders working in Chonburi (including Pattaya), Rayong, or Chachoengsao can use the EEC Labour Administration Center instead of Bangkok OSS for visa issuance, extensions, and reporting. **This is critical for the Pattaya/EEC positioning of your site.** (tteo.thaiembassy+1)

The EEC centre handles:

- SMART and LTR visa issuance after BOI endorsement
- Annual reporting
- Associated family visas (SMART-O) (thac.or)

Many EEC employers (automotive, electronics, logistics, petrochemicals) around Laem Chabang and Eastern Seaboard Industrial Estate are ideal SMART-T/H employers.

**Pattaya angle:**

- SMART professionals and executives typically live in Pattaya or Eastern Seaboard suburbs and commute to EEC industrial zones.
- Pattaya is attractive for SMART-S startup founders in digital, tourism-tech, and wellness sectors — but they must ensure their startup concept fits an official targeted industry and is properly endorsed by NIA/BOI. (conciergepattaya+1)
- Local visa agents in Pattaya can help compile documentation but cannot change BOI's strict sector and salary criteria — your copy should emphasise this reality to manage expectations. (conciergepattaya)

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. How is the SMART Visa different from the LTR Visa?**
SMART is focused on targeted industries and specific roles (talent, investor, executive, startup) and requires sector-based endorsements. LTR is broader, focused on income/asset thresholds and tax benefits, with simpler employer criteria but no targeted-industry requirement. SMART is best for people directly contributing to BOI sectors; LTR is best for affluent long-stayers prioritising residency and tax optimisation. (siam-legal+2)

**2. Do SMART Visa holders need a work permit?**
No traditional work permit book is required. SMART visas grant the right to work for the endorsed employer/investment/startup without a separate work permit application, simplifying administration. (siam-legal+1)

**3. Can my spouse work in Thailand on SMART-O?**
Yes — SMART-O spouses of SMART-T/I/E/S holders are granted the right to work in Thailand without a separate work permit, as long as they comply with relevant labour regulations. They still need appropriate tax registration and employer documentation. (conciergepattaya+1)

**4. How long can I stay on a SMART Visa?**
Up to 4 years per grant for SMART-T/I/S and typically up to 2–4 years for SMART-E depending on contract length, with multiple entries allowed and annual reporting only. (thailand-e-visas+1)

**5. Is there a minimum investment for SMART-S startup founders?**
For the extended SMART-S (up to 2 years), you must maintain THB 600,000 fixed deposit and set up a company with at least 25% ownership or board membership in a targeted-industry company within 1 year. The initial 6-month SMART-S based purely on incubator participation does not set a fixed THB investment but requires programme endorsement. (smart-visa.boi+2)

**6. Can I switch from SMART Visa to another visa type later?**
Yes — conversion to other visas (Non-B, LTR, etc.) is possible in principle but must be processed via Immigration/BOI. Planning the long-term path in advance is important; switching may require restarting qualification from scratch. (thac.or)

**7. Does SMART give any tax advantages?**
Some sources mention potential tax incentives for SMART holders, but the clear tax advantages are stronger under the LTR programme (e.g. 17% flat rate for LTR-H, foreign income exemption for LTR-W/P/T). SMART holders should not assume automatic tax benefits; always consult a Thai tax advisor. (hlbthai+1)

**8. Can a small startup with no revenue sponsor a SMART-T or SMART-E?**
No, not realistically. For SMART-T/E, the employer must be in a targeted industry and typically show scale and credibility. For early-stage startups, SMART-S is the intended route; once the company grows and is BOI-endorsed, it may qualify for SMART-T/E hires. (emerhub+1)

**9. How long does it take to get a SMART Visa?**
Around 4–8 weeks end-to-end: about 30 business days for BOI qualification endorsement plus another 3–10 working days for visa issuance at an embassy or OSS/EEC. Complex cases or incomplete documentation can push this to 12 weeks. (belaws+2)

**10. Is SMART Visa available for remote workers whose employer is not in a targeted industry?**
Generally no. If your foreign employer is not in a BOI-targeted sector, SMART-T/E will likely be rejected. **Those remote workers should consider the DTV or LTR-T (Work-from-Thailand Professional) instead.** (mbmg-group+1)

**11. Can I live in Pattaya and hold a SMART Visa while working in EEC?**
Yes — this is common. You can live in Pattaya (Jomtien, Pratumnak, etc.) and commute to Chonburi industrial estates. Your SMART Visa processing can be handled via the EEC Labour Administration Center in Chonburi or the Bangkok OSS, depending on BOI guidance. (conciergepattaya+1)

**12. Is the SMART Visa still actively used in 2026 or is LTR replacing it?**
The SMART Visa remains active and is complementary to LTR. SMART is tailored to BOI-targeted sectors and company-specific roles; LTR is broader and more individual-focused. Many EEC and tech-sector hires still use SMART, while high-income individuals without targeted-industry ties gravitate to LTR. (juslaws+1)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **Operational consolidation with LTR/EEC services:** SMART visa processing is now more tightly integrated with BOI's One Stop Service Center and EEC Labour Administration Center, making it easier for EEC-based professionals to handle everything locally instead of always going to Bangkok. (thac.or)
- **Clarifications on SMART-S pathways:** 2025–2026 guidance clarified the distinction between the 6-month exploratory SMART-S and the 2-year company-based SMART-S, tightening enforcement on the requirement to set up a company within 1 year for the longer stay. (smart-visa.boi+1)
- **Increased scrutiny on targeted-sector claims:** As BOI and Immigration step up enforcement of sector criteria for both SMART and LTR-H, companies attempting to "game" the targeted-industry definition face more rejections; documentation for sector eligibility is now more thoroughly checked. (naraipartners+1)
- **Digital application improvements:** The smart-visa.boi.go.th portal is now more stable and user-friendly than early iterations, reducing form errors and upload failures reported in 2019–2021. (lexnovapartners+1)
- **No structural changes to core thresholds reported for 2026** — salary, investment, and deposit requirements remain broadly in line with earlier years, but BOI has become more rigid about documentary proof. (thailand.acclime)
