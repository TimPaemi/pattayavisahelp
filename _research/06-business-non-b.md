## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant B (Non-B) visa is Thailand's standard work visa — the mandatory first step for any foreigner who intends to be employed, conduct business operations, or earn income within Thailand. It covers salaried employees hired by Thai companies, foreign company representatives, business owners, directors, teachers, and professionals across virtually all sectors.

**Without a valid Non-B visa, no work permit can legally be issued — and without a work permit, no foreigner may legally work in Thailand for any employer, whether paid or unpaid.** The Non-B is also used for business visit purposes (attending meetings, negotiations, or conducting market research) without the intent to take up employment — in which case no work permit is required. (thailand.acclime+3)

## Eligibility (full list)

- Valid passport with at least **6 months' remaining validity** and two blank pages (legalfirsthuahin+1)
- A confirmed job offer or employment contract from a Thai company or a verifiable business purpose in Thailand (tochka.co+1)
- The sponsoring Thai company must meet eligibility requirements (see Financial Requirements — employer side) (rlcoutsourcing+1)
- The applicant's intended occupation must not be on Thailand's list of **39 reserved occupations** restricted to Thai nationals (thailawonline+1)
- For work permit eligibility: must hold a relevant educational degree or work experience certificate matching the role (thaiembassy+1)
- Proof of adequate personal finances: **20,000 THB per individual or 40,000 THB per family** (mfa+2)
- Applicants must generally apply from outside Thailand or from their country of legal residence — applying from inside Thailand on a tourist visa is possible via conversion but heavily restricted at most offices (issacompass+1)
- No criminal record in Thailand (verification varies by embassy) (issacompass)
- Medical certificate — required by some embassies and for work permit issuance (thaiembassy+1)

## Application process — step by step

The standard process has 6 steps and takes **4–8 weeks total:**

1. **Employer obtains WP.32 pre-approval letter** from the Thai Department of Employment — this confirms that the specific foreign position is approved before the employee even applies for the visa. Most embassies require this before issuing a Non-B visa. Processing: 7–15 working days (thailandlawonline+1)
2. **Employee applies for Non-B visa** at a Royal Thai Embassy in their home country (or country of legal residence) — submitting the WP.32, employment contract, and all personal documents via the Thai e-Visa portal or in person (thailandlawoffice+2)
3. **Non-B visa issued** — standard single-entry, 90-day validity stamp. Some embassies issue 1-year multiple-entry for established positions. (hochiminh.thaiembassy+1)
4. **Employee enters Thailand on Non-B visa** — receives 90-day permission-to-stay stamp on arrival (thailandlawoffice)
5. **Apply for work permit within 15 days of arrival** — employer and employee submit combined document set to the Department of Employment (or BOI One-Stop if BOI-promoted). Work permit processing: 7–10 working days in Bangkok; up to 2 months in Phuket (thaiembassy+1)
6. **Apply for 1-year extension of stay at Immigration** — once work permit is in hand, apply at Jomtien Immigration (Pattaya) or Chaeng Watthana (Bangkok) for 1-year extension tied to the work permit (thailandlawoffice+1)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Non-B visa initial application:** Must be done at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate — typically in the applicant's home country or country of legal residence. Most embassies now accept applications via the Thai e-Visa portal. (issacompass+1)

**Work permit:** Applied for in Thailand at the Department of Employment (กรมการจัดหางาน) — Ministry of Labour offices. **For BOI-promoted companies:** at the One Start One Stop Investment Center (OSOS), 18th Floor, Chamchuri Square, Bangkok — significantly faster processing. (boi+1)

**1-year extension of stay (Pattaya):** At Jomtien Immigration Office for Chonburi-registered businesses. Jurisdiction is by company registration address. (pattaya4all)

**BOI One-Stop (OSOS):** Available only to BOI-promoted companies. Provides visa extensions, work permits, and re-entry permits in a single visit — eliminates multi-agency queuing. (osos.boi+1)

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial Non-B visa:** 90-day single-entry (most common first issue) (hochiminh.thaiembassy)
- **Permission to stay on arrival:** 90 days (hochiminh.thaiembassy+1)
- **Work permit validity:** 1 year, tied to the employment contract and company status (rlcoutsourcing)
- **1-year extension of stay:** Renewable annually, as long as employment and work permit remain valid (rlcoutsourcing+1)
- **Work permit and visa must be renewed in tandem** — letting one expire while the other is active causes immediate compliance failure (rlcoutsourcing)
- **Company change:** If you change employers, both the work permit and Non-B visa status must be updated — you cannot carry a work permit from one employer to a new one without reapplication (rlcoutsourcing)
- **BOI multi-year visa:** BOI-promoted company employees can receive multi-year visa and work permit packages, significantly reducing annual renewal burden (boi)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — mandatory.** All Non-B visa extension holders must file TM47 address reports every 90 days. Options:

- Online via immigration.go.th
- In person at Jomtien Immigration (for Chonburi-based employees)
- Via OSOS (for BOI-promoted company employees)

**BOI exception:** BOI-promoted company foreign employees only need to notify Immigration of their address **once per year** instead of every 90 days — one of the significant practical benefits of BOI promotion for foreign staff. (boi)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required.** Exiting Thailand while a 1-year extension is active without a re-entry permit cancels the extension immediately:

- Single re-entry: 1,000 THB
- Multiple re-entry: 3,800 THB

**BOI exception:** Employees at BOI-promoted companies are **exempt from re-entry permit requirements** — unlimited exits and re-entries without purchasing permits. Another major advantage of BOI promotion. (boi)

## Common pitfalls

1. **The 4:1 ratio trap for small businesses:** A foreign entrepreneur sets up a small Thai company and wants to give themselves a work permit. The company must employ 4 Thai nationals with social security contributions before even 1 foreign work permit is eligible. A sole-operator or 1–2 person business often cannot meet this — and shell "employees" to satisfy the ratio are illegal and increasingly caught. (bangkokbusinessclub+1)
2. **Undercapitalised company:** The company must have 2,000,000 THB paid-up registered capital per foreign work permit. A company registered with 1,000,000 THB cannot issue even a single foreign work permit — regardless of how legitimate the business is. Recapitalising the company is required, which involves a process at the Department of Business Development (DBD). (issacompass+1)
3. **Working before the work permit is issued:** A common misunderstanding — the Non-B visa alone does NOT authorise work. The employee must wait for the physical work permit booklet before performing any work duties. **Working from day one of arrival on the visa, before the permit is in hand, is a criminal offence in Thailand.** (thailand.acclime+1)
4. **Applying from inside Thailand on a tourist visa:** Most Thai embassies — and the Department of Employment — require the Non-B process to begin from outside Thailand. Converting from a tourist visa to a Non-B inside Thailand is technically possible at the Bangkok Immigration Bureau but is rarely approved by many regional offices, requires additional documentation, and leaves many applicants stranded. (issacompass+1)
5. **Expired work permit kills the entire visa chain:** The work permit and 1-year extension of stay are linked. If the work permit expires — even by one day — the Non-B extension of stay basis evaporates. Many employees lose track of work permit renewal dates (separate from visa renewal). Both must be tracked and renewed 30–45 days before expiry. (rlcoutsourcing)
6. **Occupation on the restricted list:** Thailand reserves **39 occupations strictly for Thai nationals** — including many manual trades, certain clerical roles, and specific professional categories. A foreigner offered employment in a restricted occupation cannot legally be issued a work permit at all, regardless of qualifications. Check the Foreign Business Act's restricted occupation list before accepting a job offer in Thailand. (thailawonline+1)
7. **Company changes employer without updating the work permit:** A foreign employee who moves to a new employer assumes their work permit transfers. **It does not** — work permits in Thailand are issued per employer per position. Switching jobs requires the old work permit to be cancelled and a new one applied for at the new company. Working for the new employer before the new permit is issued is a violation. (rlcoutsourcing)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**Jomtien Immigration for Non-B extensions:** Pattaya and wider Chonburi province employees renew 1-year Non-B extensions at Jomtien Immigration Office. The office handles:

- Annual 1-year extension of stay (based on employment)
- 90-day TM47 address reports
- Re-entry permits (single and multiple)
- Address changes and TM30 (pattaya4all)

**Documents required at Jomtien for annual Non-B extension:** The Jomtien-specific requirement includes office photos (interior + exterior with company sign visible) and a map to the workplace — requirements that Bangkok immigration offices sometimes waive but Jomtien consistently enforces. (pattaya4all)

**Pattaya business landscape:** The Pattaya/Chonburi area's dominant sectors for foreign workers with Non-B visas include hospitality and hotels, English language teaching, international schools, export-oriented manufacturing (particularly in the adjacent Eastern Economic Corridor / EEC), and a growing digital/tech sector. The EEC zones near Chonburi have significant BOI-promoted company activity — **BOI route is worth highlighting in your content as it transforms the Non-B experience.** **[verify EEC/BOI Pattaya angle]** (boi)

**Work permit at Chonburi Labour Office:** Work permits for Chonburi-based employees are issued and renewed at the Chonburi Provincial Employment Office — not the Bangkok Department of Employment. Processing timelines in Chonburi are typically 1–2 weeks **[verify current times]**.

**Agent network (§15):** Pattaya has several law firms and accounting practices offering combined company setup + Non-B + work permit packages. This is a high-value referral category — accounting/legal partners who handle company incorporation, work permits, and annual visa renewals as a bundle. (pattaya4all)

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Can I get a work permit without a Non-B visa?**
No. A valid Non-Immigrant visa (Non-B or equivalent) is the legal prerequisite for work permit issuance. A tourist visa cannot be used to obtain a work permit. (mfa+1)

**2. Can I start working as soon as I have the Non-B visa?**
No — the visa alone does not authorise work. You must wait for the physical work permit booklet to be issued, which takes 7–10 working days after arrival. Only once the permit is in hand may you legally begin work. (mfa)

**3. Can my small company of 2–3 people hire a foreign employee?**
Only if the company has 2,000,000 THB paid-up capital, employs at least 4 Thai nationals with social security contributions, and the role is not a restricted occupation. Most small companies cannot meet this without restructuring. (bangkokbusinessclub+1)

**4. What is the BOI route and is it better?**
BOI (Board of Investment) promotion exempts companies from the 4:1 ratio requirement, eliminates re-entry permit needs, reduces 90-day reporting to annually, and provides OSOS one-stop fast-track for visas and work permits. For eligible businesses, the BOI route is dramatically more efficient — but requires genuine investment-qualifying activities. (boi)

**5. Can I do freelance work on a Non-B visa?**
No — a Non-B visa and work permit are employer-specific. Freelancing for multiple clients technically requires a work permit per employer or a separate business structure (such as setting up your own Thai company). The DTV is the correct visa for foreign-income freelancers. (issacompass)

**6. What happens to my visa if my company fires me?**
Your work permit is cancelled. Your 1-year extension of stay basis immediately ceases. You typically have a brief period to find a new employer and transfer status — but this is not explicitly guaranteed in the regulations. Seek legal advice immediately. (rlcoutsourcing)

**7. Can I change jobs without leaving Thailand?**
Yes, in principle — but it requires cancelling the current work permit and applying for a new one with the new employer before starting. The process can require a new Non-B visa application at an embassy if done outside OSOS channels. Allow 4–6 weeks. (thailandlawoffice)

**8. How does the 3-year Non-B visa work?**
The 3-year multiple-entry Non-B is available for senior executives and business representatives meeting specific criteria (including company capital requirements). It reduces the frequency of embassy visits. Each entry still grants 90 days; the 1-year extension is still done annually at Immigration. (mfa)

**9. Can a company director give themselves a work permit?**
Yes — a foreign company director can sponsor their own work permit through their own company, subject to the same capital and ratio requirements. The director's position must appear in the company registration documents. (thailandlawonline)

**10. Do I need a work permit to attend business meetings in Thailand?**
No — if you are attending meetings, negotiations, or conferences without receiving Thai-sourced income, the Non-B visa for business visit purposes covers this without a work permit requirement. Signing contracts or making decisions is generally considered acceptable; performing regular work duties is not. (issacompass)

**11. What are the 39 restricted occupations?**
The full list is in the Foreign Business Act's Schedule. Restricted roles include: driving ordinary motor vehicles, clerical/secretarial work, retail sales, bricklaying/carpentry/other construction trades, hairdressing, Thai massage, silk weaving, and several others. Always verify your specific role before starting the process. (thailawonline+1)

**12. How do I renew my Non-B in Pattaya?**
Submit TM.7 + your work permit + company documents (tax returns, social security filings, office photos, employment letter) at Jomtien Immigration — up to 30 days before expiry. Cost: 1,900 THB extension fee. Jomtien also handles the 90-day report and re-entry permit at the same visit. (pattaya4all)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **e-Work Permit system launched:** A new digital work permit application system simplifies submission and tracking — employers and employees can now file electronically, reducing the physical queuing previously required at the Department of Employment (rlcoutsourcing)
- **Stricter ratio enforcement in 2025–2026:** Social security contribution records are now more thoroughly cross-checked against the stated 4:1 ratio at work permit renewal. Shell employees with no genuine contributions are flagged (bangkokbusinessclub+1)
- **Visa code consolidation (August 2025):** The Non-B category is preserved within Thailand's consolidated 7-category non-immigrant visa structure — referenced differently in some official documentation but functionally unchanged (thailand.prd+1)
- **TDAC replaces TM6:** Digital arrival card now required pre-travel for all arrivals including Non-B holders (visahq)
- **BOI EEC expansion:** The Eastern Economic Corridor (covering Chonburi, Rayong, and Chachoengsao) continues expanding BOI-promoted investment zones directly adjacent to Pattaya — increasing the number of foreign employees in the area using the BOI fast-track route **[verify current EEC BOI categories]**
- **Increased scrutiny on "business visit" Non-B usage:** Officers are more carefully distinguishing between genuine business visitors (meetings only) and de facto employees operating without work permits — companies sending staff to Thailand on repeated business-visit Non-B entries without formalising employment are being questioned (issacompass)
