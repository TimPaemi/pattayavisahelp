## Who it's for

This page covers the **short-stay options** that bring the vast majority of foreigners into Thailand:

1. **Visa Exemption** — for citizens of ~93 eligible countries arriving for tourism, business meetings, or short stays. No visa application required; 60-day stamp on arrival (subject to a proposed reduction to 30 days under review in 2026).
2. **TR (Tourist Visa, Single Entry)** — applied for in advance, gives 60 days, with one 30-day extension available in-country. For travellers from non-exempt countries or those wanting visa-in-passport before travel.
3. **METV (Multiple Entry Tourist Visa)** — 6 months of validity, multiple 60-day entries, intended for travellers making repeat trips or planning longer stays through border bouncing.

This is the highest-traffic page in the visa cluster. **Most readers searching "thailand visa" or "thailand tourist visa" land here first.** It's also the biggest top-of-funnel feeder for the long-stay visa pages — readers realise their needs exceed tourist limits and convert to DTV, retirement, marriage, or education searches.

## Eligibility (full list)

### Visa Exemption (60-day stamp, free)

- Hold a passport from one of the **~93 eligible countries** on Thailand's visa exemption list (UK, US, EU, Australia, Canada, Japan, India, Russia, most ASEAN, etc.) — full list on thaievisa.go.th
- Passport valid for at least **6 months** at time of entry
- Possess a return/onward ticket out of Thailand within 60 days
- Sufficient funds: **20,000 THB per person / 40,000 THB per family** (this requirement is occasionally enforced as a "cash check" at airports — see Pitfalls)
- Hotel/accommodation booking proof
- Completed **Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)** — mandatory pre-travel since the abolition of paper TM6 forms
- Not blacklisted, not previously deported, no overstay history

### TR Single Entry Tourist Visa

- Same passport / financial / accommodation requirements as visa exemption
- Some nationalities (smaller countries, some African and Middle Eastern nations) **must** use the TR visa because they are not on the visa exemption list
- Apply via Thai e-Visa portal (thaievisa.go.th) or at a Royal Thai Embassy abroad
- Single entry only — once you exit Thailand, the visa is consumed

### METV Multiple Entry Tourist Visa

- Same eligibility as TR but with stricter financial documentation
- Must show **bank statements with minimum balance of approximately 200,000 THB** (≈$5,600) for the past 6 months — this is the practical threshold even if some embassies cite lower figures
- Must demonstrate intent for multiple-trip travel (work-related, family in Thailand, business meetings, repeated short stays)
- Apply at a Royal Thai Embassy/Consulate abroad — **not available via the standard e-Visa system at most posts**
- Cannot be applied for from inside Thailand

## Required documents

### Visa Exemption (no application — these are checked at the border)

1. Passport (≥6 months validity)
2. Return/onward flight ticket
3. Hotel/accommodation booking
4. Cash or proof of funds (20,000 THB per person / 40,000 THB per family — see Pitfalls)
5. Completed TDAC (Thailand Digital Arrival Card) submitted before boarding

### TR Single Entry Tourist Visa (e-Visa or embassy)

1. Valid passport (≥6 months) with copy of biodata page
2. Recent passport-sized photograph (white background)
3. Proof of accommodation in Thailand (hotel booking, rental agreement, host letter)
4. Proof of onward travel (flight ticket out of Thailand within 60 days)
5. Bank statement (last 3 months) showing minimum ~20,000 THB balance — embassies vary
6. Completed Thai e-Visa application form
7. Visa application fee (currently **1,000 THB** equivalent, approximately $30 USD — fees may change after 27 April 2026 under the new consular fee structure) **[verify]**

### METV Multiple Entry Tourist Visa (embassy only)

1. All TR documents above, plus:
2. **Bank statements showing 6 months of minimum balance** ≈ 200,000 THB
3. Employment letter confirming work permitting multiple trips, OR proof of family ties to Thailand, OR business letter
4. Completed application explaining the multiple-trip purpose
5. Visa fee approximately **5,000 THB** equivalent **[verify per embassy]**

Carry physical cash or proof of funds (bank app screenshot, recent statement) when arriving.

## Application process — step by step

### Visa Exemption — no application needed

1. Confirm your nationality is on the visa exemption list (thaievisa.go.th)
2. Book return/onward flight and hotel accommodation in Thailand
3. **Complete the TDAC (Thailand Digital Arrival Card) before boarding** — mandatory since 2024, now strictly enforced
4. Arrive in Thailand with passport, TDAC confirmation, accommodation proof, return ticket, and 20,000 THB cash/proof
5. Receive 60-day permission-to-stay stamp on arrival
6. Optional: Apply for 30-day extension at any Immigration office before stamp expires (1,900 THB)

### TR Single Entry — via e-Visa portal

1. Visit https://www.thaievisa.go.th
2. Create account, select "Tourist Visa (TR)", select your country of nationality and the embassy you want to process the application
3. Upload passport, photo, accommodation, financial documents, return ticket
4. Pay visa fee online (1,000 THB equivalent)
5. Receive e-Visa approval by email — typically 1–5 working days
6. Print the e-Visa or save to phone; show on arrival at Thai immigration
7. Receive 60-day permission-to-stay stamp on arrival

### METV — embassy only (typically home country)

1. Gather all documents (passport, photo, financial, employment, accommodation)
2. Submit application in person at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate
3. Pay visa fee (≈5,000 THB)
4. Processing: 5–15 working days depending on post
5. Receive METV in passport — valid 6 months from issue, multiple entries, 60 days per entry

### Extending in Thailand (any of the three above)

1. Visit any Thai Immigration Office (Jomtien for Pattaya residents) within 30 days of stamp expiry
2. Submit TM.7 form, passport, photo, TM30, accommodation proof
3. Pay 1,900 THB
4. Receive a 30-day extension stamp (or, in rare cases at land borders, a 7-day stamp)

## Duration & extensions

- **Visa Exemption:** 60 days on arrival (proposed reduction to 30 days under review — see What Changed). Extendable once by 30 days at Immigration. Maximum stay: **90 days**.
- **TR Single Entry:** 60 days on arrival. Extendable once by 30 days. Maximum stay: **90 days**.
- **METV:** 6-month visa validity, 60-day stay per entry, **unlimited entries during the 6 months**. Each entry's 60 days can be extended by 30 days at Immigration. Theoretical maximum continuous stay using METV + repeat exits + extensions: nearly the entire 6-month visa validity period plus subsequent 30-day extension on the final entry.
- **Cannot be converted in-country to long-stay visas** in most cases — readers needing >90 days should apply for DTV, retirement, marriage, education, or business visas before arriving (or via TM.86/87 conversion in narrow cases).

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Not applicable.** 90-day TM47 reporting only applies to foreigners staying 90+ continuous days **on a Non-Immigrant visa or Extension of Stay**. Tourist stays max out at 90 days (60 + 30 extension) and don't trigger the 90-day reporting requirement.

## Re-entry permit need + cost

- **Visa Exemption / TR:** Not relevant — these are single-entry. Once you leave Thailand, the stamp is consumed.
- **METV:** Multiple-entry by design. No re-entry permit needed during the 6-month visa validity. Just exit and re-enter; you receive a fresh 60-day stamp on each entry.

## Common pitfalls

1. **The "20,000 THB Cash Trap":** Random border cash checks at major airports have been reported throughout 2025–2026, particularly at Suvarnabhumi (BKK) and Don Mueang (DMK). Travellers arriving on visa exemption without 20,000 THB cash equivalent (per person) have been turned away despite holding all other documents. Carry physical cash, traveller's cheques, or have a bank app ready to demonstrate accessible funds.
2. **Forgetting the TDAC:** The Thailand Digital Arrival Card is **mandatory** since 2024 and replaces the old paper TM6. Some travellers still arrive at the gate without completing it. Airlines increasingly check TDAC before boarding. Complete it on https://tdac.immigration.go.th up to 3 days before flight.
3. **Misunderstanding the 2-entries-per-year rule on land borders:** Visa-exempt entries via **land borders** (from Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar) are now limited to **2 per calendar year**. Air arrivals can still receive up to 6 visa-exempt entries per calendar year. The two-per-year cap on land entries effectively kills the old "weekly border-bounce" lifestyle, but doesn't affect tourists arriving by air for genuine holidays.
4. **Using visa exemption to live in Thailand long-term:** As of November 2025, immigration officers can deny entry to anyone with more than 2 prior visa-exempt entries in a rolling 12-month period without a justifiable reason. The system is now flagged at major airports. Long-stayers must use a proper long-stay visa (DTV, retirement, marriage, education, or business).
5. **Applying for the TR/METV from within Thailand:** Both must be applied for from outside Thailand. Conversions from tourist status to long-stay visas use the TM.86/87 process and are restricted in scope.
6. **Letting the 60-day stamp expire before extending:** Extensions must be applied for **before the current stamp expires**. Overstay starts the day after expiry — 500 THB/day fine, capped at 20,000 THB, with 90+ day overstays triggering blacklisting.
7. **Confusing METV with DTV:** Many remote workers and digital nomads ask about METV when they actually need DTV. METV is for tourism with repeated trips. DTV is the proper visa for working remotely from Thailand on foreign income for up to 5 years.

## Pattaya-specific considerations

- **Pattaya's tourist demographic:** The vast majority of Pattaya's first-time foreign visitors arrive on visa exemption, not TR or METV. They're typically 7–14 day tourists, not long-stayers.
- **Jomtien Immigration** handles all in-country tourist extensions for Pattaya residents — same office that handles all the long-stay visa categories. The 30-day extension typically takes 30–60 minutes if documents are complete.
- **Dress code at Jomtien:** Tourist extensions are processed at the same office as long-stay visas. The dress code is enforced — **no shorts, no flip-flops** — even for a casual tourist extension. Many tourists are caught off guard and turned away in beachwear.
- **U-Tapao Airport (Pattaya's local):** Most Pattaya tourists arrive via Suvarnabhumi (BKK) or Don Mueang (DMK), but U-Tapao receives a small but growing number of international flights. TDAC is required at all entry points.
- **The "I just want to extend my holiday" reality:** Most Pattaya tourists who fall in love with the area and want to stay longer than 60+30 days end up exploring DTV, retirement, education (Muay Thai or language), or marriage visas. **This page should funnel them clearly to those next options.**

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Do I need a visa to visit Thailand for two weeks?**
Almost certainly no — if you're from one of the ~93 visa-exempt countries (UK, US, EU, Australia, Canada, Japan, India, etc.), you'll get a 60-day stamp on arrival without any application. Just complete the TDAC before boarding.

**2. How long can I stay in Thailand without a visa?**
Up to 60 days on arrival under visa exemption (subject to a proposed reduction to 30 days currently under review as of April 2026). You can extend once by 30 days at any Immigration office for 1,900 THB, giving up to 90 days total.

**3. What's the difference between TR and METV?**
TR is a single-entry visa (one entry, 60 days, extendable by 30). METV is a 6-month multiple-entry visa allowing unlimited entries with 60 days per entry. METV requires more financial documentation (~200k THB bank balance for 6 months) and is only available at embassies abroad.

**4. Can I extend my tourist stamp twice?**
Generally no — at airport-based entries, you get one 30-day extension on top of the initial 60 days. At land-border entries, a smaller second extension (7 days) has occasionally been issued in 2025–2026 but is not guaranteed. After that, you need to leave Thailand or convert to a long-stay visa.

**5. What is the TDAC and do I really need it?**
The Thailand Digital Arrival Card is mandatory since 2024 and replaced the old paper TM6 cards. Complete it online at tdac.immigration.go.th within 3 days before your flight. Airlines increasingly check before boarding; arrivals without TDAC face delays at immigration.

**6. Can I do border runs to keep extending my tourist stay?**
Not anymore for visa-exempt entries. Land-border visa-exempt entries are limited to **2 per calendar year**. Air arrivals can still receive up to 6 per year, but immigration officers are now flagging passports with more than 2 prior visa-exempt entries in 12 months. Long-stayers must use a proper long-stay visa.

**7. What happens if I overstay my tourist visa?**
500 THB per day fine, capped at 20,000 THB. Overstays of 90+ days result in immigration blacklisting (1–10 year ban depending on duration). Always extend before the stamp expires or leave Thailand by your last legal day.

**8. Can I work on a tourist visa?**
No. All forms of work — paid or unpaid, for Thai or foreign employers — are prohibited on tourist visas, visa exemption, and METV. Even remote work for foreign clients is technically a grey area; the DTV is the proper visa for that situation.

**9. Can I convert my tourist visa to a retirement / marriage / education visa in Thailand?**
Sometimes yes, in narrow cases, via TM.86 (from TR) or TM.87 (from visa exemption). This is most common for retirement (Non-O conversion) and marriage. For education and business visas, it's possible but heavily document-dependent. For DTV, you must leave Thailand and apply from your home country.

**10. How much does a Thai tourist visa cost?**
TR Single Entry: ~1,000 THB ($30 USD). METV: ~5,000 THB ($140 USD). 30-day extension: 1,900 THB. Visa exemption: free. **A new fee structure took effect on 27 April 2026 — verify current prices on thaievisa.go.th before applying.**

**11. Is the 60-day visa-free stay being reduced to 30 days?**
There's an active proposal to reduce visa-exempt stays from 60 to 30 days, but as of April 2026 **no decision has been made and no implementation date is set.** The current 60-day allowance remains in effect. Even if reduced, a 30-day extension would still bring the maximum stay to 60 days.

**12. Can I travel within ASEAN and use Thailand as a base for several months?**
Yes — but METV is the proper visa for this lifestyle, not repeated visa-exempt entries. The METV's 6-month multiple-entry validity covers exactly this scenario: arrive in Thailand, hop to Singapore/Vietnam/etc. for short trips, return to Thailand for fresh 60-day stays. For longer than 6 months, the DTV (5-year validity) is the better long-term solution.

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **TDAC replaced paper TM6 (2024):** All arrivals must now complete the Thailand Digital Arrival Card before boarding. Airlines increasingly enforce this at the gate.
- **60-day visa exemption confirmed (carried into 2026):** The expansion from 30 to 60 days for visa-exempt nationalities (effective late 2024) is still in force as of April 2026.
- **Proposal to reduce 60 days back to 30 days (under review March–April 2026):** The Minister of Foreign Affairs announced on 20 March 2026 that the ministry is preparing a proposal to reduce visa-exempt stays back to 30 days. **No decision has been made and no implementation date is set as of April 2026.**
- **Land-border visa-exempt entries capped at 2 per calendar year (November 2025):** The old "border bounce" lifestyle of arriving by land every month is now blocked. Air arrivals still get up to 6 per year.
- **Immigration system flags repeat visa-exempt entries:** Computer systems at BKK, DMK, CNX, and HKT now flag any passport with >2 visa-exempt entries in a rolling 12-month period.
- **20,000 THB cash check enforcement:** Random border cash checks have increased throughout 2025–2026. Travellers without funds proof have been turned away.
- **New consular fee structure (effective 27 April 2026):** Thailand introduced revised visa and consular fees on this date. SMART, LTR, DTV, and other visa fees changed; specific TR/METV impact is being clarified — **verify before publishing.**
- **e-Visa system stability improvements:** thaievisa.go.th has had several backend improvements, reducing the form-error and upload-failure issues reported in earlier years.
