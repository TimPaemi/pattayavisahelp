## China-specific visa context

### Document legalisation chain — China is NOT a Hague signatory

China uses direct consular legalisation:

1. **Notarisation** by Chinese notary
2. **Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the PRC** authentication
3. **Royal Thai Embassy Beijing** or Chinese Foreign Affairs Office (FAO) certification
4. **Thai translation** in Pattaya (Chinese→Thai specialist; 1,500–3,000 THB)
5. **Thai MFA legalisation** in Bangkok
6. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Chinese chain:** 6–10 weeks, ~$150–300.

## Chinese financial considerations

### Capital controls

China has strict foreign exchange controls:

- **Annual quota:** USD 50,000 per individual per year for personal foreign exchange
- For property purchase or retirement deposit: special applications may be required
- AML scrutiny on large outbound transfers
- Practical reality: Many Chinese expats use multi-year planning + family member quotas

### Currency and transfers

- **CNY–THB rate (April 2026):** ~5 THB per CNY
- **UnionPay** widely accepted in Pattaya for daily spending
- **WeChat Pay / Alipay:** functional for Chinese tourists; less so for permanent residents
- **Major transfers** via Chinese bank → SWIFT to Thai bank; FET form generated

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Is Thailand visa-free for Chinese citizens?**
**Yes** — 60 days visa-exempt confirmed for 2026 (subject to proposed reduction).

**2. How do Chinese fund the 800k retirement deposit under capital controls?**
Annual USD 50,000 quota covers it; multi-year accumulation if needed. Some use family-member quotas combined. SWIFT transfer generates FET. Coordinate with Chinese bank early.

**3. Can I buy property in Pattaya as a Chinese citizen?**
**Yes** — same condo ownership rules apply (49% foreign quota). Many Chinese investors buy Pattaya condos. ICBC offers Thai property mortgages for Chinese buyers.

**4. Is the Privilege Visa popular among Chinese?**
**Yes** — Privilege Gold and Platinum tiers popular among affluent Chinese due to no income proof requirements and flexibility under capital controls.

**5. Where do Chinese expats live in Pattaya?**
Mixed across neighbourhoods; some concentration in newer condo developments. Less geographically concentrated than UK/German/Russian communities.

**6. Driving licence conversion?**
Chinese driving licence can be converted to Thai. **China is NOT Hague signatory**, so additional authentication required. Translation essential.

**7. Healthcare for Chinese expats?**
Bangkok Hospital Pattaya has Chinese-speaking medical staff. Pattaya healthcare quality comparable to or better than non-Tier-1 Chinese hospitals.

**8. Tax considerations?**
China-Thailand DTA exists. **Chinese tax residency** depends on multiple tests; consult Chinese tax advisor before extended Thailand stays. Thai 180-day rule applies.
