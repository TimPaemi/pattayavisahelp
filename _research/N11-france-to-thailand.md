## France-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance

- **Bulletin n°3 du casier judiciaire** — issued by Casier Judiciaire National (Nantes)
- Cost: free
- Apply online at cjn.justice.gouv.fr
- Time: 1–3 weeks

### Document legalisation

France is Hague Apostille Convention member:

1. **Apostille from Cour d'Appel** (regional court of appeal)
2. **Royal Thai Embassy Paris** certification
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya (French→Thai specialist; 1,000–2,500 THB)
4. **Thai MFA legalisation**
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total French chain:** 5–8 weeks.

## French tax considerations

### Tax residency

- **France considers you tax resident** if your main home, family, or economic interests are in France
- Living abroad doesn't automatically end French tax residency
- Establishing fiscal domicile abroad requires multiple criteria

### Pensions

- **Retraite de base** (state pension, CNAV): taxable in Thailand if remitted (Thai tax resident); French tax credit
- **Retraite complémentaire** (supplementary, AGIRC-ARRCO): same rules
- **Civil service pensions:** typically remain French-taxable under DTA

### France-Thailand DTA

Provides credits, source rules, tie-breaker.

## 6 FAQ questions

**1. Non-O-X eligibility?** Yes, France is on the 14-country list.

**2. CSG/CRDS on French income remitted to Thailand?** Yes — these French social contributions apply to French-source income regardless of residency. Specific rules; consult French tax advisor.

**3. Where do French expats live in Pattaya?** Jomtien primarily; some Pratumnak. Active French expat community.

**4. Healthcare?** French CPAM doesn't cover Thailand; Caisse des Français de l'Étranger (CFE) optional supplementary; most use Thai/international.

**5. Driving licence?** Hague member; conversion straightforward at Banglamung DLT.

**6. Pension portability?** French pensions portable to Thailand; tax treatment per DTA.
