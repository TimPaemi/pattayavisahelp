## Who it's for

The LTR Visa is Thailand's flagship 10-year premium residency programme, **administered by the Board of Investment (BOI) — not by standard immigration channels.** It targets four distinct high-value profiles: wealthy global citizens, affluent retirees, remote professionals earning foreign income, and highly-skilled professionals employed in Thailand's target industries. A fifth category covers spouses and dependents of primary holders.

**Unlike the Thailand Privilege visa, the LTR comes with substantive tax benefits** — making it the smart-money choice for high-income individuals who plan to spend 180+ days per year in Thailand and want to manage their tax position. All categories receive a digital work permit, fast-track airport clearance, reduced 90-day reporting, and no re-entry permit requirement. (ltr.boi+7)

## Eligibility (full list)

### LTR-W: Wealthy Global Citizen

- **Total assets of at least USD 1,000,000** (dct.or+1)
- **Personal income of at least USD 80,000/year** averaged over the past 2 years (iglu+1)
- **Investment in Thailand of at least USD 500,000** in any of: Thai government bonds (5+ years maturity), direct investment in Thai limited/public company, or Thai real estate (hlbthai+1)
- Health insurance with minimum USD 50,000 coverage, Thai social security, or THB 100,000 deposit (svbl.co+1)

### LTR-P: Wealthy Pensioner

- **Age 50 years or older** (pacificprime.co+1)
- Personal income of at least USD 80,000/year (boi+1)
- OR income of USD 40,000–80,000/year + investment of USD 250,000 in Thai government bonds, Thai company direct investment, or Thai real estate (hlbthai+1)
- Health insurance with minimum USD 50,000 coverage, Thai social security, or THB 100,000 deposit (iglu)

### LTR-T: Work-from-Thailand Professional

- Personal income of at least USD 80,000/year averaged over the past 2 years (dct.or)
- OR income of USD 40,000–80,000/year + Master's degree or above, OR ownership of intellectual property, OR Series A funding received (dct.or)
- **Current employer must be:** a public company listed on a stock exchange, OR a private company operating for at least 3 years with **combined revenue of at least USD 150 million in the last 3 years** (dct.or)
- At least 5 years of work experience in the relevant field over the past 10 years (dct.or)
- Health insurance with minimum USD 50,000 coverage (iglu)

### LTR-H: Highly-Skilled Professional

- Personal income of at least USD 80,000/year averaged over the past 2 years (siam-legal)
- OR income of USD 40,000–80,000/year + Master's degree (or higher) OR special expertise relevant to the Thailand assignment (siam-legal)
- OR no minimum income for professionals working for Thai government agencies (dct.or)
- **Employment in one of Thailand's 15 targeted industries** (see Pattaya/EEC section) (naraipartners)
- At least 5 years of work experience in the targeted industry — waived for PhD holders or Thai government agency employees (juslaws+1)
- Health insurance with minimum USD 50,000 coverage (juslaws)

### LTR-O: Spouse and Dependents

- Valid spouse/dependent relationship with a primary LTR holder (thaiembassy+1)
- Same visa duration as primary holder; same benefits except the 17% tax rate for Highly-Skilled (thaiembassy)
- Children under 20 qualify as dependents **[verify age cap]** (thaiembassy)

## Required documents

**Universal documents for all LTR categories:**

1. Valid passport — minimum 6 months' remaining validity, full colour scan of biodata page (siam-legal+1)
2. Recent passport-sized photograph (siam-legal)
3. Criminal background check from home country (FBI/ACPO equivalent) — validity: typically 3 months (thaielite-express+1)
4. Health insurance certificate — minimum USD 50,000 coverage, **clearly stated in English** (siam-legal+1)
5. Bank statement or financial certificate — demonstrating required assets/income (siam-legal)

### Category-specific documents

**LTR-W (Wealthy Global Citizen):**

1. Asset certificate (bank, investment, property valuations) showing USD 1,000,000+ (iglu)
2. Income evidence for past 2 years — tax returns, bank statements, income certificate (iglu)
3. Investment proof in Thailand: bond certificate, company investment agreement, or property title deed (Chanote) showing USD 500,000+ (hlbthai+1)

**LTR-P (Wealthy Pensioner):**

1. Proof of age 50+ — passport (pacificprime.co)
2. Pension/retirement income evidence — pension award letter, bank statements showing consistent income (dct.or)
3. Investment proof in Thailand if using the reduced income route: bond/company/property documentation showing USD 250,000+ (boi+1)

**LTR-T (Work-from-Thailand Professional):**

1. Employment contract with qualifying foreign employer (dct.or)
2. **Employer financial verification** — audited financial statements, SEC filings, or company revenue documentation showing USD 150M+ revenue (siam-legal+1)
3. Income evidence — tax returns, payslips (2 years) (dct.or)
4. Academic certificates if using reduced income + degree route (dct.or)

**LTR-H (Highly-Skilled Professional):**

1. Employment offer/contract with Thai employer in targeted industry (siam-legal+1)
2. Thai employer's BOI promotion certificate or evidence of targeted industry status (juslaws)
3. Academic certificates (Master's/PhD) and/or professional certifications (siam-legal)
4. Work experience documentation — employment certificates, reference letters (5 years in field) (siam-legal)
5. Income evidence — payslips, tax returns (siam-legal)

**All documents must be consistently named across all submitted materials — name discrepancies are a leading cause of delay.** (thaielite-express)

## Application process — step by step

1. **Determine category** — choose the correct LTR category based on your income, assets, and employment situation. Applying under the wrong category is the leading rejection risk (thaielite-express)
2. **Register on the BOI LTR portal** at ltr.boi.go.th — create an account with your email (ltr.boi+1)
3. **Upload all required documents** — passport, criminal background check, health insurance, financial proof, and category-specific documents. Ensure all names are spelled identically across every document (thaielite-express)
4. **BOI initial completeness check** — BOI screens for missing or unreadable documents; if items are absent, a request is issued with a response deadline. Missing the deadline = rejection (thaielite-express)
5. **BOI forwards to government agencies for qualification review** — relevant ministries and revenue department confirm income, assets, industry eligibility (thaielite-express)
6. **Receive BOI Endorsement (Qualification Approval)** — notification via portal confirming your eligibility (thaielite-express)
7. **Pay the THB 50,000 visa fee** (thaielite-express+1)
8. **Choose visa issuance method:**
   - **In Thailand:** Attend the Thailand Immigration and Examination for Smart and LTR visas Centre (TIESC) — part of the BOI One-Stop Service (ltr.boi+1)
   - **Abroad:** Collect visa sticker at any Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate (thaielite-express)
9. **Visa issued** — 5-year initial validity, extendable by another 5 years (kpmg+1)
10. **Digital Work Permit issued (where applicable)** — simultaneously through TIESC (ltr.boi)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Qualification application:** Entirely online via ltr.boi.go.th — can be done from anywhere, including from inside Thailand. This is a **BOI process, not a standard immigration or embassy process.** (ltr.boi+1)

**Visa issuance (two options):**

- **In Thailand (TIESC):** Thailand Immigration and Examination for Smart and LTR Visas Centre — One Stop Service, Chamchuri Square, 18th Floor, Bangkok (ltr.boi+1)
- **Abroad:** Any Royal Thai Embassy after receiving BOI endorsement (thaielite-express)

**Pattaya angle:** There is no TIESC in Pattaya — Bangkok attendance is required for in-Thailand visa issuance. The BOI also has a regional presence in the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) covering Chonburi province, which can assist LTR-H applicants working in EEC-promoted industries. (juslaws)

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial visa:** 5 years, multiple entry (kpmg+1)
- **Extension:** Additional 5 years (total 10 years), if qualifications are still met (kpmg+1)
- **Maximum duration per cycle:** 10 years — after which a new LTR application must be made (siam-legal)
- **Continuous stay:** No mandatory exit requirement — unlimited continuous stay (dudigitalglobal+1)
- **No re-entry permit required** — multiple entry by design (ltr.boi+1)
- **Highly-Skilled employer change:** If the LTR-H holder changes employers, the visa must be terminated and a new application filed with the new employer's details — this is a critical practical limitation (ltr.boi)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes, but dramatically reduced.** Unlike all other Thai visas, LTR holders **report to Immigration once per year only — not every 90 days.** This is one of the most practically significant benefits for long-term residents who find the standard quarterly reporting burdensome. (dudigitalglobal+2)

- Annual TM47 report via the BOI/TIESC One-Stop Service or online (ltr.boi)
- Processing: minimal — single annual visit or online submission
- Fast-track service available at Suvarnabhumi, Don Mueang, Phuket, and Chiang Mai international airports (ltr.boi)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**No re-entry permit required.** The LTR is a multiple-entry visa — holders can exit and re-enter Thailand freely with no re-entry permit at any time during the visa's 5-year validity period. This is a significant advantage over Non-O-A, Non-B, and ED visas. (ltr.boi+1)

## Common pitfalls

1. **Choosing the wrong category:** The most common reason for rejection and delay. Applicants select the closest-sounding category without verifying whether their income type, employment structure, or asset proof genuinely satisfies that category's criteria. BOI evaluators assess each technically — mismatches are rejected, not redirected. **Confirm category logic before building your document set.** (thaielite-express)
2. **Employer financial verification failure for LTR-T:** The requirement that your foreign employer has USD 150 million or more in revenue over 3 years trips up professionals at mid-sized companies, startups, or consulting firms. Freelancers and small-agency employees frequently fail this criterion — they should consider DTV instead. (siam-legal+1)
3. **Missing BOI document request deadline:** Once BOI requests additional documents, there is a hard response window. Applicants who miss it — often because they didn't monitor their email closely or didn't have documents ready — face outright rejection. **Prepare a "document reserve" (police checks, insurance certificates, employer letters) before submitting.** (thaielite-express)
4. **Health insurance USD 50,000 coverage not explicitly stated:** Policies that provide the coverage but describe it in THB, or use aggregate benefit language that doesn't clearly state USD 50,000 per person, are rejected by BOI reviewers. **The figure must be clearly stated in English on the certificate.** (siam-legal)
5. **Inconsistent name spellings across documents:** Even a minor discrepancy — "Jonathan" vs "Jon", or a middle name included in one document but not another — triggers a clarification request and delays processing by weeks. Every document must use exactly the same name as the passport. (thaielite-express)
6. **LTR-H employer change without reapplying:** Changing employers while holding an LTR-H visa requires terminating the current LTR and filing a new application with the new employer's evidence. A holder who simply starts at a new Thai company without going through this process is in violation. (ltr.boi)
7. **Conflating foreign income tax exemption with 17% flat rate:** Only LTR-H Highly-Skilled Professionals get the 17% flat rate on Thai-sourced income. The other three categories (W, P, T) get exemption on foreign-sourced income — not a special rate on Thai income. **These are fundamentally different benefits and serve different planning needs.** (thaiembassy+1)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**EEC and LTR-H:** The Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) — covering Chonburi (which includes Pattaya), Rayong, and Chachoengsao — is explicitly highlighted by BOI as a key zone for LTR Highly-Skilled Professionals. Employers in EEC-promoted industries (see below) who want to hire foreign senior professionals use the LTR-H route as the gold-standard alternative to standard Non-B + work permit. (boi+1)

**BOI's 15 targeted industries for LTR-H** (relevant to EEC/Pattaya employers):

- Automotive and next-generation vehicles
- Smart electronics
- Affluent, medical, and wellness tourism
- Agriculture and biotechnology
- Food for the future
- Robotics
- Aviation and logistics
- Biofuels and biochemicals
- Digital economy
- Medical hub
- Defence industry
- Higher education and R&D
- Financial services
- Software and embedded systems
- Human resource development (naraipartners)

**No LTR/BOI office in Pattaya:** All BOI LTR applications and TIESC visa issuance are in Bangkok. Pattaya-based applicants must travel to Bangkok for issuance — a one-day trip. (thaielite-express)

**Lead routing (§15):** LTR applicants are high-income, sophisticated individuals — the premium end of your referral network. LTR-W and LTR-P leads should be routed to a tax/financial advisory partner; LTR-H leads to a specialist immigration law firm experienced in BOI documentation.

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. How does the LTR visa differ from the Thailand Privilege (Elite) visa?**
LTR requires documented income or assets and involves BOI qualification review (4–8 weeks). Privilege requires only a fee payment (no income proof needed) but provides zero tax benefits. LTR is the choice for high-income individuals who want tax optimisation; Privilege is the choice for those who want maximum simplicity and don't mind the upfront cost. (banyangroupresidences+1)

**2. Which LTR category gets the 17% flat tax rate?**
Only LTR-H (Highly-Skilled Professionals) get the 17% flat rate on Thai-sourced employment income. The other three categories (W, P, T) receive exemption from Thai tax on foreign-sourced income — a different but also valuable benefit. (hlbthai+1)

**3. Is my foreign pension income tax-free under the LTR?**
For LTR-P (Wealthy Pensioner) holders, foreign-sourced income including pension income is exempt from Thai personal income tax under Royal Decree 743, regardless of how much you bring into Thailand. This is a major improvement over the 2024 tax law change that otherwise requires tax residency reporting. (mbmg-group+1)

**4. Can I freelance or work for multiple clients on an LTR visa?**
The LTR-T (Work-from-Thailand) category's digital work permit allows work for the qualifying foreign employer. LTR-H's digital work permit covers the specific Thai employer declared in the application. Multi-client freelance work is a grey area — consult a BOI-registered specialist. (ltr.boi+1)

**5. Can I apply for the LTR while I'm already in Thailand?**
Yes — the qualification application through ltr.boi.go.th can be submitted from anywhere. If you're already in Thailand, you can choose in-Thailand visa issuance at TIESC without leaving. (thaielite-express)

**6. How long does the LTR application take?**
Typically 4–8 weeks for BOI qualification review, plus 4–8 weeks for criminal background checks from many countries. Budget 8–16 weeks total from submission to visa in hand. (thaiembassy+1)

**7. Does my spouse get the same tax benefits as me on the LTR?**
Dependent LTR holders get the same visa benefits (10-year stay, annual reporting, no re-entry permit, airport fast-track) but not the same tax benefits. Tax benefits are personal to the primary LTR holder. (thaiembassy+1)

**8. What counts as a "targeted industry" for the Highly-Skilled Professional category?**
BOI maintains a list of 15 targeted industries — from automotive to digital economy to wellness tourism. Your Thai employer must operate in one of these sectors or be a higher education/research institution or Thai government agency. The full current list is on ltr.boi.go.th. (naraipartners+1)

**9. What happens if I change jobs after getting the LTR-H visa?**
You must notify BOI and terminate your current LTR status, then reapply with the new employer's documentation. Working for a new employer without reapplying is a violation. (ltr.boi)

**10. Is USD 500,000 Thai investment required for all LTR categories?**
No — only LTR-W (Wealthy Global Citizens) require USD 500,000 in Thai investments. LTR-P requires USD 250,000 only if your income is below USD 80,000. LTR-T and LTR-H have no Thai investment requirement. (hlbthai+2)

**11. Is the LTR visa worth it vs just doing annual Non-O-A extensions?**
For retirees with USD 80,000+ annual income: almost certainly yes. The LTR-P eliminates 800k THB seasoning, eliminates health insurance struggles, provides foreign income tax exemption, and removes 90-day reporting. The 50,000 THB visa fee is negligible compared to the administrative relief over 10 years. (thaielite-express+1)

**12. Can LTR holders own and operate a business in Thailand?**
The digital work permit covers declared employment/professional activity. Running a Thai company as an LTR holder for local revenue requires consultation with a specialist — the LTR programme was not designed for local business operators. (juslaws+1)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **BOI Announcement No. Por. 3/2568 (February 4, 2025):** Updated eligibility requirements for Highly-Skilled Professionals — revised the targeted industry list and documentation standards (juslaws)
- **BOI document list update (November 6, 2025):** Revised the official document checklist for all LTR categories — older document sets from 2022–2024 may be outdated (juslaws)
- **Tax law 2024 change (now fully in force in 2026):** Foreign income remitted to Thailand is taxable in the year earned for Thai tax residents — the LTR foreign-income exemption is now dramatically more valuable as a result, creating a clear advantage over DTV and Privilege for anyone spending 180+ days in Thailand (hlbthai+1)
- **BOI March 2026 LTR presentation:** BOI released updated overview material (March 2026, Stockholm presentation) confirming current thresholds and processes — no major structural changes but documentation requirements tightened (boi)
- **EEC expansion continues:** New BOI-promoted industries and projects in Chonburi and adjacent EEC provinces increasing LTR-H demand from multinational employers in the zone (juslaws)
- **Processing time improvement:** BOI has reportedly streamlined the qualification review process; typical applicants with complete documentation see approvals in 4–6 weeks rather than the 8–12 weeks of the early programme (thaiembassy)
