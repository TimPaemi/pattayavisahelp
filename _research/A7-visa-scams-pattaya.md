## Why this content matters

Pattaya has a well-documented visa scam ecosystem. Hundreds of expats have lost thousands of THB each (sometimes tens of thousands) to fraudulent operators. The 2025 enforcement crackdown amplified this:

- **10,000+ ED visa revocations** linked to fake schools

## Common Pattaya visa scams in 2026

### 1. Fake / "ghost" schools issuing ED visas

The 2025 crackdown's largest single category. Operations:

- "School" with no real classes, no campus, no teachers
- Pays you for "enrolment" → issues fake MOE-stamped letter → submits ED visa application
- Student attends nothing
- When MOE inspection comes: visa revoked, deportation notice

**Cost to victim:** 30,000–80,000 THB (paid for "tuition"); plus visa loss, deportation, blacklisting

**Spotting:** See [G10 — How to verify MOE-accredited schools](./G10-verify-moe-school.md)

### 2. Fake "guaranteed approval" visa agents

Visa agencies promising:

- "100% guaranteed approval"
- "We'll get you the visa even if you don't qualify"
- "Cash-only, off-the-books" services

**How they operate:**

- Take your money (10,000–50,000 THB)
- Some submit half-baked applications that get rejected
- Others pocket money entirely and disappear when you ask for the visa
- Some bribe officials (illegally) — works briefly, then everyone gets caught

**The "Pattaya visa scam" of 2024–2025** affected hundreds of tourists who paid ~32,000 THB each to a "language school" that submitted no real visas. Victims overstayed unknowingly when their visas weren't actually issued.

**Spotting red flags:**

- Cash-only payment, no official receipt
- No company registration / can't show DBD certificate
- Vague answers about exactly which Thai authority they submit to
- "Just trust me" attitude vs. transparent process
- "Guaranteed" approval (legitimate agents never guarantee — Immigration decides)

### 3. Fake TDAC / e-Visa websites

Scam websites mimic the official Thai e-Visa portal or Thailand Digital Arrival Card system:

- Charge "service fees" of $20–$80 for what's actually free or much cheaper
- Harvest personal data (passport details, credit card)
- Sometimes don't even submit the actual application

**How to spot:**

- Official Thai e-Visa: https://www.thaievisa.go.th — legitimate
- Official TDAC: https://tdac.immigration.go.th — legitimate
- Anything else charging for "TDAC service" is suspect
- Domain names like "thailand-tdac.com" or "thai-evisa-online.net" are typically scams

### 4. Fake landlord TM30 issues

Some landlords (especially in less-managed buildings):

- Don't file TM30 (their legal duty)
- Charge "fees" of 1,000–3,000 THB for filing they should do free
- Refuse to file when asked, leaving tenant unable to extend visa
- Sublet without owner's permission, creating documentation chaos

**See [G2 TM30 guide](./G2-tm30-reporting.md) for legitimate TM30 process.**

### 5. Fake health insurance policies

**The 2025 fake-insurance ring** sold counterfeit O-A-compliant policies to retirees. Buyers paid 40,000–80,000 THB/year, received documentation that looked legitimate, then discovered the policies were either never issued or had been cancelled when they tried to claim or renew their visa.

**Spotting fake insurance:**

- Verify policy on insurer's official website (not just paperwork)
- Cross-check provider against TGIA approved list (https://longstay.tgia.org)
- Be suspicious of premiums significantly below market rate (50% below = red flag)
- Ask for direct insurer contact, not just agent's number

### 6. Fake police clearance / document services

Some agents offer:

- "Police clearance from your home country in 24 hours" (impossible legitimately)
- "We can certify your foreign documents" (without actual MFA / embassy involvement)
- "Apostille service in Thailand" (Thailand doesn't issue apostilles — see [G9](./G9-foreign-marriage-legalisation.md))

These are either fraud or use forged documents that get caught at the visa application stage.

### 7. The "Embassy income letter" scam

UK, US, and Australian embassies stopped issuing income letters years ago. Some Pattaya agents still claim:

- "We can get you the embassy income letter"
- "Our agent at the embassy can issue it"
- Charging 5,000–15,000 THB for fraudulent letters

**These letters are forgeries.** Using them in retirement visa application is criminal fraud. Use the **800,000 THB bank deposit method** instead.

### 8. The "boost your bank balance" scam

Some agents offer to "park" 800,000 THB in your account temporarily for visa seasoning. This is:

- **Illegal** (immigration fraud)
- **Prosecuted** in 2025 — Thai authorities specifically targeted this
- **Detectable** by Immigration through bank records
- **Not a workaround** — it's a crime that risks deportation and blacklisting

### 9. Border-run operators selling discontinued services

After the November 2025 enforcement (2 visa-exempt land entries per year), some Pattaya operators still promote:

- "Weekly border run packages"
- "Unlimited visa-exempt entries"
- "Best border crossings to avoid scrutiny"

These services don't work in 2026. See [G4 — Visa runs vs extensions](./G4-visa-runs-vs-extensions.md).

### 10. WhatsApp / Facebook visa scammers

Solicitations on Pattaya expat WhatsApp groups and Facebook groups:

- "DM me for cheap visa services"
- "Insider connection at Immigration"
- Offers significantly below market rate

**Most are scams.** Some are legitimate but uninsurable agents. Verify thoroughly before paying.

## How to verify any visa agent

Before paying any visa agent:

1. **Check DBD registration** — every legitimate Thai business has Department of Business Development registration. Ask for company name and verify on dbd.go.th
2. **Physical office visit** — visit the office in person before paying. PO boxes and "service center" addresses are red flags.
3. **References** — ask for 3 recent client references. Call them.
4. **Specific process explanation** — agent should explain exactly which Thai office your application goes to. "Just leave it to us" is suspicious.
5. **Receipt and contract** — get written contract specifying services, deliverables, refund terms.
6. **Online reputation** — search "[agent name] scam" and "[agent name] reviews". Discount the marketing; weight community feedback.
7. **PCEC endorsement** — the Pattaya City Expats Club doesn't formally endorse but you can ask members about specific agents on PCEC channels.

## What to do if you've been scammed

### If you've paid a fake school

1. **Stop the visa application** if not yet submitted
2. **Try to recover funds** — small claims court, payment dispute (if credit card), but realistic recovery is low
3. **Find a legitimate MOE-accredited school** quickly
4. **Consult an immigration lawyer** if you're already on a visa from a fake school

### If you've paid a fake visa agent

1. **Document everything** — receipts, communications, bank transfers
2. **Report to Royal Thai Police** — Tourist Police office in Pattaya
3. **Report to Pattaya Mail / Pattaya News** — public exposure helps prevent future victims
4. **Contact your home country embassy** for advice
5. **Don't try to extend or use any visa** documentation from the scammer until verified

### If you've been issued a fake visa

1. **Visit Jomtien Immigration** to verify your visa status legitimately
2. **Report the scammer**
3. **Begin legal process** to either correct your status or leave Thailand voluntarily before forced removal

## How to find a LEGITIMATE Pattaya visa agent

Honest-by-design framing for the page:

- **Look for:** Long-established firms (10+ years), DBD registered, physical office, written contracts, positive PCEC community references, clear pricing
- **Typical legitimate fees:** Annual extension package 5,000–15,000 THB; complex setups 20,000–80,000 THB
- **Legitimate services:** TM30, 90-day reports, extension assistance, document compilation, Jomtien queue handling
- **What legitimate agents won't promise:** "Guaranteed approval", "off-the-books service", "embassy income letters" (don't exist), "boost your bank balance" (illegal)

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. How can I tell if a Pattaya visa agent is legitimate?**
Verify DBD registration, visit physical office, get written contract, check community references, look for PCEC familiarity. Skip any agent who can't provide all of these.

**2. What was the 2024–2025 Pattaya visa scam?**
A "language school" took ~32,000 THB each from hundreds of victims for ED visa applications that were never properly filed. Victims unknowingly overstayed. Investigation ongoing.

**3. Are TDAC service websites real?**
**Most are scams.** TDAC is FREE at the official site (tdac.immigration.go.th). Anyone charging for it is unauthorised. Apply directly.

**4. The agent says "100% guaranteed approval" — is that real?**
**No.** Legitimate agents never guarantee approval — Immigration decides. "Guaranteed" claims are either fraud or illegal bribery (which gets caught).

**5. Can I really get a fake bank balance for visa seasoning?**
**No — it's illegal and prosecuted.** Thai authorities specifically targeted this scam in 2025. Detection means deportation and blacklisting. Use real funds via legitimate transfer.

**6. My friend recommended a Facebook group visa agent — should I trust them?**
Verify independently. Friend trust ≠ legitimate operation. Check DBD, visit office, get references. Some agents recommended in expat groups have been long-running scammers.

**7. What if I've already paid a fake school?**
Stop visa application (if not yet submitted). Try to recover funds (low success). Find legitimate MOE-accredited school. Consult immigration lawyer if visa already issued from fake school.

**8. How do I report a visa scammer?**
Pattaya Tourist Police, Royal Thai Police, your home country embassy, Pattaya Mail / Pattaya News. Public exposure helps protect future victims.
