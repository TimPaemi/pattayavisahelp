## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant O-A (Long Stay) visa is Thailand's primary retirement visa for foreigners aged 50 years and over who wish to live in Thailand long-term without working. It is the most widely used long-stay option for the global expat retiree community — from British and American pensioners to European early retirees and Australian seniors. It covers leisure, family time, and day-to-day retirement living only. **No employment of any kind is permitted under this visa.** (thaiembassy+2)

## Eligibility (full list)

- **Minimum age: 50 years old** on the date of application (crownsiam+1)
- Valid passport with a minimum of **18 months' remaining validity** at time of application (lawyer-vwork+1)
- No criminal record — police clearance certificate from home country required (siacthai+1)
- No prohibited diseases under Thai Immigration law (tuberculosis, leprosy, elephantiasis, third-stage syphilis, drug addiction) — medical certificate required (crownsiam+1)
- Must meet one of the three financial requirements (see Financial Requirements below) (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)
- Must hold valid health insurance meeting Thai government minimum coverage thresholds (smartlifethailand+1)
- Not on the Thai Immigration blacklist or previously deported (crownsiam)
- Nationals of eligible countries only — most Western countries qualify; check your specific embassy's eligibility list (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)
- **Employment of any kind in Thailand is strictly prohibited** (lawyer-vwork+1)

## Required documents

**Core documents — all applicants:**

1. Valid passport — minimum 18 months remaining validity, with copies of all relevant pages (biodata, current visa, entry stamps) (siacthai+1)
2. Completed Thai visa application form (3 copies at some embassies) (siacthai)
3. Three recent passport-sized photographs (4×6 cm, white background, taken within 6 months) (pattaya4all+1)
4. Personal data form (some embassies) (siacthai)

**Financial proof (one of the three options):**

5a. **Bank deposit route:** Thai bank statement or passbook showing 800,000 THB — seasoned for at least 2 months before initial application, 3 months before annual extension application (asq+1)
5b. **Income route:** Income certificate showing 65,000 THB/month minimum — pension award letter, certified tax return (prior year), or bank statements showing consistent monthly deposits (issacompass+1)
5c. **Combination route:** Bank deposit + annual income totalling 800,000 THB — e.g. 400,000 THB in bank + 400,000 THB annual income (thaiembassy+1)

6. Letter of guarantee from bank confirming the deposit (original, if using bank statement route) (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)

**Health insurance (O-A mandatory):**

7. Health insurance certificate from approved provider showing minimum coverage:
   - **Outpatient:** 40,000 THB minimum (official requirement) — some embassies now enforce 120,000 THB (cyprusconsulate+1)
   - **Inpatient:** 400,000 THB minimum (official requirement) — some sources cite 3,000,000 THB total enforced from 2026 **[verify current embassy standard]** (benoit-partners+1)
   - Policy must cover entire duration of stay in Thailand (thaiembassy+1)

**Background checks:**

8. Police clearance certificate from home country — national-level background check (FBI, ACPO, ACIC, etc. depending on nationality) (crownsiam+1)
9. Medical certificate from licensed doctor in home country confirming no prohibited diseases (crownsiam+1)

**For in-country extension (Jomtien / Thai Immigration):**

10. TM.7 Extension of Stay application form (pattaya4all)
11. TM.30 address registration receipt (landlord-filed, but bring proof) (pattaya4all)
12. Proof of Thai address — rental agreement, utility bill, or letter from accommodation (pattaya4all)
13. Last 90-day TM47 report receipt (pattaya4all)
14. Passport-sized photos (2 copies) (pattaya4all)
15. Bank book (original + copies) or income letter + bank statements (pattaya4all)

| Bank deposit only | 800,000 THB | ~$22,500 | Seasoned 2 months before initial application; 3 months before annual renewal (issacompass+1) |

| Combination | Deposit + income = 800,000 THB/year | — | E.g. 400k deposit + 400k annual income (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy) |
| Mid-year minimum balance | 400,000 THB | ~$11,250 | Balance may not drop below this at any point during the year after extension granted (asq) |

**Critical seasoning rules** (the most-misunderstood aspect of this visa):

- **Before initial application:** 800,000 THB must be in your Thai bank account for a minimum of **2 months** (asq)
- **Immediately after extension is granted:** balance must remain at 800,000 THB for **3 full months** (asq)
- **After those 3 months:** balance may fall but must **never drop below 400,000 THB** — not even for a single day (asq)
- **Before the following year's extension application:** balance must be back up to 800,000 THB, held for **2 full months before you apply** (not before expiry — before application date) (asq)

## Application process — step by step

### Path A: Applying at a Thai Embassy abroad (Non-O-A, 1-year visa)

1. Open a Thai bank account (Bangkok Bank or Kasikorn recommended) or arrange income documentation (thaiembassy)
2. Obtain police clearance from your home country (allow 4–8 weeks for national-level checks) (siacthai)
3. Obtain medical certificate from a licensed doctor (siacthai)
4. Purchase qualifying health insurance policy (smartlifethailand)
5. Gather all documents, ensure bank balance is seasoned for 2+ months (asq)
6. Submit application at Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your home country — in-person at most posts (go2-thailand)
7. Pay application fee (lawyer-vwork)
8. Receive Non-O-A visa sticker — valid for 1 year from date of issue, allowing one or multiple entries depending on embassy (go2-thailand)
9. Enter Thailand on or before the visa expiry date; receive a 1-year permission to stay stamp on arrival (go2-thailand)

### Path B: Entering on Non-O (90 days) then extending in-country

1. Apply for a Non-Immigrant O visa (retirement purpose) from Thai Embassy abroad — typically issued for 90 days (siam-legal+1)
2. Enter Thailand, open Thai bank account, season 800,000 THB (lawyer-vwork)
3. After residing for 60 days (or when you have 30 days or fewer remaining on your permission to stay), apply for 1-year extension at local Thai Immigration office (visakohsamui+1)
4. Submit TM.7 form + all documents at Jomtien Immigration (Pattaya) (pattaya4all)
5. Pay 1,900 THB extension fee (topthairealestate)
6. Receive 1-year stamp (topthairealestate)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Initial Non-O-A visa:** Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your country of nationality or legal residence. Most posts require an in-person appointment. Some embassies (check yours) now accept applications by post. (go2-thailand)

**Annual extension** (once in Thailand): At any Thai Immigration office. Pattaya residents use **Jomtien Immigration Office**. Extensions are completed in-country — no need to leave Thailand annually if you maintain compliance. (pattaya4all)

**Note:** Unlike the DTV, the Non-O-A can only be initially obtained abroad; however, once you've done the first year, annual extensions are done at Jomtien/Thai Immigration indefinitely without leaving the country. (go2-thailand)

## Realistic processing time

| Scenario | Timeline |
|---|---|
| Embassy application (best case) | 3–7 business days |
| Embassy application (typical) | 1–2 weeks |
| Police clearance wait (home country) | 4–8 weeks — start this first (siacthai) |
| In-country annual extension at Jomtien | Same day — 1–2 hours if documents complete (retiredinpattaya) |

**Practical advice:** The police clearance bottleneck means total lead time from "I want this visa" to "visa in hand" is commonly **6–10 weeks** if starting from scratch. The bank account seasoning adds another 2–3 months before you can do your first in-country extension.

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial Non-O-A visa validity:** 1 year from date of issue (embassy-issued) (go2-thailand)
- **Permission to stay on entry:** 1 year from date of arrival (go2-thailand)
- **Annual extension:** 1 year, applied for at Thai Immigration, fee 1,900 THB (topthairealestate)
- **Extensions are unlimited** as long as financial and insurance requirements are maintained year on year (go2-thailand)
- **No maximum cumulative stay** — retirees can renew indefinitely (go2-thailand)
- **Conversion:** Non-O-A can be "converted" to other statuses but rarely done; most retirees simply extend year after year (issacompass)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — 90-day reporting (TM47) is mandatory for all retirement visa holders.** Every foreigner staying in Thailand for 90 or more consecutive days must report their current address to Thai Immigration every 90 days. Options:

- **Online:** via immigration.go.th TM47 online portal — open from 15 days before to 7 days after due date
- **In person:** Jomtien Immigration Office, Pattaya — typically a 5-minute process with online pre-report (retiredinpattaya)
- **By post:** Registered mail to Jomtien Immigration — rarely used

**Penalty for missing:** 2,000 THB fine, typically paid before your next immigration visit. Accumulating missed reports can affect extension approval. Re-entering Thailand resets the 90-day clock entirely. (retiredinpattaya)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required.** If you exit Thailand while your 1-year extension is active, you must have a re-entry permit (single or multiple), otherwise your extension is cancelled on departure and you must start the process again from a new Non-O-A. (thaiembassy)

| Type | Cost | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| Single re-entry permit | 1,000 THB | One trip abroad during current extension |
| Multiple re-entry permit | 3,800 THB | Unlimited exits/re-entries during current extension |

Re-entry permits are purchased at any Thai Immigration office (including Jomtien) **before** you exit Thailand — not at the airport. Airport purchase is possible but riskier. Permit expires when current extension expires. (thaiembassy)

## Common pitfalls (5+ specific ones, real examples)

1. **The seasoning trap — most common first-year failure:** Wiring 800,000 THB to a Thai bank account the week before your extension application and expecting it to count. The rules are explicit: funds must sit for 2 full months before you apply. Immigration can (and does) check the transaction history on your bank book. Late deposits are rejected. (asq)
2. **The 400,000 THB floor — silent killer of renewals:** After your extension is granted, most retirees think the 800k is "done" and spend freely. But the balance can never fall below 400,000 THB — not even for a single day during the year. One ATM withdrawal that dips below 400k can cause next year's extension to be denied. Immigration asks for a full 12-month bank statement. (asq)
3. **Health insurance — coverage gaps cause extension denial:** Buying a policy that technically meets minimums on paper but has major exclusions (pre-existing conditions, age caps, hospitalisation sub-limits) is increasingly scrutinised. In 2026, some embassies and immigration offices are cross-checking policy documents. A lapsed policy — even for one day — can trigger extension refusal. (benoit-partners+1)
4. **Police clearance expiry:** Many countries' national-level police clearances are only valid for 3–6 months. If yours expires before your embassy appointment or extension application, you must obtain a new one. Allow 6–8 weeks for US (FBI), UK (ACPO DBS), Australian (ACIC) clearances. (siacthai)
5. **No re-entry permit before a trip abroad:** Leaving Thailand (even for a weekend in Cambodia or Laos) without a re-entry permit cancels your active 1-year extension. The stamp is gone. You return on a fresh entry, revert to 30-day visa-exempt entry, and must start the process again. This catches retirees who "just pop to Vietnam" unexpectedly. (thaiembassy)
6. **TM30 not filed by landlord:** If your landlord (condo developer, hotel, or private owner) fails to file the TM30 accommodation report within 24 hours of your arrival, the document won't exist when Immigration asks for it at extension time. This is technically the landlord's legal responsibility — but it's your visa on the line. Get the TM30 receipt and guard it. **[verify — TM30 enforcement varies by office]**
7. **Medical certificate from wrong type of doctor:** Some embassies require the medical certificate to be issued by a licensed physician and stamped by a medical council or notarised. A letter from a general practitioner on plain letterhead is often rejected at stricter posts. Confirm requirements with your specific embassy. (siacthai)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**Jomtien Immigration Office** is the primary immigration office serving all Pattaya and Chonburi province residents. Retirement visa extensions, 90-day reports, re-entry permits, and TM30 matters are all handled here.

- **Address:** Jomtien Immigration Office, Soi 5, Jomtien Beach Road, Nong Prue, Bang Lamung, Chonburi 20150 **[verify current address]**
- **Operating hours:** Monday–Friday, 08:30–16:30, closed 12:00–13:00 **[verify]**
- **Queue reality for retirement extensions:** Arrive by 07:30 to queue before 08:30 opening. Bring a number from the machine immediately on entry. Tuesday–Thursday are the least busy days. Mondays and Fridays after public holidays are chaotic. (retiredinpattaya)
- **Processing time at Jomtien:** Retirement extension typically takes 1–2 hours on a normal day (retiredinpattaya)
- **Re-entry permits at Jomtien:** Typically 10–20 minutes — no appointment needed, do this at the same visit as your extension (retiredinpattaya)
- **Local Pattaya retirement visa agents:** Several operate on Sukhumvit Road and Jomtien Beach Road. Services include: gathering and photocopying documents, queuing on your behalf, and submitting extensions. Typical service fee: 2,000–6,000 THB. Useful for those who find the paperwork daunting. Quality varies — ask for recent client references. (pattaya4all)
- **Pattaya-specific bank options for the 800k deposit:** Bangkok Bank (Pattaya branch), Kasikorn Bank, and SCB are most popular among retirees for seasoning the 800,000 THB. Bangkok Bank historically most immigration-friendly for showing statements in required format. **[verify current bank acceptance]**
- **Medical letters for insurance purposes:** Several Pattaya hospitals (Bangkok Hospital Pattaya, Pattaya Memorial Hospital) can provide medical certificates and assist with insurance requirements for the Non-O-A — a potential warm referral for your specialist network. **[verify]**

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Can I get a retirement visa if I'm under 50?**
No. The minimum age is exactly 50 years old on the date of application. There is no exception. Under-50s wanting long stays should look at the DTV, LTR, or education visa. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)

**2. Do I need to keep 800,000 THB in a Thai bank account permanently?**
Not permanently. You need 800k for 2 months before applying, then 800k for 3 months after your extension is granted, then you can drop to 400k minimum for the rest of the year. Before next year's renewal, bring it back up to 800k for 2 months. (asq)

**3. Can I use my pension income instead of the bank deposit?**
Yes. If you can document a regular monthly income of at least 65,000 THB (~$1,825) — pension, annuity, or investment income — you qualify via the income route without needing 800,000 THB in a Thai bank. Documentation must show 12 months of consistent payments. (issacompass+1)

**4. Is health insurance mandatory for the retirement visa?**
Yes, mandatory for the Non-O-A visa. The official minimums are 40,000 THB outpatient and 400,000 THB inpatient coverage. However, some embassies and immigration offices in 2026 are enforcing higher thresholds. Maintain a policy that clearly covers these minimums continuously. (benoit-partners+1)

**5. Can I work part-time in Thailand on a retirement visa?**
Absolutely not. Employment of any kind is strictly prohibited — paid or unpaid, for a Thai or foreign company. Even "informal" work can result in deportation and blacklisting. If you want to work, you need a work permit and a Non-B visa. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy)

**6. What happens if I leave Thailand for a holiday?**
If you exit without a re-entry permit, your 1-year extension is cancelled and you return on visa-exempt entry. Always buy a re-entry permit (single 1,000 THB or multiple 3,800 THB) before leaving Thailand. (thaiembassy)

**7. How early should I start the annual renewal process?**
Start 30–45 days before your current permission-to-stay expires. Most Pattaya agents recommend beginning the bank seasoning process at least 3 months ahead of your planned extension date. (pattaya4all)

**8. Can I do my 90-day report online?**
Yes. The TM47 online system at immigration.go.th accepts online reports from 15 days before to 7 days after your due date. In-person at Jomtien takes about 5 minutes if your paperwork is in order. (retiredinpattaya)

**9. Can I bring my foreign spouse to Thailand under this visa?**
Your spouse would need their own visa. A non-Thai spouse cannot be added as a dependent on a retirement visa. A Thai spouse may be relevant if you're also eligible for the Non-O Marriage route. **[verify]**

**10. What is the difference between a Non-O-A and a Non-O for retirement?**
The Non-O-A is applied for at a Thai Embassy abroad and grants 1 year permission to stay on entry. The Non-O (retirement basis) is also applied for abroad but initially grants 90 days; you then apply for a 1-year extension in Thailand. The end result is similar, but the Non-O-A is simpler for most retirees. The Non-O-A specifically requires health insurance; the Non-O extension in-country has less consistent enforcement. (benoit-partners+1)

**11. What if my health deteriorates and I can't get affordable insurance?**
This is the hardest practical challenge for retirees over 70. Insurance premiums escalate sharply with age and pre-existing conditions. Some insurers cap coverage at 75–80. Over-70 retirees in Pattaya often work with brokers specialising in high-age coverage; Bangkok Hospital and Bumrungrad's own insurance products are sometimes used. This is a common reason retirees switch to the Thailand Privilege visa (no insurance requirement). (issacompass+1)

**12. Can I convert my retirement visa to a marriage visa or LTR?**
Conversion to other visa types is possible in principle at Thai Immigration but varies by office. Most advisors recommend simply applying fresh for the new visa type rather than attempting in-country conversion. The LTR Wealthy Pensioner category is specifically designed for higher-income retirees and is worth considering at age 50+. (issacompass)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **Health insurance requirements increasingly enforced above official minimums:** Immigration offices and embassies in 2026 are applying more scrutiny to policy documents. Some posts now expect total coverage closer to 3,000,000 THB (combined in/outpatient) rather than the old 440,000 THB combined floor. **[verify current official minimum — this is an active area of change]** (chiangraitimes+1)
- **O-X insurance requirements updated:** The Non-O-X 5-year retirement visa now requires substantially higher coverage — outpatient minimum raised from 40,000 to 120,000 THB, inpatient from 400,000 to 3,000,000 THB — for the full 5-year duration. (cyprusconsulate)
- **Closer compliance checks at extensions:** Immigration offices in Pattaya and Bangkok are more diligently reviewing bank statements for the full 12 months, checking for dips below the 400,000 THB floor. Leniency that existed pre-2024 has been withdrawn. (isanrealestate)
- **Visa code consolidation (August 2025):** Thailand's consolidation from 17 to 7 non-immigrant visa categories affected naming conventions; the Non-O-A retirement route is preserved but may be referenced differently in official documentation. (thailand.prd+1)
- **Border run era effectively over:** Prior to 2024, some retirees managed extended stays via frequent border runs. Post-2025 enforcement caps visa-exempt entries at 2 per calendar year — this path is now closed. Annual extensions at Jomtien are the only viable long-term option. (geosthai)
- **TM6 paper arrival card abolished:** Digital arrival card (TDAC) now required pre-travel. Affects arrival experience for all visa types including Non-O-A. (visahq)
- **Combination financial method under inconsistent enforcement:** The income + deposit combination method (5c above) is officially valid but some immigration offices and embassies are becoming less flexible in how they calculate and verify the combination. **[verify]** (isanrealestate)
