## Cost analysis over 5 years

**DTV scenario (5 years of typical use, retiree-style stay):**
- Initial visa: 10,000 THB
- 180-day extensions in-country: 1,900 THB × ~5 = 9,500 THB
- **5-year total: ~20,000 THB (~$560)**

**Privilege Bronze scenario (if still available):**
- Initial fee: 650,000 THB
- Annual extension fees: 10,000 × 5 = 50,000 THB
- **5-year total: 700,000 THB (~$19,650)**

**Privilege Gold scenario:**
- Initial fee: 900,000 THB
- Annual extension fees: 10,000 × 5 = 50,000 THB
- **5-year total: 950,000 THB (~$26,650)**

**Cost premium for Privilege Gold over DTV: ~$26,000 USD over 5 years.**

## Who DTV is right for

- **Remote workers earning foreign income** (digital nomads, freelancers, employees of foreign companies)
- **People with foreign income but no special qualifications** (don't qualify for LTR)
- **Those willing to do extension paperwork** at Jomtien every 180 days
- **Cost-conscious long-stayers** who don't need premium concierge service
- **Soft-power participants** (Muay Thai students, Thai cooking school students, language school students with 6+ month enrolment)
- **People with frequent international travel** who'd otherwise pay for METV repeatedly

**Persona:** Sarah, 32, freelance designer earning $80k/year from US clients. Wants 3–4 months in Pattaya, then 2 months in Vietnam, repeated. DTV is her visa.

## Who Privilege (Elite) is right for

- **High-net-worth individuals** who want zero administrative hassle
- **Frequent travellers** who want airport VIP and concierge
- **People who can't get a Thai bank account easily** (DTV has banking problems; Privilege opens doors via EPL)
- **Those who want stay continuity beyond 360 days** (DTV requires exit after extension; Privilege doesn't)
- **Older retirees who can't get health insurance for O-A** but have 650k+ THB available
- **Those who value time over money** — Privilege saves 2–3 days/year of bureaucracy

**Persona:** David, 58, sold his business, has $2M net worth. Wants to live in Pattaya 8+ months/year, doesn't want to think about visas, willing to spend 1M THB once for that peace. Privilege Gold or Platinum.

## Hidden tradeoffs most articles miss

### DTV's unspoken weakness: bank account

Privilege holders walk into Bangkok Bank or Kasikorn with their Elite card and get an account. DTV holders are classified as "tourist" and routinely rejected. **If you need a Thai bank account, DTV makes life harder.** See [G6 Thai bank account guide](./G6-thai-bank-account.md).

### Privilege's unspoken weakness: no tax benefits

Privilege holders staying 180+ days/year become Thai tax residents. With the 2024 tax law change (foreign income remitted in same year is taxable), this can mean substantial Thai tax bills on income you brought in to spend. **Privilege has zero tax benefits — LTR is the smart-money alternative if you qualify.**

### The 360-day cycle on DTV

DTV stays max out at 180 days + 180-day extension = 360 days. After that, you must exit Thailand. For most users this is fine. For those who want absolute stay continuity, Privilege's "1 year per entry" rule wins.

### Bronze tier deadline

The Privilege Bronze tier (650k THB) had a deadline of 31 March 2026. As of April 2026, it may no longer be available — making Gold (900k THB) the entry tier. **Verify current availability with Thailand Privilege Card Co. before quoting prices.**

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Which is cheaper over 10 years?**
DTV: ~$1,100 over 10 years (assuming 2 visa renewals + extensions). Privilege Gold: ~$28,000. **DTV wins on raw cost by a factor of 25×.**

**2. Can I have both DTV and Privilege?**
You can hold either, not both simultaneously. You'd need to cancel one to get the other.

**3. Which is better for opening a Thai bank account?**
**Privilege.** DTV's "tourist" classification creates major banking obstacles in 2026.

**4. Which lets me work in Thailand?**
Neither. Both are non-work visas. Working locally requires Non-B + work permit or LTR-T/H/SMART.

**5. Which is better for high-income earners (>$80k/year)?**
**Neither — LTR is.** LTR offers tax benefits (foreign income exemption, 17% flat rate for skilled professionals) that beat both DTV and Privilege.

**6. Can I bring family?**
DTV: spouse + children under 20 as dependents. Privilege: only from Platinum tier (10-year/1.5M THB) and above.

**7. Which has airport fast-track?**
Privilege only (Suvarnabhumi and Phuket). DTV holders use standard immigration queues.

**8. If I'm a Pattaya retiree, should I get either of these?**
Probably not — **Non-O retirement (in-country path) is cheaper and simpler** if you're 50+ with 800k THB available. See [Non-O retirement page](../visas/retirement-non-o/) for details.
