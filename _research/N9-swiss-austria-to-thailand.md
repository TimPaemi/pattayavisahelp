## Country-specific notes

### Switzerland

- **Swiss police clearance:** Strafregisterauszug from Federal Office of Justice
- Cost: CHF 20; time 1–2 weeks
- **Apostille:** Hague Convention member; from cantonal authority
- **Pension:** AHV (1st pillar), Pillar 2/3 — taxable in Thailand if remitted; Swiss-Thai DTA provides credits
- **Banking:** Swiss banks cooperate with Thai retirement seasoning; Swiss franc to THB stable
- **Royal Thai Embassy Bern:** Kirchstrasse 56, 3097 Liebefeld

### Austria

- **Austrian police clearance:** Strafregisterbescheinigung from Bundesministerium für Inneres
- Cost: ~€15; time 1–2 weeks
- **Apostille:** Hague Convention member
- **Pension:** Austrian pension (Pensionsversicherung, Beamtenpension) — taxable in Thailand if remitted; Austrian-Thai DTA
- **Royal Thai Embassy Vienna:** Cottagegasse 48, 1180 Vienna

## Cost comparison vs Pattaya

Both Switzerland and Austria are high-cost countries; Pattaya delivers 50%+ savings on equivalent lifestyle.

## 6 FAQ questions

**1. Can Swiss/Austrian citizens get Non-O-X?**
**Yes** — both nationalities on the 14-country eligibility list.

**2. Pension taxation?**
Both countries have DTAs with Thailand. Swiss/Austrian pensions taxable in Thailand if remitted and Thai tax resident; home-country tax credits.

**3. Where do they live in Pattaya?**
Jomtien primarily; some Pratumnak. Often integrated with German community.

**4. Health insurance from home country?**
Swiss compulsory health insurance (Krankenkasse) limited coverage abroad; usually replaced by Thai or international policy. Same for Austrian system.

**5. Driving licence?**
Both Hague signatories; conversion at Banglamung DLT, no driving test.

**6. Currency and money transfer?**
CHF/EUR via Wise or SWIFT. Standard process; SWIFT preferred for retirement seasoning.
