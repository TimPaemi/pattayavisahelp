## What it is

The TM30 is Thailand's mandatory residence notification form — officially titled **"Notification from House-Master, Owner or the Possessor of the Residence where Alien has Stayed"**. It is a legal requirement under Section 38 of the Immigration Act B.E. 2522 (1979) that obliges the **owner, landlord, or manager of any accommodation** housing a foreigner to notify their local Immigration office within 24 hours of the foreigner's arrival.

The TM30 tells Thai Immigration precisely where each foreigner in Thailand is living at any given time — and in 2026, it has practical consequences that **directly affect every long-stay foreigner's ability to complete visa extensions, 90-day reports, and any other Immigration appointment**. (thaiembassy+4)

## Legal basis

- **Section 38 of the Immigration Act B.E. 2522 (1979):** Mandates that "house owners, heads of households, landlords, or managers of hotels who house foreign nationals staying in the Kingdom temporarily must notify the local Immigration authorities of such within 24 hours of the foreigner's arrival." (geosthai+1)
- The law has existed since 1979 but was only lightly enforced for decades. **Stricter enforcement began around 2019** and has continued to tighten annually. (thailand-elite+1)
- The TM30 is officially the **landlord's responsibility — not the tenant's**. However, a missing or incorrect TM30 is **the tenant's practical problem** at every Immigration appointment. (thailandlawonline+1)

## Who is responsible (and who bears the consequences)

### Responsible for filing TM30

The legal duty rests with the property owner, house-master, or manager — in Pattaya, this typically means:

- **Hotel or guesthouse management** (they handle TM30 automatically for guests) (visathailand+1)
- **Condo juristic office (building management)** — in Pattaya condos, the juristic office is often authorised to file on behalf of all unit owners and tenants (siam-legal+1)
- **Private landlord** (house or condo owner renting directly to you) (thaiembassy+1)
- **A subtenant** who is themselves leasing and sub-letting to a foreigner (visathailand)

**If you own your own condo in Thailand as a foreigner, you are responsible for filing TM30 yourself** — you are effectively your own "house-master". (thailandlawonline+1)

### Consequence of non-filing falls on the tenant

- The fine is technically levied on the landlord (**up to 800 THB per incident**) (thaiembassy)
- But the **tenant's visa extension, 90-day report, or any Immigration appointment will be blocked or refused** if a valid TM30 receipt is not on file (internationalcenter.bu.ac+1)
- **In practice:** you, the foreigner, need to ensure your TM30 is filed — even though it is not your direct legal obligation (thailandlawonline+1)

## The 24-hour rule

- TM30 must be filed **within 24 hours of the foreigner's arrival at the property** (rwtlaw.co+1)
- A new TM30 must be filed every time the foreigner:
  - Arrives for the first time at a property
  - **Returns to Thailand from abroad** — even to the same property (siam-legal+1)
  - Changes their accommodation address (siam-legal)
- **Staying at a hotel or resort** — the hotel handles TM30 automatically; you do not need to do anything (rwtlaw.co+1)
- **Staying at the same condo long-term without any international travel** — one TM30 filing covers the continuous stay; no need to refile (siam-legal)

## Three filing methods

### 1) Online (Section 38 App / Immigration extranet)

- The official online system for registered property owners is at: **extranet.immigration.go.th** (visathailand)
- The **Section 38 mobile app** is the newer, preferred method for landlords in 2025–2026 (geosthai+1)
- **Who can file online:**
  - Property owners registered on the Immigration extranet system (visathailand+1)
  - In Pattaya: most larger condo buildings' juristic offices now use the online system (siam-legal)
- **Steps:**
  1. Landlord/juristic logs into extranet.immigration.go.th or opens the Section 38 app (visathailand)
  2. Enters foreigner's details: name, passport number, nationality, entry date, address, and visa details (visathailand)
  3. Uploads copies of passport, visa, and property documentation (visathailand)
  4. Submits — system generates a TM30 receipt (downloadable PDF) (visathailand)
- **Online filing is free and takes approximately 10 minutes** if the system cooperates (visathailand)
- Common issue: Some juristic offices or landlords are **not registered for online filing** and must do it in person (visathailand)

### 2) In person at Immigration office

- Landlord (or authorised representative) visits **Jomtien Immigration** for Pattaya/Chonburi properties (visathailand)
- **Documents the landlord brings:**
  - Copy of landlord's Thai ID card or passport (siam-legal)
  - Copy of house registration book (Tabien Baan) or title deed (Chanote) (geosthai+1)
  - Official rental agreement (signed) (siam-legal)
  - Foreigner's passport copy (biodata, visa/stamp, entry stamp) — **tenant provides this to landlord** (thaiembassy+1)
- Officer stamps/issues the TM30 receipt (visathailand)
- Time: approximately 15–30 minutes depending on queue (visathailand)
- Cost: **Free** (visathailand)

### 3) By post

- Less common in 2026 but still technically possible
- Landlord sends signed TM30 form and supporting document copies to the local Immigration office by registered mail (thaiembassy)
- Receipts are returned by mail — slow and unreliable; not recommended for Pattaya-based long-stay residents who need the receipt quickly for appointments (thaiembassy)

## The TM30 receipt — why every long-stay foreigner must have one

The TM30 receipt is the **physical/digital confirmation that your residence has been properly reported**. You need it at:

- **Every 90-day TM47 report** at Jomtien (pcec+1)
- **Every visa extension application** (retirement, marriage, business, ED, Non-O, Non-B) (internationalcenter.bu.ac+1)
- Any address change notification
- Any visa conversion (TM.86/87)

**Jomtien practice in 2026:** Officers at Jomtien Immigration consistently request the TM30 receipt at extension appointments and increasingly at 90-day reports. **Turning up without one results in being turned away** — a common source of frustration for new arrivals. (asq.in+3)

**What the receipt looks like:** A stamped paper form or printed PDF showing:

- Your name and passport number
- Property address
- Arrival date
- Immigration office stamp and date (visathailand)

## Penalty for non-compliance

| Who | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Landlord / property owner (fails to file within 24 hours) | Fine up to 800 THB per incident (thaiembassy+1) |
| Tenant (direct legal penalty) | None — TM30 is the landlord's obligation (thailandlawonline) |
| Practical consequence for tenant | Extension/report refused at Immigration until TM30 is filed and receipt obtained (geosthai+1) |

The 800 THB fine on the landlord is low — but **the practical enforcement is through the tenant's visa process**, which is why landlords and property managers in Pattaya are increasingly aware of the obligation. (thailandlawonline+1)

## Do I need to re-file every time I re-enter Thailand?

**Yes — technically, a new TM30 must be filed every time you re-enter Thailand**, even returning to the same property. This is because each re-entry generates a new arrival number, and the TM30 is linked to that arrival record. (rwtlaw.co+1)

**In practice:**

- **Large condo buildings with active juristic offices in Pattaya** typically refile TM30 automatically when notified of a tenant's return — many have WhatsApp groups specifically for this
- **Private landlords or smaller buildings** may not know to refile — you must remind or instruct them
- **Hotels** handle this automatically for guests (rwtlaw.co)

## The Pattaya condo reality

Pattaya has thousands of condo units occupied by foreign long-stay residents, across buildings ranging from self-managed juristic offices to absentee foreign owner rentals. **The TM30 reality varies significantly:**

**Buildings that handle TM30 automatically:**

- Most major condo developments along Jomtien Beach, Pratumnak, and North Pattaya with active juristic offices and high foreigner occupancy (e.g., View Talay complexes, Centara, Intercontinental-managed projects)
- The juristic office has an extranet/Section 38 login and files online for all tenants on arrival (siam-legal)

**Buildings that expect tenants to handle it:**

- Smaller buildings or privately owned units rented directly by Thai owner-landlords
- Short-term holiday rental condos where the owner lives overseas
- Any building where the juristic office has no online access or has never registered (thaiembassy+1)

**What to do if your landlord refuses or doesn't know how:**

1. **Politely explain** that it is their legal obligation and it directly affects your ability to extend your visa
2. **Offer to accompany them** to Jomtien Immigration in person to file it together — officers will help
3. **Use a proxy:** If you own the property, you can file it yourself (you are the house-master)
4. **Engage a visa agent:** Many Pattaya agents handle TM30 filing as a standalone service (typical fee: 500–1,500 THB)
5. **Pay the landlord's 800 THB fine at Immigration directly** if they failed to file and a receipt is urgently needed — some offices allow this during an extension appointment (thaiembassy)

## What to provide to your landlord (tenant's side)

Even though the TM30 is the landlord's job, **you need to give them:**

1. Copy of your passport biodata page
2. Copy of your current visa or extension stamp
3. Copy of your latest entry stamp
4. Copy of your **TDAC digital arrival card confirmation** (from 2025 onwards, replacing TM6) (kidsenglishthailand+1)

**Prepare these copies in advance and give them to your landlord or juristic office on the day of every arrival** — don't wait to be asked.

## 12 real FAQ questions

**1. Is TM30 my responsibility or my landlord's?**
Legally, the TM30 is your landlord's responsibility to file within 24 hours of your arrival. However, if it's not filed, **Immigration will still refuse your extension or 90-day report**. You must ensure your landlord does it — practically, it's your problem even though it's not your legal duty. (thailandlawonline+1)

**2. My condo juristic office says it's automatic — do I need to check?**
Yes, **verify**. Ask the juristic office to show you a copy of the TM30 receipt after each re-entry from abroad. Some buildings have the process but inconsistently apply it. **Having a copy of your receipt removes all doubt.** (geosthai+1)

**3. I just got back from a trip to Singapore — does a new TM30 need to be filed?**
Yes — every re-entry into Thailand triggers a new TM30 requirement, even returning to the same address. Your arrival number changes, which requires a new TM30 filing. **Inform your juristic office or landlord on the day you return.** (rwtlaw.co+1)

**4. Do I need TM30 if I'm staying in a hotel?**
No — hotels file TM30 automatically as part of their standard guest registration. You have no action required when staying at a hotel, guesthouse, or resort. (rwtlaw.co+1)

**5. I'm a foreigner who owns my own condo in Thailand — who files TM30 for me?**
You are the house-master — **you must file TM30 yourself** each time you re-enter Thailand and stay at your own property. Register for online access via the Immigration extranet or Section 38 app, or file in person at Jomtien. (thailandlawonline+1)

**6. What happens at Jomtien if I don't have a TM30 receipt?**
You will typically be turned away and told to come back with a valid TM30 receipt. Officers at Jomtien are known to consistently ask for TM30 at extension appointments. Some offices allow you to file TM30 on the spot, but **don't count on this — get it before your appointment**. (asq.in+2)

**7. My landlord lives overseas and won't respond — what do I do?**
Contact a local Pattaya visa agent who offers TM30 filing as a service. They can often handle it with the juristic office or by filing on behalf of an owner under a proxy arrangement. Alternatively, if you are the registered occupant/sub-leaseholder, you may be able to file yourself at Jomtien in some circumstances. (thaiembassy)

**8. How do I get a copy of my TM30 receipt?**
Your landlord or juristic office should provide you with a copy when they file online (downloadable PDF from extranet) or give you the stamped paper receipt from the in-person filing. **Always request a copy and keep it with your passport.** (geosthai+1)

**9. Do I need a new TM30 every time I change condo within Pattaya?**
Yes — moving to a new address requires a new TM30 at the new property's address. Your new landlord or juristic office must file it within 24 hours of your move-in date. (siam-legal)

**10. Can my visa agent file TM30 for me?**
Yes — many Pattaya visa agents handle TM30 as a standalone service or as part of a visa extension package. They act as a proxy for the landlord and file at Jomtien, **typically for a fee of 500–1,500 THB**. (visathailand)

**11. The juristic office charges me 200–500 THB to file TM30 — is that normal?**
Some Pattaya condo buildings charge small administrative fees for this service. It is not an official government fee (TM30 filing itself is free) but it is common practice and generally acceptable. (thaiembassy+1)

**12. Is TM30 the same as 90-day reporting (TM47)?**
**No — they are completely different.**
- **TM30:** the landlord's address notification (filed when you arrive or move in)
- **TM47:** YOUR 90-day report to Immigration confirming your continued presence (filed every 90 days by you)
Both are required for long-stay residents. (thaiembassy+1)

## 2025–2026 enforcement changes

- **Section 38 mobile app launched:** The new official Section 38 app simplifies online TM30 filing for registered property owners, replacing the older and notoriously glitchy web portal. **Adoption is increasing in Pattaya's larger buildings.** (geosthai+1)
- **Cross-checking TM30 with TM47 at Jomtien:** As of 2025–2026, Jomtien officers more consistently cross-reference TM30 records with TM47 reports, **flagging discrepancies between declared address and TM30 on file**. (kidsenglishthailand+1)
- **TDAC arrival data integration:** The digital arrival card (TDAC, replacing TM6) creates a cleaner link between arrival records and TM30 filings — making it easier for Immigration to identify cases where no TM30 was filed post-arrival. (kidsenglishthailand+1)
- **Strict at Jomtien, patchy elsewhere:** Enforcement is highly consistent at Jomtien and Bangkok Immigration offices; smaller provincial offices may be more relaxed, but **Pattaya-area residents cannot rely on lax enforcement**. (pcec+1)
- **Increased awareness among Pattaya condo buildings:** Following the 2019 enforcement push and continued tightening, most well-managed Pattaya condominium developments now have a working TM30 system in place. **The persistent problem area is privately rented units and absentee foreign owner-landlords.** (siam-legal+1)
