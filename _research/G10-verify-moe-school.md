## Why this matters in 2026

Between May 2025 and early 2026, **Thailand revoked nearly 10,000 student visas** in the largest crackdown in the country's ED-visa history. Many of those revoked visas belonged to students who had paid "ghost schools" — institutions that existed on paper but ran no real classes — and never attended a single hour of education.

The crackdown was triggered by:

- A 2024–2025 Ministry of Higher Education review of ED visa abuse
- Discovery of widespread non-compliant programs and shell institutions
- Implementation of monthly attendance reporting requirements (effective May 14, 2025)
- Cross-referencing of attendance data with Immigration records

**The 2026 reality:** the only safe way to get and keep an ED visa is to enrol at a genuinely MOE-accredited institution that actually runs classes, tracks attendance, and reports to the Ministry. Anything less puts your visa, time, and money at risk.

## What "MOE-accredited" actually means

The Thai **Ministry of Education (MOE)** is the only authority that licenses schools to issue documents supporting ED visa applications. An MOE license means:

- The school is registered as an officially recognised educational institution
- The school's curriculum has been reviewed and approved
- The school is authorised to issue enrolment letters that the Thai Immigration Bureau will accept
- The school has reporting obligations (attendance, student status updates, monthly compliance) to the MOE
- The school is subject to MOE inspections

**Without MOE accreditation:**

- The school **cannot legally issue ED visa documentation**
- Any "ED visa enrolment letter" from a non-MOE school is fraudulent
- Students using such documentation face visa revocation, deportation, and re-entry bans

## How to verify a school's MOE license

### Step 1 — Ask the school directly for proof

Ask any school you're considering for:

1. **MOE registration certificate** — physical document or digital copy
2. **Registration certificate number** — a unique identifier
3. **License date and expiry** — confirm the license is currently active

A legitimate school will show this without hesitation. **A school that refuses or stalls is a red flag.**

### Step 2 — Cross-check with the Ministry

The MOE maintains records of registered schools. Verification options:

- **MOE website:** moe.go.th — has searchable databases for various school types **[verify current portal]**
- **Direct MOE inquiry:** Email or call the relevant MOE department to verify a school's registration
- **Provincial education office:** Local education offices in Chonburi (for Pattaya schools) can confirm registration

### Step 3 — Verify with Immigration

The Immigration Bureau and MOE share data systems. While Immigration won't typically pre-verify a school for you, you can:

- Ask any visa agent who handles ED applications regularly — they know which Pattaya schools are MOE-current
- Check whether the school has a track record of successful ED visa approvals (not just enrolments)

### Step 4 — Check for monthly attendance reporting

A school that is genuinely MOE-compliant will:

- Track and record student attendance daily
- Submit monthly attendance reports to MOE/Immigration via the electronic compliance system
- Have a clear process for the 80% attendance threshold (required for visa extensions)

Ask: **"How do you track attendance, and how often do you report to Immigration?"**

A school that can't answer this clearly is non-compliant.

## Red flags — schools to avoid

The 10,000+ visa revocations targeted schools displaying these red flags:

### 1. Won't show their MOE certificate

If a school can't or won't produce its MOE registration on request, **walk away**. Legitimate schools display their certificate prominently.

### 2. "Guarantees" your visa

Phrases like "100% visa approval" or "guaranteed approval" are sales tactics that **legitimate MOE schools don't use**. Visa approval is decided by Immigration, not the school. A school that claims to guarantee approval is suggesting a non-compliant route.

### 3. No physical campus or classes

If you can't visit the school in person, see classrooms, meet teachers, or sit in on a class — it's likely a shell institution. **MOE schools must run real classes.**

### 4. No attendance tracking

If a school says "you don't need to attend, just enrol" — they're operating non-compliantly. The 80% attendance threshold is mandatory and reported monthly.

### 5. Advertises only on visa-tourism sites

Legitimate schools have an educational mission. Schools that exist only to issue ED visas (and advertise primarily on visa agency sites or expat forums with "easy ED visa" promotions) are suspect.

### 6. Asks for full payment via cash, crypto, or untraceable methods

Reputable schools accept normal payment methods (bank transfer, credit card, official receipt). Cash-only or crypto-only payment requirements are red flags.

### 7. Promises courses below 6 months

For Muay Thai ED specifically, the course must be at least 6 months. Schools offering "3-month ED visa packages" are misrepresenting the visa rules.

### 8. Has been on official MOE crackdown lists

The Ministry has published lists of schools whose registrations were revoked or suspended. Cross-check before paying.

## Green flags — schools that are likely legitimate

### 1. Long operating history

Schools that have been operating for 5+ years with consistent MOE accreditation have proven track records. New schools aren't necessarily bad, but additional verification is wise.

### 2. Clear MOE certificate display

Certificates posted on websites, in offices, and willingly shown on request. The school is proud of its accreditation.

### 3. Real campus, real teachers, real students

You can visit, attend a trial class, meet teachers, see other students attending classes. Look for evidence of actual education happening.

### 4. Public attendance reporting

The school clearly explains the 80% attendance rule and has a process for tracking and reporting to MOE.

### 5. References from past students

Genuine schools have students who have completed courses and obtained ED visa approvals through legitimate paths. References are easy to find.

### 6. Realistic pricing

Tuition at MOE-accredited Pattaya language schools typically falls in the range of **30,000–80,000 THB for 12 months** (Thai language) or **120,000–135,000 THB for 12 months** (Muay Thai package including visa support). Prices significantly below or above this range warrant additional questions.

### 7. Established website, business registration, and online presence

Real schools have professional websites, are listed in business directories, and have a public reputation. Schools with no online presence beyond a single Facebook page are suspect.

### 8. Clear curriculum and learning outcomes

Real schools can describe what you'll learn, how lessons are structured, what exams or assessments exist. Vague answers ("you'll just attend whenever") are red flags.

## Pattaya school landscape (categories, not endorsements)

**Pattaya has a strong concentration of legitimate MOE-accredited schools** because of its established expat population. Common school types:

### Thai language schools

- Several long-established institutions on Sukhumvit Road and in central Pattaya
- Typically offer 12-month courses suitable for ED visa holders
- Class formats: 4-hour weekday morning sessions are common
- Tuition: 30,000–80,000 THB/year
- Most have 5+ years operating history with MOE accreditation

### English language schools

- Multiple MOE-licensed English schools serving local Thai students plus foreign learners
- Some offer ED visa support for foreign students learning English (less common than Thai language schools, but legitimate)
- Tuition varies widely; verify accreditation specifically

### Muay Thai camps with MOE licenses

- A subset of Pattaya Muay Thai gyms hold MOE school licenses
- These camps offer "Thai language and culture + Muay Thai" combined curricula to satisfy MOE structure requirements
- Real camps, real training, real attendance tracking
- Cost: 120,000–135,000 THB for 12 months including visa support
- **Critical:** Most Muay Thai gyms in Pattaya are NOT MOE-licensed. Always verify before paying.

### Other MOE-accredited categories (less common)

- Cooking schools
- Massage / wellness schools
- Cultural / Thai dance schools

These are rarer in Pattaya but exist. The same verification process applies.

## What to do if you've already paid a fake school

If you've enrolled at what turns out to be a non-MOE school:

### If you haven't yet applied for the ED visa

- **Stop the process immediately.** Don't submit an application using fake documentation.
- Try to recover your tuition (often difficult — schools that operate fraudulently rarely refund)
- Find a legitimate MOE-accredited school as quickly as possible
- Consult a visa lawyer if substantial money is at stake

### If you already have an ED visa from a fake school

- Your visa is at high risk of revocation
- Some students caught in the 2025 crackdown received deportation orders within weeks
- Options:
  - **Voluntary withdrawal:** Cancel the visa and apply for another visa type that fits your situation (DTV, marriage, retirement)
  - **Quick switch to legitimate school:** Some students transferred to MOE-accredited schools within the legal grace window — verify with an immigration lawyer if this is still possible
  - **Departure and re-entry plan:** Leave Thailand voluntarily before forced removal; reduce risk of blacklisting

### If you receive a revocation notice

- Contact a Pattaya immigration lawyer immediately
- Do not ignore the notice — overstays after revocation can result in immediate detention
- You typically have a brief window (often 7 days) to depart voluntarily before enforcement actions

## Common questions and clarifications

### Does MOE accreditation guarantee my ED visa will be approved?

**No.** MOE accreditation is the school's qualification, not yours. Your visa approval depends on:

- Your own eligibility (passport, finances, no overstays)
- Your enrolment being genuine (you actually attend)
- Your application meeting all Immigration requirements

But: **without MOE accreditation, your visa cannot be issued legally.** It's a necessary condition, not a sufficient one.

### Are online courses MOE-accredited for ED visas?

**No.** As of 2025, online-only courses are no longer accepted for ED visa purposes. The visa requires in-person, on-campus study at a physical MOE-accredited school. This was a major 2025 change tied to the crackdown.

### Can I switch schools mid-visa?

In principle yes, but it's a multi-step process: cancel current visa, transfer enrolment (subject to MOE approval), apply for new ED visa documentation. Often easier to complete the current course or wait until renewal.

### What happens at MOE inspections?

Inspectors visit MOE-accredited schools periodically to verify:

- Real classes are running
- Attendance records are accurate
- Students physically attend
- Curriculum matches what's registered

Schools that fail inspections lose accreditation. Students enrolled at the time may face complications.

## 12 FAQ questions

**1. How can I tell if a school is really MOE-accredited?**
Ask for the MOE registration certificate (physical document or digital copy with registration number). Cross-check with the MOE website or a Pattaya visa agent who handles ED applications regularly. **Schools that refuse to show their certificate are red flags.**

**2. What was the 2025 crackdown about?**
The Thai Ministry of Higher Education ordered immigration to revoke visas for students enrolled in non-compliant programs effective May 14, 2025. Approximately **10,000 student visas were cancelled** through 2025 and into 2026. Schools that failed to comply with monthly attendance reporting lost accreditation; their students lost visas.

**3. What's the attendance requirement for ED visas?**
**80% attendance** is the standard requirement for visa extension. Schools track and report attendance monthly via an electronic system to MOE/Immigration.

**4. Can I get an ED visa for a 3-month Muay Thai camp?**
**No.** ED visa courses must be at least 6 months. Schools offering "3-month ED packages" are not operating legitimately.

**5. Are all Pattaya Muay Thai gyms MOE-licensed?**
**No.** Only a subset of Muay Thai gyms hold MOE school licenses. Most don't — they're sports facilities, not educational institutions. **Always verify before paying for an ED visa package.**

**6. What's a "ghost school"?**
A school that exists on paper to issue ED visa documentation but runs no real classes. No physical campus, no teachers, no students attending. The 2025 crackdown specifically targeted these. **Visa documents from ghost schools are worthless and dangerous.**

**7. How do I report a fake school?**
Contact the Thai Ministry of Education or local provincial education office. Reports help protect future students. The Immigration Bureau also accepts reports of fraudulent visa documentation.

**8. Are language schools and Muay Thai camps treated the same way?**
Yes — both must hold MOE school licenses to support ED visa applications. Curriculum format differs (language vs Muay Thai + Thai language/culture), but the MOE accreditation requirement is identical.

**9. Can I do online ED visa courses in 2026?**
**No.** Online-only courses are not accepted. ED visa requires in-person attendance at a physical MOE-accredited school.

**10. How much should a legitimate Pattaya MOE-accredited school charge?**
**Thai language schools:** 30,000–80,000 THB for 12 months. **Muay Thai ED packages (including visa support):** 120,000–135,000 THB for 12 months. Significantly below this range = suspect; significantly above = compare alternatives.

**11. What documents should a legitimate school provide me?**
A genuine MOE school provides: (1) Enrolment letter on school letterhead, (2) Copy of school's MOE registration certificate, (3) Tuition receipt showing 50%+ paid, (4) MOE approval letter (after the 2–4 week review process). All of these are required for the ED visa application.

**12. I'm visa-shopping in Pattaya — what's the safest path?**
Visit the school in person before paying. Sit in on a class. Meet teachers. See other students. Ask for the MOE certificate. Cross-check with a Pattaya visa agent or the PCEC for community references. **Pay only after physical verification.**

## 2025–2026 changes affecting MOE accreditation

- **May 14, 2025:** Ministry of Higher Education ordered immigration to revoke visas for non-compliant ED programs. Mass revocations begin.
- **August 2025:** Approximately 10,000 student visas revoked (cumulative through August).
- **Monthly attendance reporting required:** Schools must file attendance reports via electronic system.
- **Online courses excluded:** No longer accepted as ED visa basis (effective 2025).
- **Embassy verification:** Embassies now actively verify school credentials at the visa application stage, not just immigration.
- **DTV cross-impact:** DTV applications are also being checked against school accreditation when "soft power" justification is claimed.
- **Ongoing monthly enforcement:** Crackdown continues into 2026; schools that fail reports face accreditation revocation.
