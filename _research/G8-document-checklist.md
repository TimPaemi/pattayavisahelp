## Universal documents (required for almost every Thai visa)

These are the foundation documents you need regardless of visa type. Get these in order first; everything else is visa-specific.

### Passport

- **Validity:** At least 6 months remaining from intended entry date
- **Blank pages:** At least 1 blank page (2+ recommended for some applications)
- **Condition:** No water damage, no torn pages, no markings or alterations on the biodata page
- **Recent renewal:** If your passport was renewed recently and you have stamps in the old one, **carry both passports** when entering Thailand

### Photos

- **Standard size for embassy applications:** 3.5 × 4.5 cm (35 × 45 mm)
- **Visa on Arrival size:** 4 × 6 cm (40 × 60 mm)
- **Number required:** Typically 2 photos for embassy applications; 3 for some retirement visa types
- **Background:** White (some embassies accept light blue — verify per post)
- **Age of photo:** Taken within the last 6 months — strictly enforced at multiple embassies
- **Expression:** Neutral, mouth closed, **no smiling**
- **Glasses:** No tinted glasses; clear-frame glasses generally acceptable but eyes must be visible
- **Head coverage:** Religious headcoverings allowed if face fully visible
- **Head height:** ~70% of total photo height for e-Visa applications

### Application form

- **Embassy applications:** Form provided by the relevant embassy/consulate; some posts use the e-Visa portal at thaievisa.go.th
- **In-country extensions:** TM.7 form (available at Immigration office or print in advance)
- **In-country conversions:** TM.86 (from tourist visa) or TM.87 (from visa-exempt entry)
- **90-day reports:** TM.47
- **Re-entry permits:** TM.8

### Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)

- **Mandatory** for all foreign arrivals since 2024 (replaces the old paper TM6)
- Submit online at **tdac.immigration.go.th** within 72 hours before flight
- Airlines increasingly check TDAC before boarding
- Free to submit

## Photocopies — what gets copied vs originals

**Universal rule:** Bring originals AND photocopies. Officials typically inspect originals, take photocopies of relevant pages, and return originals.

**Standard photocopy stack** for any Immigration appointment:

1. Passport biodata page
2. Current visa or extension stamp page
3. Latest entry stamp page
4. TDAC arrival confirmation
5. Re-entry permit page (if applicable)
6. Previous 90-day report slip (if applicable)
7. TM30 receipt
8. **All copies must be signed** in pen by the applicant

**Where to get photocopies:**

- Photocopy shops just outside Jomtien Immigration: 1–2 THB per page
- Most hotels and condo offices will copy for you
- 7-Elevens occasionally have copying services
- **Bring your own copies** to avoid delays at counters

## Visa-specific documents

### Tourist Visa (TR) / Visa Exemption

- Universal docs above
- Onward/return flight ticket
- Hotel/accommodation booking
- Bank statement showing 20,000 THB / 40,000 THB per family (cash check enforcement)
- TDAC submitted before boarding

### Destination Thailand Visa (DTV)

- Universal docs above
- 6-month bank statement showing 500,000 THB minimum balance, seasoned 3+ months
- Proof of legal residence in country of application
- Proof of remote work / soft power activity / talent qualification
- For Workcation: employment contract, payslips, freelance portfolio (some embassies)
- For Soft Power: enrolment letter from MOE-licensed Thai institution (6+ months)
- For Talent: skill credentials and references
- Dependents: marriage/birth certificates

### Non-O-A Retirement Visa (1-year, embassy)

- Universal docs above (3 copies of application form at some embassies)
- 18-month passport validity (more than the standard 6 months)
- 800,000 THB Thai bank deposit certificate (or 65,000 THB/month income proof) seasoned 2+ months
- Bank guarantee letter confirming deposit
- **Health insurance certificate** (40,000 THB OPD / 400,000 THB IPD minimum) — mandatory
- Police clearance certificate from home country
- Medical certificate from licensed doctor in home country (no prohibited diseases)

### Non-O Retirement Visa (in-country path)

- Universal docs above
- Initial 90-day Non-O from embassy or in-country conversion (TM.86/87)
- 800,000 THB Thai bank deposit, seasoned 2+ months at Jomtien
- Bank passbook (original) + bank letter dated same day or previous day of appointment
- TM30 accommodation receipt
- Rental agreement or utility bills (proof of address)
- TM.7 extension form
- **No health insurance required** for in-country extension

### Non-O-X Retirement (10-year)

- Universal docs above
- 18-month passport validity
- 3,000,000 THB Thai bank deposit certificate (or 1.8M + qualifying income)
- Health insurance covering 5 years (commonly 120,000 THB OPD / 3,000,000 THB IPD)
- Police clearance certificate from home country
- Medical certificate
- Personal biography / CV
- Eligible nationality (one of 14 specific countries)

### Non-O Marriage Visa

- Universal docs above
- Thai marriage certificate (Kor Ror 2 + Kor Ror 3)
- For foreign marriages: legalised + translated + Amphoe-registered (Kor Ror 22) — see G9 guide
- Thai spouse's ID card and house registration (Tabien Baan)
- Thai spouse's passport
- 400,000 THB Thai bank deposit, seasoned 2+ months at Jomtien
- Bank book + bank letter
- **5–10 photos of couple together** (inside home, family events, daily life)
- Map / hand-drawn directions to your residence
- TM.7 extension form
- TM30 receipt
- Affirmation of Freedom to Marry (if married in Thailand — issued by your home country's embassy)
- **Spouse must attend** the extension appointment in person

### Education Visa (ED)

- Universal docs above
- MOE approval letter (issued 2–4 weeks after enrolment)
- Enrolment letter on school letterhead
- Copy of school's MOE registration certificate
- Proof of tuition payment (50%+ paid)
- Criminal background check (for some nationalities — UK, US)
- TM30 (if applying in-country)
- For extension: 70–80% attendance record signed by school
- Current student ID

### Non-B Business Visa + Work Permit

- Universal docs above
- WP.32 pre-approval letter from Department of Employment
- Employment contract from Thai employer
- Cover letter from employer to Royal Thai Embassy
- Educational certificates / degree transcripts
- Medical certificate
- 20,000 THB / 40,000 THB family fund proof
- **Employer documents:** Company registration (DBD), shareholder list (BOJ 5), VAT registration (PP 20), corporate tax return (PND 50), VAT monthly (PP 30), social security records (4:1 ratio compliance), company profile, office photos and map, employment agreement
- Work permit (separate application, after Non-B issued and arrival in Thailand)

### LTR Visa (all categories)

- Universal docs above
- Recent passport-sized photograph
- Criminal background check (typically 3-month validity)
- Health insurance certificate (USD 50,000 minimum, **clearly stated in English**) OR Thai social security OR USD 100,000 deposit
- Bank statement / financial certificate
- **Category-specific:**
  - LTR-W: Asset certificate (USD 1M+), 2-year income evidence, USD 500k Thai investment proof
  - LTR-P: Age 50+ proof, pension/income evidence, USD 250k Thai investment (if reduced income route)
  - LTR-T: Employment contract, employer USD 150M+ revenue verification, 2-year income proof
  - LTR-H: Thai employer offer in targeted industry, BOI promotion certificate, degree, 5-year experience proof

### SMART Visa

- Universal docs above
- BOI Letter of Qualification Endorsement
- **Category-specific:**
  - SMART-T: Employment contract in targeted industry, qualifications, salary proof
  - SMART-I: 20M THB investment evidence
  - SMART-E: Executive contract, 10-year experience proof, degree, 200k THB/month salary, 600k THB deposit
  - SMART-S: Incubator/accelerator participation OR company incorporation + 600k THB deposit + 25% ownership
  - SMART-O: Marriage/birth certificate

### Thailand Privilege (Elite)

- Colour scan of passport biodata page
- Recent colour photograph (white background)
- Privilege application form
- PDPA consent form
- For under-20: parental authorisation letter
- For Platinum+ family additions: marriage/birth certificate
- **No notarisation. No police clearance. No medical certificate. No bank statements.**

### Non-M Media Visa

- Universal docs above
- Official letter from media entity to MFA Director-General
- Applicant CV
- Company profile of media entity
- Proof of media entity registration (6+ months)
- Employment contract
- Criminal clearance from country of permanent residence
- Portfolio of 10–12 most recent media reports
- Bank statement (30,000 THB single-entry / 60,000 THB multiple-entry)

## Documents that need legalisation

Some documents must be **legalised** (authenticated by both your home country and the Thai MFA) before Immigration accepts them. Common cases:

1. **Foreign marriage certificates** — required for Non-O Marriage. See G9 for country-by-country chain.
2. **Foreign birth certificates** — for dependent applications
3. **Foreign police clearance certificates** — for Non-O-A, Non-O-X, LTR
4. **Foreign academic degrees** — for some Non-B / LTR-H applications

**Important note:** Thailand is **NOT a signatory to the Hague Apostille Convention.** This means:

- Apostille from your home country alone is **not sufficient**
- After your home country's authentication (apostille or legalisation), the document must also be:
  - Certified by the **Royal Thai Embassy in your home country**, OR
  - After arrival in Thailand, certified by your country's embassy in Bangkok, then legalised by the **Thai Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA)**
- Translations into Thai must be done by a certified translator (typically MFA-registered)

**Allow 4–8 weeks** for the full legalisation chain.

## Translation requirements

- **Documents not in Thai or English** typically need translation
- **Foreign-language documents for Thai authorities:** translated to Thai by certified translator
- **Thai-language documents for foreign use:** translated to English by certified translator
- **MFA-certified translators:** searchable on the Thai MFA website
- **Pattaya translation services:** Multiple agencies offering certified translation; typical fee 500–2,000 THB per document depending on length

## The "always bring extras" rule — 5 rules for documents at any Immigration appointment

1. **Bring more copies than you think you need.** 2 copies of each is the safe minimum; 3+ for marriage extensions where multiple counters may need them.
2. **Sign every photocopy in pen.** Not pencil. Not initials. Full signature.
3. **Bring originals AND copies.** Officials check originals, keep copies.
4. **Have photos extra.** 2–4 spare photos in your bag for forms that surprise you.
5. **Bring cash.** Government fees are cash-only in baht. Bring exact change where possible.

## Common pitfalls

1. **Photocopies not signed.** Many counters reject unsigned photocopies. Sign every copy in pen.
2. **Photos taken more than 6 months ago.** Strictly enforced at multiple embassies. Get fresh photos before applying.
3. **Foreign marriage cert without full legalisation.** Apostille alone isn't enough — Thailand isn't in the Hague Convention. Need MFA legalisation chain.
4. **Bank letter older than the day before.** Some Jomtien officers reject bank letters more than 1 day old. Get it the morning of your appointment.
5. **Medical certificate from wrong type of doctor.** Some embassies require medical council–stamped certificates. Confirm requirements with your specific embassy.
6. **Police clearance expired.** National-level checks (FBI, ACPO, ACIC) take 4–8 weeks to obtain — start early. Many are valid only 3–6 months.
7. **Passport renewed recently with stamps in old book.** Carry both passports until you transfer the stamps at Immigration or finish your current extension.
8. **Wrong photo size.** 3.5×4.5 vs 4×6 vs digital format requirements vary. Verify exact spec before printing.

## 12 FAQ questions

**1. What's the most common reason visa applications get rejected for document issues?**
**Outdated photos** (more than 6 months old) and **incomplete bank seasoning** documentation are the top two. Both are easy to avoid with planning.

**2. Do I need to translate my home country documents into Thai?**
For foreign-language documents (marriage certificates, birth certificates, police clearances), yes — translation to Thai by a certified translator is typically required. English-language documents from the UK, US, Australia, etc. may be accepted as-is at some posts but should be translated for Immigration applications in Thailand.

**3. Where do I get a TM.7 extension form?**
Free at Jomtien Immigration on arrival. You can also download and print it in advance from the Thai Immigration website.

**4. Can I download the TDAC on the day of arrival?**
TDAC must be submitted within 72 hours before flight. Most travellers complete it the day before or morning of departure. Submit at tdac.immigration.go.th.

**5. How many photocopies should I bring to Jomtien?**
Minimum 2 of each relevant page. For marriage extensions, 3+ as multiple counters may need them. **Bring your own** — photocopy shop next to the office charges 1–2 THB per page if you forget.

**6. What size photo do I need for a Thai visa?**
Standard embassy applications: 3.5 × 4.5 cm. Visa on Arrival: 4 × 6 cm. White background. No smiling. Taken within 6 months.

**7. Is apostille enough for foreign documents in Thailand?**
**No.** Thailand isn't a Hague Apostille signatory. After your home country's authentication, you need either Royal Thai Embassy certification (in your home country) or Thai MFA legalisation (in Thailand). See G9 for the full chain.

**8. Do I need a medical certificate for the Non-O retirement (in-country) extension?**
No — the Non-O in-country path doesn't require a medical certificate. Only the embassy-applied Non-O-A and Non-O-X require this.

**9. How long is a Thai police clearance valid?**
The Thai check (immigration history) doesn't expire as part of the application. Foreign police clearances (FBI, ACPO, ACIC) are typically valid 3–6 months from issue date for visa applications.

**10. Can I use scanned documents instead of originals at Jomtien?**
No — Immigration requires originals plus photocopies. Officers inspect originals, retain photocopies. Bring everything.

**11. What's the cheapest way to get certified translations in Pattaya?**
Multiple translation agencies in Pattaya central and Jomtien offer certified translation: typical 500–2,000 THB per document. MFA-recognised translators may charge slightly more. Compare 2–3 agencies before committing.

**12. If I lose my passport, what do I do?**
Report loss immediately to local police (file a police report), then to your home country's embassy in Bangkok for a replacement passport. With the new passport, visit Jomtien Immigration to transfer your visa stamps and update records. Allow 1–4 weeks total.

## 2025–2026 changes affecting documentation

- **TDAC mandatory since 2024** — paper TM6 abolished
- **Stricter photo enforcement** — multiple embassies rejecting >6-month-old photos
- **Bank letter same-day enforcement** at Jomtien for retirement extensions (more strict in 2025–2026)
- **Tax Identification Number** increasingly requested for bank account opening (CRS/FATCA compliance)
- **MFA legalisation appointments required since 2025** — wait can be 2–4 weeks; agents can expedite
- **Digital cross-checking** — TM30, TM47, TDAC, and bank account data now cross-referenced across systems
