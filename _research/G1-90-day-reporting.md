## What it is and who it applies to

Thailand's 90-day reporting rule requires most foreign nationals staying in Thailand longer than 90 consecutive days to report their current address to Immigration every 90 days, using **Form TM.47**. The rule applies regardless of visa type — business, retirement, marriage, ED, Non-B, LTR, etc. — with the main exceptions being permanent residents and some special categories.

**If you leave Thailand before you hit 90 consecutive days, your reporting clock resets on re-entry and you do not owe a report for that stay.** (pcec+4)

## Legal basis

- The 90-day reporting requirement comes from **Section 37(5) of the Immigration Act B.E. 2522**, which obliges foreigners staying in Thailand to report their current address at specified intervals. (thaiembassy+1)
- Thai Immigration's official description: "Notification of staying in the Kingdom over 90 days" — implemented via Form TM.47. (immigration)
- The regulation is **administrative, not criminal**, but failure to comply can result in fines and complications with future extensions or re-entry. (thaiembassy)

## Who must report (and who doesn't)

**Must report:**

- Any foreigner who:
  - Stays in Thailand 90 or more consecutive days on a valid non-immigrant or long-stay permission (Non-B, Non-O-A, Non-O retirement/marriage, Non-ED, LTR, SMART, media, etc.) (thaiembassy+1)
  - Continues to stay beyond 90 days without leaving Thailand (thaiembassy)
- Reporting continues every 90 days as long as you remain in the Kingdom. (immigration+1)

**Does NOT need to report:**

- Visitors whose permission to stay is 90 days or less, such as:
  - **Tourist visa exemption stamp** (60 days + 30-day extension = 90 — if you leave on or before day 90, no report due) (thaiembassy)
  - **Single-entry TR tourist visa** stays of up to 90 days (thaiembassy)
  - **Visa on Arrival** stays (less than 90 days) (thaiembassy)
- Foreigners who **depart Thailand before reaching the 90-day mark** — the clock resets to day 1 upon re-entry. (kidsenglishthailand+1)
- Foreigners with **permanent residency** — they follow different rules and are not part of the standard TM47 system. (thaiembassy)

## When to report — timing rules

- You must file TM47 once every 90 consecutive days of stay. (immigration)
- **Official window:**
  - Earliest: up to **15 days before** the 90-day due date
  - Latest: up to **7 days after** the due date (pcec+1)
- **Example:**
  - Arrive 1 January → Day 90 is 31 March
  - Filing window: 16 March – 7 April (15 days before to 7 days after) (pcec+1)
- If you leave Thailand before day 90, no report is due; your next 90 days start from the date you come back in. (kidsenglishthailand+1)

## Channels: four ways to file TM47

Thai Immigration recognises four channels for 90-day reporting:

1. **In person at Immigration** (e.g. Jomtien for Pattaya) (immigration+1)
2. **Online via TM47 portal or Immigration app** (tm47.immigration+2)
3. **By post (registered mail)** (thaiembassy+1)
4. **Through an authorised representative/agent** with a signed Power of Attorney (thaiembassy)

### 1) In-person reporting (TM47 at Immigration)

**What you need — standard document stack:**

- Completed TM.47 form (printed and signed) (inter.chula+1)
- Passport (original) (pcec+1)
- Photocopies, **each signed**:
  - Passport biodata page
  - Current visa or extension stamp
  - Last entry stamp
  - Re-entry permit page (if any)
  - Previous 90-day report slip (if applicable)
  - TM6 card (if you were issued one — older arrivals) or TDAC details (pcec+1)
- TM30 receipt (proof of address) — increasingly requested at many offices (asq.in+1)
- **There is no fee for 90-day reporting itself.** (pattaya-pages+1)

**How it works at Jomtien (Pattaya-specific):**

- Go to Jomtien Immigration Office (see G6 guide for full logistics)
- Take a queue ticket for 90-day reporting
- Hand in:
  - Completed TM47
  - Passport and copies
  - Previous slip (if any)
- Officer updates your record and prints a new receipt with your next due date (pcec+1)
- Typical time: **15–60 minutes**, depending on the queue (asq.in)

### 2) Online TM47 reporting (portal & app)

**Requirements:**

- You must have already completed at least one 90-day report in person or via another channel — **you can't do your first report online**. (thaiembassy+1)
- You must be within the allowed filing window; **online reporting must be submitted no later than the due date** (no 7-day late grace period online). (tm47.immigration+1)
- Some provinces and offices still have patchy systems; online reports may be rejected with a message telling you to report in person. (thaiembassy+1)

**Steps:**

1. Go to TM47 online portal: **https://tm47.immigration.go.th** (tm47.immigration)
2. **Create an account or log in:**
   - Provide passport number, date of birth, email, phone, and current address (tm47.immigration+1)
3. **Fill in online TM47 form:**
   - Passport details
   - Visa type and expiry
   - Last entry date
   - Current address (tm47.immigration)
4. Submit the report. The system shows your application status as "pending" until an officer reviews it (tm47.immigration)
5. **Within about 3 working days**, you receive:
   - **Approval:** online confirmation with your new due date — print and keep a copy; or
   - **Rejection:** email saying "Please contact the nearest Immigration Office in person as soon as possible." (tm47.immigration)
6. An updated slip is not always physically stamped in your passport when filing online; your printed confirmation serves as your record (thaiembassy)

**Common online failures:**

- Portal "Under Maintenance" — very common; try different browsers or times of day (kidsenglishthailand)
- Passport number / data mismatch with Immigration database; often tied to:
  - Name spelling differences
  - New passport issued since last report
  - Incorrect recorded address (thaiembassy+1)
- Wrong province — some provinces redirect you to file in person instead (thaiembassy)
- App glitches — the Immigration mobile app has mixed reviews and frequent login issues (kidsenglishthailand+1)

### 3) 90-day reporting by post

Postal reporting remains an option but is used less in 2026 due to reliability concerns.

**Requirements and steps:**

- Must send **registered mail** to your local Immigration office (thaiembassy+1)
- You need to include:
  - Completed TM.47 form (signed) (thaiembassy)
  - Photocopies (signed) of:
    - Passport biodata page
    - Current visa/extension stamp
    - Last entry stamp
    - Previous 90-day report slip (if any)
  - Self-addressed return envelope with **10 THB stamp** so Immigration can mail your receipt back (thaiembassy)
- Must arrive **at least 7 days before your due date** to be safe (thaiembassy)
- If no receipt arrives, you may still need to report in person to avoid fines

### 4) Using an agent or representative

- You may authorise a visa agent, lawyer, or friend to file TM47 on your behalf
- **Requirements:**
  - Completed and signed TM47
  - Signed photocopies of passport pages
  - Signed Power of Attorney authorising the representative (thaiembassy)
- The agent queues and files the report at Immigration; you receive your new slip later
- Common in Pattaya for retirees who dislike queues; **typical agent fees range from 500–1,500 THB** for a standalone 90-day report, often bundled with other services (pcec+1)

## Penalties for missing 90-day reporting

- **Official fine for late reporting: up to 2,000 THB.** (immigration+2)
- If you are arrested for other reasons **and also found to have missed reporting**, the fine can be up to 5,000 THB. (thaiembassy+1)
- Additional **daily fines for overstaying** (if your permission to stay also expired) are separate (500 THB per day, up to 20,000 THB) (thaiembassy)
- **In practice:**
  - Reporting a few days late usually results in a 2,000 THB fine at the time of filing
  - Chronic non-compliance can affect extension approvals and may be queried at future visa applications (thaiembassy)

> **This resolves the 2k-vs-5k penalty discrepancy across earlier visa research drops:**
> - **2,000 THB** = standard late filing fine
> - **5,000 THB** = maximum if arrested for other reasons AND found to have missed reporting
> All visa pages should reference these consistently going forward.

## How the 90-day clock resets

- The 90-day count starts:
  - From your **last arrival date** into Thailand; or
  - From the **date of your last 90-day report** — whichever is more recent (pcec+1)
- **If you leave Thailand:**
  - The clock is reset; you start again from day 1 on the date you re-enter (pcec+1)
  - Example: If your report is due on day 90 but you exit on day 89, no report is required; your next due date will be 90 days after you re-enter (kidsenglishthailand)

## Pattaya: Jomtien Immigration 90-day report process

For Pattaya/Chonburi residents, 90-day reports are handled at **Jomtien Immigration Office**. (pcec+1)

**What to bring:**

- Passport (original)
- Completed TM.47 (can be filled out at the office)
- Photocopies of:
  - Passport biodata page
  - Last entry stamp
  - Visa/extension page
  - TM6 or TDAC print details, if applicable
  - Previous 90-day slip (if any)
- TM30 receipt — often required in Chonburi (asq.in+1)

**Process:**

1. Take a queue ticket for TM47 reporting
2. Submit TM47 and documents at the counter
3. Officer prints/stamps a new 90-day receipt page with next due date
4. **Keep the new slip with your passport** — you'll need it next time (pcec+1)

**Pattaya tips:**

- **Combine 90-day report with:**
  - Annual extension visit (retirement/marriage/ED) when dates coincide
  - Re-entry permit purchase if you plan to travel soon (visa.thainexus+1)
- Arrive early (before 09:00) to minimise waiting
- No fee — but photocopy shops outside Jomtien charge small fees for document copies

## Step-by-step: online TM47 walkthrough (2026)

Using the TM47 portal — generic flow for the guide page: (tm47.immigration+3)

**1. Login / Register**

- Visit tm47.immigration.go.th
- Click "Register" for new users
- Enter:
  - Passport number
  - Date of birth
  - Email
  - Phone number
  - Current address in Thailand

**2. Create TM47 Application**

- Choose "Notification of Staying in the Kingdom over 90 days"
- Enter:
  - Passport details (number, issuing country, issue/expiry dates)
  - Latest visa/extension type and expiry date
  - Date of last arrival in Thailand
  - Address at which you're staying (must match TM30 in many provinces)

**3. Review and Submit**

- Check for spelling mistakes — especially in your name and address
- Confirm date fields are correct (Thai format vs international can trip people up)
- Click "Submit"

**4. Wait for approval**

- Status initially shows as pending
- Within about 3 days, one of:
  - **Approved:** system might show a new due date and generate a confirmation; save and print it
  - **Rejected:** you receive an email instructing you to visit your local Immigration in person (tm47.immigration)

**5. Print confirmation**

- Print and staple confirmation to your passport or store digitally; many expats also keep a photo on their phone

## Common myths and clarifications

1. **"If I go to Cambodia for the day my report is reset"** — **TRUE.** Leaving Thailand, even for a short border run, resets your 90-day count. When you re-enter, you start at day 1 again. (kidsenglishthailand+1)
2. **"I have to physically attend to report"** — **FALSE.** You can report online, by mail, or via an agent. In-person is only one option; online and agent-based reporting are widely used. (thaiembassy+2)
3. **"It's optional — nobody checks"** — **FALSE.** 90-day reporting is mandatory and is checked when you apply for extensions or new visas. Officers often verify your TM47 record during annual extension processing. (pcec+1)
4. **"Online reporting always works now"** — **FALSE.** The portal has improved but still suffers outages, browser incompatibilities, and data mismatches. Many expats still use in-person or agent reporting. (kidsenglishthailand+1)
5. **"If my visa is multiple-entry, I don't need to report"** — **FALSE.** Multiple-entry status has nothing to do with TM47. If you stay inside Thailand 90+ days continuously on any non-immigrant permission, you must report. (thaiembassy)

## 12 real FAQ questions

**1. I've just got my first 1-year extension at Jomtien; when is my first 90-day report due?**
90 days after the date you entered Thailand, or 90 days after your last report if you've already reported. **It is not measured from the date of the extension stamp.** (pcec+1)

**2. If I leave Thailand on day 89, do I still need to report?**
No. Leaving before day 90 cancels your current reporting obligation. When you return, a new 90-day period begins. (pcec+1)

**3. How early can I do my 90-day report?**
You can file it up to 15 days before your due date. Many people go 1–2 weeks early to avoid last-minute issues. (immigration+1)

**4. How late can I be without getting a fine?**
Officially, you have 7 days after the due date. After that, a fine (up to 2,000 THB) is normally imposed. **Don't rely on the grace period — file within the window.** (immigration+2)

**5. Does the 90-day report apply to DTV, LTR, Elite, SMART?**
- **DTV, Non-O-A, Non-O, Non-B, ED, Marriage:** Yes — standard TM47 every 90 days
- **LTR & SMART:** Reporting is reduced to once per year — they have special rules
- **Thailand Privilege (Elite):** TM47 is handled by the service provider on your behalf (ltr.boi+2)

**6. Can I change my address during a 90-day report?**
Yes — the TM47 records your current address. However, you should also ensure your TM30 address notification is up to date, as some offices cross-check TM30 with TM47. (thaiembassy+1)

**7. I moved from Chiang Mai to Pattaya — where do I report now?**
You report at the Immigration office that covers your current address — Jomtien for Pattaya. **Make sure your TM30 has been filed for the new address before your next report.** (asq.in+1)

**8. What happens if I forget and report months late?**
You'll typically be fined around 2,000 THB when you finally report. If you also overstayed your permission to stay, you may face additional overstay fines and complications. (thaiembassy+1)

**9. Can my visa agent handle my TM47 every time?**
Yes, as long as you sign a Power of Attorney and provide the necessary copies. Many Pattaya agents bundle 90-day reporting into their annual extension packages. (pcec+1)

**10. Is 90-day reporting the same as visa extension?**
No. 90-day reporting only confirms your address; it does not extend your permission to stay. Annual extensions (1-year retiree, marriage, business, ED, etc.) are a separate process with separate fees and documents. (thaiembassy+1)

**11. Do I need to bring my TM30 receipt for TM47?**
In many provinces (including Chonburi), officers expect your TM30 to be properly filed. Some actively ask to see the TM30 receipt during 90-day reporting. **If it's missing, they may ask you to sort TM30 first.** (asq.in+1)

**12. Is there any benefit to reporting exactly on day 90 vs earlier?**
No real benefit. Your next due date is always 90 days from the date Immigration logs your report. Many expats choose to report a few days early to avoid missing the window. (immigration+1)

## 2025–2026 enforcement changes

- **Online TM47 platform stabilisation:** The TM47 portal is more reliable in 2025–2026 compared to early versions, though outages still occur and some provinces reject online filings (tm47.immigration+2)
- **TDAC rollout:** With TDAC replacing TM6 for most arrivals, Immigration now has better digital entry data, making TM47 a "live digital handshake" with the national database (thailandinsiderguide+1)
- **Greater cross-checking with TM30:** Some provincial offices (including Chonburi and Bangkok) now more consistently cross-check TM47 with TM30 to verify that landlords have registered guests correctly (asq.in+1)
- **Clarified timing guidance:** Immigration pages and embassy guides now clearly state the 15 days before / 7 days after rule and emphasise that online reporting must be done by the due date, not after (pcec+2)
- **Increased use of agents and online options:** As queues at major offices grew in 2024–2025, more long-stay foreigners shifted to using agents or the online portal, making 90-day reporting less of a practical burden than it once was (pcec+1)
