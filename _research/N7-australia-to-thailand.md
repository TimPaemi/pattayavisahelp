## Australia-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance

- **Australian Federal Police (AFP) National Police Check** — for criminal record certificate
- Apply via afp.gov.au or accredited body (Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission — ACIC)
- Cost: AUD 42–88 depending on type
- Time: 5–10 working days

### Document legalisation

Australia is Hague Apostille Convention member:

1. **Apostille** from Australian Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT)
2. **Royal Thai Embassy Canberra** or consulates (Sydney, Melbourne, Perth) certification
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya
4. **Thai MFA legalisation**
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Australian chain:** 5–8 weeks, ~AUD 200–300.

### Australian Embassy income letter — DISCONTINUED

Australian Embassy Bangkok **stopped issuing income letters** for Thai retirement visa applications years ago. Australian retirees must use **800,000 THB Thai bank deposit method**.

## Australian tax considerations

### Australian residency for tax

- **Resides test, 183-day test, domicile test** — multiple criteria for Australian tax residency
- Living abroad doesn't automatically end Australian residency
- Important to formally establish non-resident status if relocating permanently

### Pension and superannuation

- **Age Pension:** May be portable to Thailand depending on residency rules; reduced rates possible
- **Superannuation withdrawals:** Subject to specific rules; typically taxable in Thailand if remitted (Australian tax credit available under DTA)
- **DASP (Departing Australia Superannuation Payment):** if you've worked in Australia on temporary visa and leave permanently

### DTA

Australia-Thailand DTA provides:
- Foreign tax credit mechanism
- Specific rules for various income types
- Tie-breaker rules for dual residency

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Can I get a Non-O-X 10-year retirement visa as an Australian?**
**Yes** — Australia is one of 14 eligible nationalities. Requires 3M THB Thai bank deposit and qualifying insurance.

**2. Will Australian Age Pension be paid in Thailand?**
Generally yes, with conditions. Continuing Australian residency status affects rate. Consult Centrelink before relocating; some Australians maintain technical Australian residency to preserve full Age Pension.

**3. Australian Embassy income letter for retirement visa?**
**No longer issued.** Use 800,000 THB Thai bank deposit method.

**4. Can I keep my Australian Medicare?**
**No** — Medicare doesn't cover treatment outside Australia. Use private Thai or international expat insurance.

**5. Australian driving licence conversion?**
Hague Convention member; conversion at Banglamung DLT skips driving test. Translation needed (~1,500 THB).

**6. Where do Australians live in Pattaya?**
Mostly Jomtien and Pratumnak. Australian community is well-integrated with broader Anglo expat network.

**7. Tax considerations for Australians retiring in Pattaya?**
Establish non-resident status carefully. DTA provides credits. Thai 180-day tax residency applies. Professional advice recommended for super withdrawals.

**8. Can I bring my Australian super to Thailand?**
Withdrawals taxable in Australia first (varies by structure); remitted income may be Thai-taxable. DTA provides credit. Many Australians maintain super in Australia and withdraw progressively.
