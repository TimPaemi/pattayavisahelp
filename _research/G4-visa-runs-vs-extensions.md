## What this guide covers

Two related questions every long-stay-curious foreigner in Thailand asks:

1. **"Can I just keep doing border bounces instead of getting a real visa?"** (Short answer: no, not anymore.)
2. **"What's the difference between extending in-country vs running to a border?"** (Short answer: extension stamps are the only sustainable path.)

This guide explains current 2026 enforcement, what still works, what doesn't, and which visa is actually the right answer for your situation.

## The death of the visa run (2024–2026)

For roughly 15 years (2008 to 2023), it was common for foreigners to live in Thailand on rolling visa-exempt entries — fly out, stay a night in Cambodia or Laos, fly back, get a fresh stamp. Pattaya, Chiang Mai, and Phuket built entire expat communities around this lifestyle.

**That era is over.** The November 2025 enforcement announcement and the digital flagging systems rolled out at major airports have closed the loophole.

**What changed:**

- **Land borders capped at 2 visa-exempt entries per calendar year** (Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Malaysia). Strictly enforced from November 2025.
- **Air arrivals capped at 6 visa-exempt entries per calendar year** — a higher limit but still finite.
- **Computer flagging systems** at Suvarnabhumi (BKK), Don Mueang (DMK), Chiang Mai (CNX), and Phuket (HKT) now flag any passport with more than 2 visa-exempt entries in a rolling 12-month period for officer review.
- **Officer discretion to deny entry** is now formally enabled. Approximately **2,900 foreigners were denied entry in 2025** under the new enforcement standards.
- **Pattern of brief exits and returns** — the classic 24–48 hour border run shape — is the highest-risk profile statistically.

## What still works in 2026

### Legitimate short-trip travel
If you visit Thailand once or twice a year as a tourist, return home in between, and stay for under 90 days each time — you face zero issues. The new rules target *living in Thailand long-term without a long-stay visa*, not actual tourism.

### In-country 30-day extension at Immigration
- Anyone on a tourist visa or visa-exempt entry can extend once for 30 days at any Immigration office (Jomtien for Pattaya).
- **Cost: 1,900 THB**
- One extension per stay. A second extension is technically possible at land borders but unreliable.
- This is NOT a border run — it's extending what you already have.

### Re-entry permits (for long-stay visa holders)
- If you have a 1-year extension (retirement, marriage, business, ED, etc.), buy a re-entry permit before leaving Thailand. See `/guides/re-entry-permits/`.
- Single re-entry: 1,000 THB. Multiple: 3,800 THB.
- This isn't a "visa run" — you're preserving your existing long-stay status.

### Multiple-entry visas (designed for repeat travel)
- **METV** (Multiple Entry Tourist Visa): 6 months validity, 60 days per entry, unlimited entries during validity.
- **DTV**: 5 years validity, 180 days per entry, unlimited entries.
- **LTR / SMART / Privilege**: All multiple-entry by design. No re-entry permit needed.
- Each fresh entry on these visas does NOT count as a visa-exempt entry — they're proper visas, not exemptions.

### Genuine cross-border tourism
- Going to Siem Reap for a 5-day Angkor Wat trip, then re-entering Thailand → fine.
- Going to Singapore for a business meeting, returning a week later → fine.
- The enforcement targets *patterns of brief exit-and-return*, not legitimate travel.

## What doesn't work anymore

### Repeated 24–48 hour land-border runs
The classic Pattaya pattern — bus to Aranyaprathet/Poipet (Cambodia border), overnight in Cambodia, back across in the morning — **flagged automatically after 2 entries.** Officers will deny entry on the third attempt without a justifiable reason.

### Same-day in-and-out
Foreign nationals departing and re-entering Thailand on the same day are **explicitly excluded from getting an extension** and increasingly questioned at the border.

### Living in Thailand year-round on visa exemption alone
This was the standard "expat with no proper visa" lifestyle for 15 years. As of 2025–2026, it's effectively impossible. Officers actively look for this pattern.

### "Just one more border run" if you've had 2 land entries this year
The 2-per-calendar-year hard cap on land entries means a third attempt before January 1 is denial-of-entry territory — even with all documents, even at a different border crossing.

### Land border visa-exempt entries can't be extended
Foreign nationals entering by land border on visa exemption **cannot apply for the 30-day Immigration extension.** The extension is only available to air arrivals.

## Which visa is actually right for you?

Decision tree based on your situation:

### "I want to live in Thailand long-term but don't want bureaucracy"
- **Over 50, want simplicity** → **Thailand Privilege/Elite** (650k–5M THB upfront, no annual hassle)
- **Working remotely for foreign income** → **DTV** (10k THB, 5 years validity, 180 days per entry)
- **High income (>$80k/yr)** → **LTR Wealthy Pensioner** or **LTR Work-from-Thailand** (10-year visa with tax benefits)

### "I'm a retiree in my 50s+"
- **800k THB available + over 50** → **Non-O retirement (in-country)** is the simplest path. No insurance required.
- **Want embassy-issued 1-year visa upfront** → **Non-O-A retirement** (requires insurance)
- **3M THB available + eligible nationality** → **Non-O-X 10-year retirement**

### "I'm married to a Thai national"
- **Have 400k THB or 40k/month income** → **Non-O Marriage**

### "I want to study Thai language or Muay Thai"
- **At an MOE-accredited school** → **Non-ED Education visa**

### "I have a job offer or want to start a business"
- **Foreign company sending me** → **Non-B + work permit**
- **BOI-promoted company** → **SMART or Non-B with BOI fast-track**

### "I just want to stay 60–90 days for tourism"
- **Visa exemption** (60 days) + 30-day extension at Immigration = up to 90 days. Perfect for actual tourism.

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**Aranyaprathet/Poipet (Cambodia) border** — historically the most popular Pattaya border-bounce destination:

- Roughly 4-hour drive or bus ride from Pattaya
- Multiple operators run "border run" services (5,000–8,000 THB) — these still operate but **no longer make sense for visa-exempt long-stayers**
- Now mostly used by people with multiple-entry visas (DTV, METV, LTR) who genuinely want to leave and re-enter
- **Honest advice:** if you find yourself contemplating an Aranyaprathet border run to keep living in Pattaya, you've outgrown visa exemption. Time to apply for a proper long-stay visa.

**Trat → Cambodia (Koh Kong)** — secondary Cambodia option, popular with Pattaya islanders. Same rules apply.

**Mae Sai (Myanmar)** — closed for foreigners as of 2024–2026 due to Myanmar instability. Not viable.

**Pattaya City Expats Club (PCEC)** maintains updated guidance on what's currently working at the border. Worth a partner relationship.

## Common pitfalls

1. **"Just one more border run" thinking.** If you've used 2 visa-exempt entries this year, a third is high-risk. Plan ahead — switch to a proper visa BEFORE you run out of entries.
2. **Confusing METV / DTV with visa exemption.** People holding METV or DTV worry the 2-per-year rule applies to them. **It doesn't.** Multiple-entry visas don't count against the visa-exempt cap.
3. **Same-day border bounces.** These are explicitly rejected for the in-country 30-day extension and increasingly questioned at the border.
4. **Trying to extend a land-border visa-exempt entry.** Can't be done. The 30-day Immigration extension is air-arrival only.
5. **Assuming officer discretion = officer leniency.** Officers have discretion to deny, not to wave through. Pattern-flagged passports get denied regularly.
6. **Forgetting that 90-day reporting and re-entry permits apply to everyone.** Border runs reset the 90-day clock; re-entry permits preserve long-stay extensions. These are separate from the visa-run question. See G1 and G3 guides.
7. **Border run agency promises.** Some Pattaya agents still promote "border run packages" as if 2018 rules apply. Verify their info against current 2026 enforcement before paying.

## 12 FAQ questions

**1. Can I still do a border run to Cambodia in 2026?**
Yes for actual travel; no for "live in Thailand without a visa." Land-border visa-exempt entries are capped at 2 per calendar year. Air arrivals at 6 per year. After that, denial of entry is likely.

**2. How is "border run" different from "visa extension"?**
A border run = leaving Thailand and re-entering to get a fresh stamp. A visa extension = staying in Thailand and getting more time at Immigration office for 1,900 THB. Extensions are still the standard path for legitimate long-stay residents.

**3. Will my multiple-entry visa (METV / DTV) count against the 2-per-year limit?**
No. Multiple-entry visas are not visa-exempt entries — they're entries on a visa you paid for and applied for. Use them as designed.

**4. I've already used 2 visa-exempt land entries this year. Can I fly in instead?**
Probably yes — air arrivals have a separate 6-per-year cap. But officer discretion may still result in scrutiny if your passport shows a clear pattern. **The cleaner answer: get a proper long-stay visa now.**

**5. Can I extend my visa-exempt stay if I came in by land?**
No — land-border visa-exempt entries cannot be extended at Immigration. Only air arrivals can extend.

**6. What's the maximum stay I can get on visa exemption + extension?**
Currently 60 days exemption + 30-day extension = 90 days continuous, **for air arrivals only**. Land arrivals: 60 days only, no extension.

**7. If I leave Thailand for a real holiday, does that count against the 2-entry limit?**
The 2-entry limit applies specifically to visa-exempt entries via land borders. If you fly out for a genuine 2-week holiday in Vietnam, the air arrival on return counts toward your 6-per-year air cap. Genuine separated trips are fine; brief border-run shapes are flagged.

**8. Will the 60-day visa exemption be reduced to 30 days?**
There's an active proposal under review (announced March 2026 by the Minister of Foreign Affairs). **No decision has been made and no implementation date is set as of April 2026.** Even if reduced, a 30-day extension would still bring the maximum to 60 days for air arrivals.

**9. I'm an EU citizen — do these rules apply to me?**
Yes. The 2-per-year land cap and 6-per-year air cap apply to **all visa-exempt nationalities**, regardless of country.

**10. What if I have a justifiable reason to enter more than 2 times via land?**
Officers can grant entry with a documented reason (medical emergency, family event, etc.). But "I'm visiting friends in Pattaya again" is not justifiable. Don't rely on this.

**11. How do I know if I've been "flagged" by the system?**
You won't get an alert, but signs include: longer questioning at immigration on entry, requests to show return tickets and accommodation, secondary inspection. If you've had 2+ visa-exempt entries this year, expect more scrutiny.

**12. What's the cheapest legitimate long-stay alternative to border running?**
**DTV at 10,000 THB** for 5 years of multiple entries with 180 days per stay is the best value option for most remote workers and frequent visitors. **Education visa** (Thai language or Muay Thai) is also relatively affordable but requires real attendance. **Non-O retirement** is the cheapest path for over-50s with 800k THB available.

## 2025–2026 enforcement timeline

- **November 2025:** Immigration Bureau Commissioner announces formal 2-per-calendar-year cap on visa-exempt land border entries. Computer flagging system rolled out at major airports.
- **2025 cumulative:** Approximately 2,900 foreigners denied entry under new standards.
- **Early 2026:** Multiple reports of air arrivals receiving 30-day stamps (instead of 60) after 2+ prior visa-exempt entries — officer discretion in action.
- **March 2026:** Minister of Foreign Affairs announces proposal to reduce visa exemption from 60 to 30 days. Under review as of April 2026 — no decision yet.
- **Ongoing:** TDAC (Thailand Digital Arrival Card) replaces TM6, giving Immigration cleaner pre-arrival data on travel patterns.
