## Canada-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance

- **RCMP Criminal Record Check** — federal-level certificate
- Cost: CAD 25–60 depending on type
- Time: 1–4 weeks (longer if fingerprints required)
- Apply via accredited body (Canadian Criminal Real Time Identification Services)

### Document legalisation

**Canada became Hague Apostille Convention member in 2024.** This significantly simplified the chain:

1. **Apostille from Global Affairs Canada** (Authentication of Canadian Documents Section)
2. **Royal Thai Embassy Ottawa** certification
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya
4. **Thai MFA legalisation**
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total Canadian chain:** 5–8 weeks (improved post-2024 vs the older multi-step legalisation).

## Canadian tax considerations

### CRA residency

- **Canadian residency for tax** based on facts (residential ties, dwelling, family, social connections)
- Departure tax may apply when officially becoming non-resident
- Sale of principal residence rules

### Pension

- **CPP (Canada Pension Plan):** Generally portable; taxable in Thailand if remitted
- **OAS (Old Age Security):** Special rules — must have lived 20 years in Canada for full portability abroad
- **RRSP/RRIF withdrawals:** Subject to 25% Canadian withholding for non-residents (or 15% under DTA); credit available against any Thai tax

### Canada-Thailand DTA

Provides foreign tax credit, source rules, tie-breaker for dual residency.

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Can Canadians get Non-O-X 10-year retirement visa?**
**Yes** — Canada is on the 14-country list.

**2. What about CPP and OAS in Thailand?**
**CPP** is portable. **OAS** requires 20 years' Canadian residency to be paid in full abroad indefinitely; less than 20 years means OAS stops 6 months after departure.

**3. RRSP/RRIF withdrawals?**
Subject to 25% Canadian withholding (or 15% under Canada-Thailand DTA). Credit available against any Thai tax. Many Canadians plan withdrawals carefully for tax efficiency.

**4. Provincial healthcare in Thailand?**
**No** — provincial healthcare (OHIP, MSP, etc.) doesn't cover treatment outside Canada. Most Canadians take private Thai or international expat insurance.

**5. Where do Canadians live in Pattaya?**
Jomtien, Pratumnak primarily. Mixed with broader Anglo community.

**6. Driving licence?**
Canada is now Hague signatory; conversion at Banglamung DLT skips driving test. Translation needed.

**7. Tax residency formalities?**
Departure from Canada involves potential departure tax on unrealized capital gains; consult Canadian accountant. Establishing non-resident status formally important for tax planning.

**8. Currency considerations?**
CAD–THB ~26 THB per CAD. Wise preferred for routine; SWIFT for retirement seasoning.
