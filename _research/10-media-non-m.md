## Who it's for

The Non-Immigrant M visa is Thailand's official visa category for foreign media personnel — journalists, foreign correspondents, newspaper/TV/radio reporters, documentary crews, and film production staff — who intend to work in Thailand for 90 days or more as accredited members of the media. A second variant covers film crew producing commercial films, motion pictures, or advertisements in Thailand.

It is processed via the MFA's **MMOS (MFA Media Online Service)** platform and ultimately accredited by Thailand's Department of Information, Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

**In 2026 this visa sits at the centre of a growing grey area:** YouTube creators, travel vloggers, and social media influencers based in Pattaya and across Thailand often occupy a legally ambiguous zone between "media professional" and "content creator without accreditation" — and enforcement has begun to tighten. (mmos.mfa+2, youtube, thaiembassyjakarta+1)

## Eligibility (full list)

The MFA revised its Non-M eligibility criteria — the current (2026) requirements are:

1. Must be **affiliated with or employed by a credible media entity duly registered with the competent authority** in Thailand or abroad **for at least 6 months** at the time of application (mmos.mfa+1)
2. Employed solely as a media professional — must not engage in any other occupation during the entire stay in Thailand (mfa+1)
3. Must discharge media duties in Thailand for a minimum of 90 days per year and **produce a minimum of 12 pieces of content per annum** pertaining to Thailand or the wider region (budapest.thaiembassy+1)
4. Must not have previously produced content or behaved in a way that constitutes a threat to society, public order, or public safety — and must not be the subject of an arrest warrant by any foreign government (mmos.mfa+1)
5. Must be able to demonstrate a clear, verifiable media role — journalist, correspondent, documentary director, film producer, or film crew member (pretoria.thaiembassy+1)
6. Must apply via the MMOS portal for stays of 3 months or longer; ad-hoc short trips by foreign media may use a standard Non-B visa (mmos.mfa)

**What this means in practice:**

- **Traditional media** (BBC, Reuters, AP, CNN, Al Jazeera) — clearly eligible
- **Documentary filmmakers** with credited production company — eligible
- **YouTubers or travel vloggers** affiliated with a verifiable, registered media organisation — potentially eligible with strong documentation
- **Independent YouTubers** with no media registration — **not eligible for Non-M** (siamvisapro, youtube)
- **Bloggers and influencers** — not eligible for Non-M; DTV or other visas are the correct route (siamvisapro)

## Required documents

**Core documents for all Non-M applicants:**

1. Valid passport — minimum 6 months' validity (image.mfa+1)
2. Passport-sized photographs — 2 recent photos (pretoria.thaiembassy)
3. Completed Thai visa application form (via Thai e-Visa portal) (kualalumpur.thaiembassy)
4. **Official letter from the news agency / media entity headquarters** addressed to the Director-General of the Department of Information, Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Thailand — must state:
   - Applicant's position and scope of work
   - Period of employment/assignment in Thailand
   - Request for visa issuance
   - Request for work permit or press card (mfa+1)
5. Applicant's curriculum vitae (CV) (mfa+1)
6. Company profile of the media entity (mfa+1)
7. **Proof of registration of the media entity** with competent authority in Thailand or abroad (mfa+1)
8. Copy of employment contract (if applicable) (mfa)
9. Criminal background / certificate of clearance from the competent authority in the country where the applicant is a permanent resident (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)
10. **Portfolio of 10–12 most recent media reports/content pieces** produced by the applicant (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)
11. Financial evidence: recommendation letter from employer or bank statements for past 3 months showing balance of at least:
    - **THB 30,000 for single-entry** applicants
    - **THB 60,000 for multiple-entry** applicants (image.mfa)
12. Proof of income and tax in Thailand (if applicable — for accredited media who are tax-resident) (mfa+1)

**For in-country extension:**

1. TM.7 Extension of Stay form (pretoria.thaiembassy)
2. Current work permit or press card (vientiane.thaiembassy)
3. Proof of current employment/assignment letter updated to current period (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)
4. Evidence of minimum 12 pieces of content produced (mmos.mfa)

The financial requirement is relatively modest — the main barriers are documentation quality and media entity legitimacy, not financial thresholds.

## Application process — step by step

### For foreign media staying 90+ days (standard Non-M)

1. **Register on MMOS (MFA Media Online Service)** — https://mmos.mfa.go.th (mmos.mfa)
2. Your media entity's headquarters prepares and submits the official letter to the Department of Information, Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Bangkok (pretoria.thaiembassy+1)
3. Upload all required documents on MMOS — passport, CV, company profile, media registration proof, portfolio, criminal clearance (mmos.mfa+1)
4. **MFA Department of Information reviews the application and issues a Letter of Authorisation** (endorsement) if approved (vfsglobal)
5. Apply for the visa using the authorisation number at a Royal Thai Embassy/Consulate or via the Thai e-Visa portal — at least 15 working days before intended travel (kualalumpur.thaiembassy)
6. Visa issued — valid 90 days from issue date (thaiembassyjakarta)
7. On arrival in Thailand, apply for 1-year extension of stay and associated **press card / work permit** — processed through Immigration Bureau and coordinated with MFA (pretoria.thaiembassy+1)

### For short-term / ad-hoc media assignments (<90 days)

- Apply for a standard Non-B (Business) visa — accredited ad-hoc assignments do not require the full Non-M/MMOS process (mmos.mfa)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

- **MMOS registration and MFA authorisation:** Always starts online at mmos.mfa.go.th — regardless of whether you are in Thailand or abroad (mmos.mfa)
- **Visa issuance:** At any Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate, or via the Thai e-Visa portal after receiving the MFA authorisation number (kualalumpur.thaiembassy)
- **In-country extension:** At the Immigration Bureau (Chaeng Watthana Bangkok or Jomtien for Pattaya-based media) in coordination with the MFA press accreditation (pretoria.thaiembassy)
- Pattaya-based foreign media typically manage extensions at Jomtien Immigration and keep their MFA accreditation current annually **[verify with any Pattaya-based foreign correspondents]**

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial Non-M visa:** 90 days from date of issue (thaiembassyjakarta+1)
- **In-country extension of stay:** 1 year, extendable annually as long as employment/assignment continues and MFA accreditation is maintained (mfa+1)
- The 1-year extension is **tied to the press card and work permit renewal cycle** (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)
- **Minimum annual output required for renewal: 12 pieces of content per year** pertaining to Thailand or the region — MFA will check this at renewal (budapest.thaiembassy+1)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — standard 90-day TM47 reporting applies** to Non-M extension holders, the same as Non-B and other long-stay visas. There is **no reduced annual reporting equivalent** to the LTR or SMART programmes for media visa holders.

- Report every 90 days at Jomtien (Pattaya) or Chaeng Watthana (Bangkok)
- Online TM47 filing also available via immigration.go.th

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required.** Exiting Thailand while on a 1-year Non-M extension without a re-entry permit cancels the extension:

- Single re-entry: 1,000 THB
- Multiple re-entry: 3,800 THB

Foreign correspondents who travel frequently for story coverage should purchase a **multiple re-entry permit at the start of their extension year**.

## Common pitfalls

1. **Independent creators assuming they qualify:** The most common misunderstanding in 2026. A Thailand-based YouTuber with 200,000 subscribers who produces weekly Thailand content is **not eligible for Non-M** unless they are employed by or formally affiliated with a registered media entity. The MFA definition of "credible media entity" requires registration with a competent authority and a demonstrable audience — not just a YouTube channel. (reddit+2)
2. **Media entity not registered for at least 6 months:** Even legitimate media companies can fail at this hurdle if they are newly launched. The entity must have existed and been registered for a minimum of 6 months before the application. New digital media startups trying to send correspondents to Thailand immediately after launch are rejected. (budapest.thaiembassy+1)
3. **Producing content outside the declared scope:** The Non-M accredits you for specific media duties within a specific assignment. Creating commercial content — sponsored posts, paid brand integrations, advertising — while on a Non-M is outside the visa's remit and could be construed as working without a work permit for commercial purposes. (youtube, reddit)
4. **Failing to produce the minimum 12 pieces of content:** Renewal of accreditation requires a verifiable track record. Coming to renewal with only 6 or 7 pieces of published content — or content not clearly "pertaining to Thailand or the region" — can result in renewal refusal. (mmos.mfa+1)
5. **Ignoring the 2026 influencer crackdown:** Thai Immigration conducted a major **Phuket sweep in 2024 detaining approximately 9–10 foreign influencers** working without proper authorisation. Pattaya is on the same enforcement trajectory. Operating publicly as a content creator in Pattaya with large visible Thai-audience engagement on any tourist or visa-exempt stay is an increasing risk. (reddit)
6. **Mixing media and business income:** If a foreign correspondent on a Non-M visa is also earning income from sources other than media work (e.g. consulting, online teaching, running a Thailand-focused service) they are in violation of the "employed solely as a media professional" condition. (mmos.mfa+1)
7. **Forgetting the press card / work permit link:** The Non-M is linked to a press card and work permit issued by the relevant Thai authorities. Holding the visa extension without maintaining the active press card causes a compliance gap — especially if the press card expires before the annual extension is renewed. (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

**Pattaya's YouTube/content creator community:** Pattaya has a significant and growing community of foreign-based YouTube creators, travel vloggers, and lifestyle content producers. In 2026 this population spans:

- Long-established foreign correspondents and documentary producers (genuinely accredited, holding valid Non-M)
- Lifestyle vloggers running Thailand channels with tens of thousands to millions of subscribers — **vast majority operating on tourist visas, DTV, or ED visas, not Non-M** (reddit, youtube)
- New arrivals attracted by DTV who create content alongside remote work (siamvisapro)

**The honest Pattaya legal landscape for creators:** Most Thailand-based YouTube creators producing Pattaya nightlife, travel, or lifestyle content are **not on Non-M visas** — they are on tourist visa extensions, DTV, or retirement/marriage visas. The Non-M is largely used by genuine foreign press (The Guardian, BBC, Reuters stringers, freelance correspondents for major outlets). Influencers and lifestyle creators are best served by: (reddit+1)

- **DTV** (if earning foreign income from creative work) — the recommended route in 2026
- **LTR-T** (if income meets the $80k threshold and employer qualifies)
- **NOT the Non-M**, unless they have formal affiliation with a registered media entity

**Jomtien Immigration for Non-M renewals:** Accredited foreign media based in Pattaya renew their Non-M extensions at Jomtien. Unlike tourist extensions, these require bringing press card documentation and a current employment/assignment letter. **[verify exact Jomtien Non-M handling vs Bangkok MFA]**

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Can I get a Non-M visa if I'm a travel YouTuber?**
Only if you are formally employed by or affiliated with a registered media entity (newspaper, broadcaster, online news platform) that has been registered for at least 6 months. An independent YouTube channel alone — even a popular one — does not qualify. Most travel YouTubers in Thailand are better suited to the DTV. (siamvisapro+1)

**2. What counts as a "credible media entity"?**
The MFA guideline describes it as a media entity "duly registered with the competent authority in Thailand or abroad" with a "significant number of viewers and followers." This covers established broadcasters, newspapers, news agencies, and credible digital news outlets. The threshold for "significant" is not numerically defined — it is a discretionary judgment by the MFA. (budapest.thaiembassy+1)

**3. Can I do sponsored brand partnerships on a Non-M visa?**
This is legally risky. The Non-M accredits you specifically for media/journalism work. Paid brand collaborations are effectively commercial advertising work — outside the media visa's defined scope and potentially requiring a separate work permit. (reddit+1)

**4. Is there a minimum follower count or views required to qualify?**
No official number is published. The MFA uses "significant number of viewers and followers" as the qualifier for the media entity — not for the individual applicant. The entity's reach and registration are what matter. (mmos.mfa)

**5. What happens if immigration discovers I'm creating commercial content on a Non-M?**
You could face visa cancellation, deportation, and potential bans. Thailand has deported foreign influencers — though most publicised cases involved tourist visa holders, not Non-M holders specifically. The risk is real and growing. (youtube, reddit)

**6. I'm a freelance documentary filmmaker. Can I get a Non-M?**
Potentially yes, if your production company is properly registered and has been operating for at least 6 months, and you can demonstrate the project pertains to Thailand. Film crew and documentary producers are explicitly listed as qualifying categories. (thaiconsulatela.thaiembassy+1)

**7. Does the Non-M give me a work permit?**
Yes — once you are in Thailand with a Non-M extension, you can be issued a press card and work permit through the coordinated MFA/Department of Employment process. The press card is effectively the media equivalent of a standard work permit. (vientiane.thaiembassy+1)

**8. How many articles/videos do I need to produce to keep my Non-M?**
At renewal: **12 pieces of content per year** pertaining to Thailand or the region. This is explicitly stated in the 2026 revised MFA guidelines. (budapest.thaiembassy+1)

**9. What is the MMOS portal?**
The MFA Media Online Service at mmos.mfa.go.th is the mandatory online registration and application portal for all Non-M applicants seeking stays of 90+ days. All document uploads and MFA correspondence go through this portal. (mmos.mfa)

**10. Can I switch from Non-M to another visa type?**
Yes — visa conversions are handled at Immigration. Common switches: Non-M → Non-B (if joining a company in a different role), Non-M → Non-O (if spouse/retirement eligible). Seek legal advice as the conversion requires proper documentation and timing. (pretoria.thaiembassy)

**11. What is the difference between Non-M and Non-F?**
Non-F is the "Official" visa category for government/official media representatives. Non-M is for commercial and independent foreign press. The application process via MMOS is similar, but Non-F is issued to official state broadcaster employees and government media representatives. (mfa+1)

**12. I'm a Pattaya-based vlogger with 500k YouTube subscribers. Should I get a Non-M?**
**Almost certainly not the right visa.** Unless you are employed by a registered media company, you don't qualify. Most vloggers of this profile in Thailand use the **DTV (Destination Thailand Visa) as of 2026** — this covers self-employed individuals earning foreign income from creative work. (mbmg-group+1)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **MFA revised Non-M guidelines (updated through 2025–2026):** Content minimum increased from 10 to 12 pieces per year in current embassy-published versions — a tightening of the renewal criteria (budapest.thaiembassy+1)
- **Phuket and Chiang Mai influencer crackdowns (2024–2025):** Thai immigration and police detained groups of foreign influencers working without proper authorisation; **Pattaya enforcement is expected to follow the same pattern** (reddit)
- **DTV effectively absorbs the content creator cohort:** With the launch of the DTV in 2024, most content creators and lifestyle vloggers who previously operated in a grey zone now have a cleaner legitimate option — reducing pressure on the Non-M pathway for non-journalists (facebook+1)
- **Definition expansion of "online media":** MFA guidelines explicitly include "credible and reliable online media agencies duly registered... with a large number of viewers and followers" — a nod to digital-native media, but the registration and credibility requirements still exclude individual channels (mfa+1)
- **Social media scrutiny in visa screening (April 2026):** US Embassy in Bangkok announced expanded social media screening for visa applicants — a signal of growing global alignment between public social media presence and immigration decisions (x+1)
- **"Vlogger immigration registration" rumour debunked:** A December 2025 claim circulated on YouTube suggesting Thailand was introducing a mandatory "vlogger registration" system — **this has been debunked**; no such system exists as of April 2026 (youtube)
