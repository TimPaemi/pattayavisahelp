## UK-specific visa requirements

### Police clearance certificate (when required)

For Non-O-A retirement, Non-O-X, LTR, and some other applications:

- **ACRO Criminal Records Office** — UK's national-level police certificate
- Cost: £55–£90 depending on service speed
- Time: 5–10 working days standard; 24-hour express available
- Apply: https://www.acro.police.uk/police-certificates
- **Validity:** typically 6 months for Thai visa applications

### Document legalisation chain (for foreign marriage, etc.)

Per [G9 Foreign marriage legalisation](./G9-foreign-marriage-legalisation.md):

1. **Apostille** from FCDO Legalisation Office (gov.uk/get-document-legalised) — £30, 2 working days
2. **Royal Thai Embassy London** certification — typically £25, ~1 week
3. **Thai translation** in Pattaya (500–1,500 THB)
4. **Thai MFA legalisation** in Bangkok (200 THB, 2–4 week appointment wait)
5. **Amphoe registration** for marriages → Kor Ror 22

**Total UK chain:** 5–8 weeks, ~£100–150.

### UK State Pension and tax

**Critical UK-specific issues:**

- **State Pension is FROZEN** when you move to Thailand. No annual inflation increases (unlike if you stayed in UK or moved to EU/Commonwealth countries with reciprocal agreements). You receive whatever amount applied at relocation date, forever.
- **Class 2 National Insurance changes from April 2026:** UK expats can no longer pay the cheap Class 2 voluntary contributions (~£180/year). Must pay Class 3 (~£900/year) to maintain State Pension entitlement. **Massive cost increase** for those building state pension while abroad.
- **UK State Pension is taxable in Thailand** when remitted — classified as foreign-source income, no specific exemption under UK-Thailand DTA. See [A1 Thai tax guide](./A1-thai-tax-foreign-residents.md).

### UK pension considerations

- **State Pension:** taxable in Thailand if remitted (Thai tax resident)
- **Civil service / military pensions:** typically remain UK-taxable under DTA (depends on type)
- **Private/SIPP/company pensions:** taxable in Thailand if remitted; UK tax credit available
- **QROPS transfers:** complex; consult specialist (Harrison Brook, MBMG, etc.)

## 8 FAQ questions

**1. Will my UK State Pension increase if I retire in Thailand?**
**No** — Thailand is not on the UK's list of countries where the State Pension increases. Your pension is frozen at the rate when you relocated, forever.

**2. Should I pay voluntary National Insurance from April 2026?**
**Class 2 is being phased out** for most expats from 6 April 2026. Class 3 (~£900/year) replaces it. Worth it if you have <35 qualifying years and want full State Pension.

**3. How much UK pension do I need for a comfortable Pattaya retirement?**
~£1,500–£2,000/month delivers comfortable lifestyle. £1,000–£1,300/month is workable at lower tiers. Many UK retirees combine State Pension with private/SIPP withdrawals.

**4. Do I owe Thai tax on my UK pension?**
**Yes** if you spend 180+ days/year in Thailand AND remit the pension to Thailand. UK-Thailand DTA provides credit for UK tax already paid (where applicable). See [A1](./A1-thai-tax-foreign-residents.md).

**5. Can I keep my UK bank account?**
**Mostly yes**, but UK banks may restrict expat accounts. **HSBC Expat, Lloyds International, Barclays International** offer expat-friendly accounts. Some high street banks close UK accounts for non-resident customers.

**6. NHS — can I still use it?**
On UK visits: **yes** for emergency treatment as a UK national. Routine non-emergency care for non-residents is increasingly limited. Many returning expats use NHS for major procedures (planned visits home).

**7. UK driving licence in Thailand?**
Valid with International Driving Permit for up to 1 year. After that, convert to Thai licence (5-year, 505 THB fee, no driving test) — see [A4](./A4-driving-licence-thailand.md).

**8. Where do most UK retirees live in Pattaya?**
**Jomtien** (largest concentration), **Pratumnak** (premium), **Wong Amat** (quieter premium), **East Pattaya** (budget/family). British concentrations in specific buildings; ask any UK Pattaya expat.
