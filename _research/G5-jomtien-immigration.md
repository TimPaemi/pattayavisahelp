## What it is

Jomtien Immigration Office (officially the **Pattaya Immigration Office**) is the Thai government Immigration facility serving all of Pattaya, Jomtien, Banglamung, Naklua, Pratumnak, and the wider Chonburi province area for visa-related services. It is **the single venue** where every long-stay foreigner in Pattaya handles every immigration appointment — extensions, 90-day reports, re-entry permits, TM30 issues, certificates of residence, and more.

If you live in Pattaya as a foreigner, you will visit this office multiple times per year for the rest of your stay.

## Address and contact

**Address:** Soi 5, Moo 12, Jomtien Beach Road, Nongprue, Banglamung, Chonburi 20150

**Phone:** +66 38 252 750

**Google Maps coordinates:** Search "Pattaya Immigration Office Jomtien Soi 5"

**Note on relocation:** The office moved to the current Jomtien Soi 5 location a few years ago. Older online references may show outdated addresses — always verify the current address before visiting.

## Services available at Jomtien

All services are walk-in basis with a queue ticket. **No appointment system.**

### Visa-related services

- **1-year extension of stay** — Non-O retirement, Non-O marriage, Non-B (work), Non-ED, Non-O-A, Non-O-X (5-year extension), Non-M media
- **90-day extension** — for Non-ED student visa holders (every 90 days)
- **30-day extension** — for tourist visa or visa-exempt entries (one-time, 1,900 THB)
- **Visa conversion** — TM.86 (from tourist visa) or TM.87 (from visa-exempt) to Non-Immigrant O
- **Status verification** — for embassy applications, banks, employers

### Reporting and notifications

- **TM47 90-day reporting** — for any foreigner staying 90+ continuous days on a non-immigrant visa or extension
- **TM30 accommodation notification** — landlord filings, tenant verifications
- **Address change notifications**
- **Lost passport report** — for foreigners whose passport is lost in Thailand

### Permits

- **Re-entry permit (single)** — 1,000 THB
- **Re-entry permit (multiple)** — 3,800 THB
- **Certificate of Residence** — issued for bank account opening, driving licence applications, vehicle registration, etc. Approximately 500 THB **[verify]**

### Other

- **Visa fine payments** — overstays, missed 90-day reports
- **Border-pass services** (limited)
- **Information desk** — general queries (Thai-only at some counters)

## Queue strategy — how to actually get through

Jomtien is one of the busiest Immigration offices in Thailand. Queue management is a real factor in whether your trip succeeds.

### Best days to visit

| Day | Volume | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | 🔴 Busiest | Avoid if possible |
| Tuesday | 🟢 Manageable | Recommended |
| Wednesday | 🟢 Manageable | Recommended |
| Thursday | 🟢 Manageable | Recommended |
| Friday | 🟡 Busy | OK but second-best |
| Day after public holiday | 🔴 Chaotic | Avoid |

### Best time to arrive

- **07:30–08:00** — get there before doors open at 08:30. Queue forms outside.
- **First in line by 07:30** = guaranteed early-morning service, often done by 10:00.
- **Arriving after 09:00** on a Monday or Friday = high risk of all-day wait or being turned away.
- **Avoid lunch hour return:** office closes 11:30–13:00. Don't queue at 11:00 expecting to be seen.

### Queue ticket logic

- Take a ticket from the machine immediately on entry (no need to wait in line first)
- Different ticket types for different services — confirm with information desk if unsure
- Some services use a single queue, others have dedicated counters

### What slows things down

- Missing TM30 receipt → turned away to fix TM30 first
- Missing photocopies → sent to the photocopy shop next door
- Wrong photo size or background colour
- Outdated bank letter (older than the previous day for retirement extensions)
- Spouse not present at marriage extension appointment

## Dress code and behaviour

**Officially:** Jomtien Immigration has posted signs requesting **"appropriate attire"** — no beach clothing, no shorts, no flip-flops, no sleeveless tops.

**In practice:** Officers have turned people away for shorts and beach attire. This is not arbitrary harassment — it's a documented requirement.

**Recommended dress:**

- Long trousers or smart casual skirt
- Collared shirt (men) or modest top (women)
- Closed-toe shoes
- Tidy appearance overall

**Why it matters:** Some officers take this seriously, especially for first-time interactions. Wearing beach attire signals a tourist mindset; smart-casual signals you're a legitimate long-stay resident who respects the process.

## What to bring (universal checklist)

For any Jomtien appointment, bring:

1. **Passport** — original, in good condition
2. **Photocopies, each signed** — biodata page, current visa/extension, latest entry stamp, all relevant pages
3. **TM30 receipt** — proof of accommodation, increasingly required at every appointment
4. **Recent passport photos** — 2–4 photos, 4×6 cm, white background, taken within 6 months
5. **Specific documents for your service type** (see per-service guide below)
6. **Cash** — most fees are cash-only; bring 2,000–10,000 THB depending on service
7. **Pen** — forms must be filled out by hand
8. **Patience** — typical visit 1–3 hours

**Photocopy shops:** Multiple shops just outside Jomtien Immigration offer 1–2 THB per page photocopies if you forget anything. Don't rely on them — bring your own copies.

## Per-service process flow

### 1-year extension (Non-O retirement / marriage / Non-B)

1. Arrive 07:30, take ticket
2. Submit TM.7 form, passport, bank book, bank letter, TM30, photos, supporting documents at counter
3. Officer reviews, may ask for supervisor approval
4. Pay 1,900 THB cash
5. Receive 1-year extension stamp same day (typically 1–3 hours total)

### 90-day TM47 reporting

1. Take ticket for 90-day reporting
2. Submit TM.47 form, passport, copies, previous 90-day slip
3. Officer prints new receipt with next due date
4. **No fee.** Typically 15–60 minutes.

### Re-entry permit

1. Take ticket (often combined with extension day)
2. Fill out TM.8 form, submit passport, photo, copies
3. Pay 1,000 THB (single) or 3,800 THB (multiple)
4. Re-entry stamp added to passport same day

### Certificate of Residence

1. Take information-desk ticket
2. Request Certificate of Residence
3. Submit passport, copies, address proof (TM30)
4. Pay ~500 THB **[verify]**
5. Certificate issued same-day or 1–2 working days

### TM.86 / TM.87 conversion (tourist → Non-O)

1. Bring 800k THB seasoned bank deposit (for retirement) or 400k THB (for marriage)
2. Submit conversion form, passport, financial proof, supporting documents
3. Officer forwards to Bangkok HQ for approval
4. Processing: ~15 working days
5. Returns for collection, receive 90-day Non-O stamp
6. Then apply for 1-year extension within last 30 days

## Practical Pattaya tips

### Getting to Jomtien Immigration

- **Driving:** Soi 5 off Jomtien Beach Road; parking available on site (limited)
- **Songthaew (baht bus):** Jomtien Beach Road route; ask driver for "Soi 5 Immigration"
- **Bolt / Grab taxi:** ~150–250 THB from central Pattaya; ~80–150 THB from Jomtien
- **Walking from Jomtien Beach hotels:** 10–20 minutes from beachfront

### What's nearby (for the wait)

- **Coffee shops** — multiple options on Jomtien Beach Road and surrounding sois
- **Photocopy shops** — directly outside the office, 1–2 THB per page
- **Photo studios** — passport photos taken on-site, ~150 THB for 4 prints
- **7-Eleven** — within walking distance, cash and snacks
- **Restaurants** — Jomtien Beach Road has many options for breakfast/lunch

### Currency

- All government fees are **cash only** (Thai baht)
- Bring exact change where possible — counters may not have change for 1,000 THB notes
- ATMs nearby but don't rely on them

### Language

- Most counter officers handle English at basic-to-intermediate level
- Complex situations may benefit from a Thai-speaking friend or visa agent
- Information desk usually has English-capable staff
- Forms are in Thai and English

## Common pitfalls

1. **Showing up after queue tickets stop being issued** (~14:30–15:00). Same-day service no longer guaranteed.
2. **Arriving without TM30 receipt.** Turned away for nearly all extension types.
3. **Wearing shorts, flip-flops, beach attire.** Documented refusals.
4. **Outdated bank letter** for retirement extensions. Some officers want it dated the same day or previous day.
5. **Missing photocopies.** Doable to fix on site at the photocopy shop, but adds delay.
6. **Coming on a public holiday or day after.** Office closed or chaotically busy.
7. **Bringing a Thai-speaking friend who doesn't know the process.** Better to bring your documents prepared and your own questions written down.
8. **Trying to convert to Non-O at Jomtien when you're not a Chonburi-registered resident.** Jurisdiction is by registered address; non-Chonburi residents must use their local Immigration office.
9. **Assuming online TM47 = no Jomtien visit needed.** Some online TM47 attempts fail and require an in-person follow-up.

## 12 FAQ questions

**1. Where exactly is Jomtien Immigration Office?**
Soi 5, Moo 12, Jomtien Beach Road, Nongprue, Banglamung, Chonburi 20150. About 10–20 minutes from central Pattaya by car or taxi.

**2. What are the opening hours?**
Monday–Friday, 08:30–11:30 and 13:00–16:30, with a lunch break 11:30–13:00. Closed weekends and Thai public holidays.

**3. Do I need an appointment?**
No — Jomtien is walk-in only. Take a queue ticket on entry. Queue numbers stop ~2:30–3:00 PM.

**4. What's the best time to go?**
Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday, arriving 07:30–08:00 before doors open. Avoid Mondays and the day after public holidays.

**5. Can I do a 90-day report and an extension on the same visit?**
Yes — many Pattaya retirees combine these when timing aligns. You'll need to take a queue ticket for each service.

**6. Can I leave my documents and pick them up later?**
For most services (extensions, 90-day reports, re-entry permits), processing is same-day. Some conversion processes (TM.86/87) take ~15 working days and require a return visit.

**7. What if my appointment runs into lunch?**
Office closes 11:30–13:00. If your queue number is called and you're not there, you may forfeit your spot. Plan ahead — start early.

**8. Can I pay by card?**
No — all fees are cash only (Thai baht). Bring exact change where possible.

**9. Can I bring a Thai-speaking friend or agent?**
Yes — many do. For complex situations (marriage extension home photo verification, Non-B with employer queries), a knowledgeable Thai friend or agent can save hours.

**10. What if I'm denied at the counter?**
Most denials are document-related and fixable. Officers typically tell you what's missing — go fix it (TM30, photo, photocopy) and return. Some denials require a more senior officer's involvement; ask politely for clarification.

**11. Is there parking?**
Limited parking on-site, often full by 09:00. Songthaew (baht bus) along Jomtien Beach Road is the easier option. Bolt/Grab works too.

**12. How do I get a Certificate of Residence from Jomtien?**
Take a ticket for the Certificate of Residence service. Submit passport, copies, and address proof (TM30 or rental agreement). Pay approximately 500 THB. Certificate issued same-day or 1–2 working days.
