## Who it's for

The Thai Marriage Visa is officially the Non-Immigrant O visa based on family reasons (marriage to a Thai national). It is for foreign nationals legally married to a Thai citizen who wish to reside in Thailand long-term alongside their spouse. It is also available to foreigners married to another foreigner who holds a Non-B work visa in Thailand (dependent spousal route) — though in this case no work permit rights are granted to the dependent.

**This is one of the most scrutinised visa categories in the Thai immigration system,** as officers actively investigate the authenticity of marriages and conduct unannounced home visits in suspected sham-marriage cases. (rlcoutsourcing+1, youtube)

## Eligibility (full list)

- Must be **legally married to a Thai national** — proven via Thai marriage registration (thailandlawonline+1)
- For marriages conducted outside Thailand: the foreign marriage certificate must be **apostilled or certified by the Thai Embassy** in the issuing country, then officially translated into Thai and registered at an Amphoe (district office) — a Kor Ror 22 form is issued (issacompass+1)
- Passport must be valid for at least 6 months (some embassies require more) (pretoria.thaiembassy+1)
- Must hold a valid Non-Immigrant O visa at the time of applying for the 1-year extension (thaiembassy+1)
- Must meet one of the financial requirements (see Financial Requirements) (thailandlawonline+1)
- Must reside at a registered Thai address — TM30 filed, address registered on Thai spouse's Tabien Baan (house registration) or with a declared address (thailandlawonline)
- No record of visa overstays, criminal convictions in Thailand, or previous deportation (thailandlawonline)
- **Genuine cohabiting relationship** — Immigration reserves the right to investigate, interview, and conduct home visits (youtube, siam-attorney, legal)
- No minimum age (but marriage itself must be legally valid under Thai law — minimum age 17 with parental consent) **[verify]**

## Required documents

**Core documents for Non-O initial application (at Thai Embassy abroad):**

1. Valid passport — minimum 6 months' validity, copies of biodata page and all relevant stamp pages (siam-attorney+1)
2. Completed Thai visa application form (online via e-Visa portal or physical form at embassy) (rlcoutsourcing)
3. Two passport-sized photographs — 4×6 cm, white background, taken within 6 months (rlcoutsourcing)
4. **Thai marriage certificate** — original Kor Ror 2 (marriage registration) and Kor Ror 3 (marriage certificate), with copies (thaivisaexpert+1)
5. For foreign marriages: legalised/apostilled marriage certificate + Thai translation certified by a licensed translator (issacompass)
6. Thai spouse's ID card — copy (siam-attorney+1)
7. Thai spouse's passport — copy (rlcoutsourcing)
8. Thai spouse's house registration (Tabien Baan) — copy (siam-attorney+1)
9. Financial proof — bank statement or income documentation meeting thresholds (see Financial Requirements) (thaiembassy+1)

**Additional documents for annual 1-year extension at Jomtien Immigration:**

1. TM.7 Extension of Stay form (thailandlawonline)
2. TM.30 address registration receipt (thailandlawonline)
3. **Photos of the couple together** — inside the home, outside the home, and family photos (siam-attorney)
4. **Map / hand-drawn directions to your residence** (siam-attorney+1)
5. Bank book (original) showing 400,000 THB balance, with copies (siam-attorney)
6. Bank guarantee letter (from your Thai bank, confirming balance) (siam-attorney)
7. Bank statement — full statements covering the required seasoning period (siam-attorney)
8. **Thai spouse's presence** — spouse must attend in person on the day of the extension appointment at most offices (issacompass)
9. Copy of Affirmation of Freedom to Marry (if married in Thailand — issued by your home country's embassy prior to the marriage) (thaivisaexpert)

## Financial requirements (THB and USD, current 2026 figures)

| Method | Amount | Seasoning | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bank deposit | 400,000 THB (~$11,250) | 2 months before application (some offices require 3 — check Jomtien's current rule) (asq) | In applicant's name only (asq) |
| Monthly income | 40,000 THB/month (~$1,125/month) | 12 months' documented history | Letter from embassy verifying income required (thaiembassy) |
| Combination | Deposit + annual income = 400,000 THB total | — | Less commonly used; verify acceptance at Jomtien **[verify]** |

**Key rules on the 400,000 THB deposit:**

- Must be in a Thai bank account in the **applicant's name only** — not a joint account (asq)
- Must be **seasoned for the official minimum of 2 months** before the extension application date — the police order is explicit on this (asq+1)
- Some offices (including reports from Ratchaburi and others) enforce 3 months — Jomtien's current standard should be confirmed locally (asq)
- After submitting the extension application and receiving the "under consideration" stamp, there is no legal obligation to maintain the balance — though keeping it in until the stamp is finalised is strongly advised (asq)
- **There is no mandatory mid-year floor on marriage extensions** (unlike the retirement visa's 400k floor throughout the year) — the rule is purely about the seasoning period before application (asq)

## Application process — step by step

### Path A: Applying from outside Thailand

1. Gather marriage certificate, financial proof, and spouse documents (thaiembassy+1)
2. Apply for Non-Immigrant O visa (marriage) at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your home country — via e-Visa portal or in person (thaiembassy)
3. Receive 90-day single entry Non-O visa (or sometimes a 1-year multiple-entry Non-O at some posts) (thailandlawonline)
4. Enter Thailand, open Thai bank account, deposit 400,000 THB, season for 2–3 months (thaiembassy)
5. Within the final 30 days of your 90-day permission to stay, apply for 1-year extension of stay at Jomtien Immigration with full document package (thaiembassy)
6. Pay 1,900 THB extension fee (thailandlawonline)
7. Receive 1-year permission-to-stay stamp (thailandlawonline)

### Path B: Converting from inside Thailand (tourist/visa-exempt)

1. If in Thailand on a tourist visa or visa-exempt entry, marry your Thai partner at an Amphoe (district office) (thailandlawonline)
2. File a TM.86 form (if on tourist visa) or TM.87 form (if on visa exemption) to convert to Non-Immigrant O status — done at Bangkok Immigration Bureau or in some cases at regional offices (thaivisaexpert)
3. Once Non-O status granted, proceed to gather documents and apply for 1-year extension as above (thaivisaexpert)

## Where to apply (consulate vs in-Thailand)

**Initial Non-O visa:** Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your country of nationality or legal residence, or via the Thai e-Visa portal. Some applicants apply at Thai embassies in neighbouring countries (Vientiane, Penang, Phnom Penh) — though increasing scrutiny means home-country applications are more reliable. (siam-legal+2)

**Annual 1-year extension:** At the Thai Immigration office covering your registered address. For Pattaya residents: **Jomtien Immigration Office, Chonburi**. Jurisdiction is strictly by registered address — you cannot extend at an office outside your province. (issacompass)

## Duration & extensions

- **Initial Non-O visa:** 90 days per entry (single or multiple-entry, depending on embassy) (thailandlawonline)
- **1-year extension of stay:** Renewable annually, indefinitely, as long as marriage continues and financial requirements are met (thailandlawonline)
- **No maximum:** The extension can be renewed every year without limit for the duration of the marriage (thailandlawonline)
- **Divorce or death of Thai spouse:** Extension ceases to be available; holder must immediately switch to a different visa category or depart (thailandlawonline)
- **Work permit eligibility:** Once on a 1-year extension based on marriage to a Thai national, the holder is **eligible to apply for a work permit** — a unique and significant advantage over most long-stay visas (rlcoutsourcing)

## 90-day reporting applicability

**Yes — mandatory.** TM47 90-day address reports are required for all marriage visa extension holders staying 90+ consecutive days. Options:

- Online via immigration.go.th (15 days before to 7 days after due date)
- In person at Jomtien Immigration — typically very quick with correct paperwork
- By post (registered mail)

**Penalty:** Up to 5,000 THB per missed report (note — the marriage visa FAQ sources cite 5,000 THB, versus the 2,000 THB standard; the legal maximum is 5,000 THB — **verify current enforcement at Jomtien**). Re-entering Thailand resets the 90-day clock. (issacompass)

## Re-entry permit need + cost

**Yes — required.** Exiting Thailand while a 1-year extension is active without a re-entry permit cancels the extension permanently. Purchase before departure:

- **Single re-entry:** 1,000 THB — at Jomtien Immigration
- **Multiple re-entry:** 3,800 THB — valid until extension expires

The re-entry permit can also be purchased at international airport immigration counters, but doing so at Jomtien before travel is safer and avoids check-in stress. (thailandlawonline)

## Common pitfalls

1. **Unseasoned bank funds — most common extension rejection cause:** Depositing 400,000 THB one week before the extension appointment. The bank book is examined cover to cover. A lump-sum deposit with a very recent date stamp is an immediate red flag. Keep the money sitting untouched for the full 2–3 months required by your local office. (asq+1)
2. **Spouse not attending on extension day:** At Jomtien and most regional offices, the Thai spouse must be physically present on the day of the extension application. Applicants who show up alone are routinely turned away — even if all documents are perfect. Confirm Jomtien's current rule before scheduling. (issacompass)
3. **Foreign marriage certificate not legalised:** If you married outside Thailand, handing over an untranslated, non-apostilled foreign marriage certificate at Immigration is an automatic rejection. The certificate must be apostilled (or certified by the Thai Embassy in the issuing country) and professionally translated into Thai. This process takes 4–8 weeks and must be done before applying. (issacompass)
4. **Home address not matching registered address:** Your TM30 receipt and your Tabien Baan (house registration) address must match your actual living address. Immigration conducts random home visits for marriage visa holders — officers have knocked on doors at the registered address and found nobody living there, resulting in non-renewal. (youtube, siam-attorney)
5. **Sham marriage scrutiny — unannounced home visits:** Thai Immigration runs active checks on marriage visa holders, especially in areas with high concentrations of foreign expats (including Pattaya). Officers visit the registered home address, ask neighbours about the couple, review photos at the appointment, and sometimes interview spouses separately. (legal, youtube, siam-attorney)
6. **Applying at the wrong Immigration office:** Jurisdiction for extensions is determined by your registered address, not where you happen to be. A Pattaya resident registered in Chonburi must extend at Jomtien — not at the Bangkok Immigration Bureau. Showing up at the wrong office wastes a day and accomplishes nothing. (issacompass)
7. **Marriage only registered abroad, not recognised in Thailand:** Some couples marry overseas and never register the marriage in Thailand. The Thai Amphoe registration is essential — without it, Thai Immigration does not recognise the marriage. The Kor Ror 2 and Kor Ror 3 documents are non-negotiable proof. (thaiembassy+1)
8. **Using a joint bank account:** The 400,000 THB must be in a sole account in the applicant's name only. A joint account with your Thai spouse does not satisfy the requirement. (asq)

## Pattaya-specific considerations

- **Jomtien Immigration Office** handles all marriage extension matters for Chonburi province residents (thailandlawonline)
- **Spouse attendance confirmed at Jomtien:** Anecdotal reports from expat forums consistently confirm that Jomtien requires the Thai spouse to be present on extension day — confirm this requirement before every appointment as rules can change (youtube)
- **Home visit risk in Pattaya:** Pattaya is a well-known area for sham marriages. Immigration officers are reportedly more active in conducting home checks in Banglamung (greater Pattaya area) than in many other provinces. Genuine couples should have no issue — but ensure your registered address is where you actually live, and that your home looks like a shared residence (legal, youtube)
- **TM30 at Jomtien:** Marriage visa holders must have a valid TM30 on file. If renting privately, ensure your landlord has filed it — if not, it can be filed at Jomtien directly. Some condo buildings (particularly expat-heavy condos in Jomtien and Pratumnak) have building managers who handle TM30 as standard practice **[verify]**
- **Recommended documents for Pattaya application:** Bring 5–10 recent dated photographs of you and your spouse at your home — informal shots showing day-to-day life, family gatherings, etc. are more convincing than posed photos. Include photos showing the interior of your shared home (siam-attorney)
- **Local marriage visa agents in Pattaya:** Several agents on Sukhumvit Road and Jomtien Beach Road assist with marriage extensions, spouse attendance coordination, and document preparation. Particularly useful for first-time applicants navigating the photo and home-evidence requirements (siam-attorney)

## FAQ (12 real questions from Reddit r/Thailand, ThaiVisa forum, Quora)

**1. Do I need to be married before entering Thailand to get a marriage visa?**
No. You can enter on a tourist visa or visa-exempt entry, marry your Thai partner at an Amphoe, then convert to Non-Immigrant O status using TM.86 or TM.87 forms. The marriage must be legally registered in Thailand before you can apply. (thailandlawonline)

**2. How much money do I need for the Thailand marriage visa?**
400,000 THB in a Thai bank account in your name only, seasoned for a minimum of 2 months before your extension application. Alternatively, 40,000 THB/month in documented income. (asq+1)

**3. Does my Thai wife need to attend my visa extension appointment?**
At most Thai Immigration offices, including Jomtien, yes — your Thai spouse must be present on extension day. Confirm this requirement with Jomtien before your appointment, as failure to bring your spouse means being turned away. (issacompass)

**4. Can I work in Thailand on a marriage visa?**
Yes — once you hold a 1-year extension of stay based on marriage to a Thai national, you are eligible to apply for a work permit. This is one of the key advantages of this visa type. (rlcoutsourcing)

**5. What happens if my marriage ends in divorce?**
Your visa basis ceases to exist. You must switch to another visa category or depart Thailand. There is no grace period explicitly guaranteed in the rules — consult an immigration lawyer if facing this situation. (thailandlawonline)

**6. We married in the UK. Does my UK marriage certificate work?**
Only if legalised. It must be apostilled by the UK Foreign Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO), then certified at the Royal Thai Embassy in London, then professionally translated into Thai and registered at a Thai Amphoe. An uncertified UK marriage certificate alone is rejected. (issacompass)

**7. Will Immigration check whether our marriage is real?**
Yes. Thai Immigration conducts random and scheduled home visits — particularly in Pattaya and other tourist-heavy areas — to verify that couples genuinely cohabit. Officers may also interview neighbours or call your Thai spouse separately. Bring genuine evidence of shared life at every extension. (youtube, siam-attorney)

**8. Can I do a border run instead of renewing my annual extension?**
No — this is not viable in 2026. Since the crackdown on repeated visa-exempt entries (maximum 2 per calendar year), border runs cannot substitute for annual extensions. The 1-year extension route is the only sustainable path for long-term residents. (geosthai)

**9. Can I use the income method instead of the bank deposit?**
Yes — 40,000 THB/month in verifiable income qualifies. You'll need a letter from your home country's embassy confirming your income, plus 12 months of bank statements showing consistent deposits. In practice, the bank deposit method is more straightforward for most applicants. (thaiembassy)

**10. How does the marriage visa compare to the retirement visa?**
The marriage visa requires 400,000 THB (versus 800,000 THB for retirement), but involves significantly more scrutiny — home visits, spouse attendance, photo evidence. Retirement is administratively simpler. If you're over 50 and married to a Thai national, you can choose either — retirement is often described as "easier to maintain." (legal)

**11. What documents prove our house is shared?**
Photos inside the home, utility bills in both names or at the shared address, rental agreements, items belonging to both partners visible in photos, and your Thai spouse's Tabien Baan listing the shared address. (siam-attorney)

**12. How long can I stay in Thailand on a marriage visa?**
The initial Non-O visa gives 90 days. After extending to a 1-year permission to stay, you can remain in Thailand continuously (with 90-day reporting) and renew annually indefinitely as long as the marriage is valid and financial requirements are met. (thailandlawonline)

## What changed in 2025–2026

- **Sham marriage crackdowns intensified:** Reports from Pattaya, Phuket, and Bangkok confirm increased frequency of unannounced home visits and immigration officer interviews in 2025–2026. Officers are more systematically documenting couple cohabitation evidence at annual extension appointments. (legal, youtube)
- **Stricter enforcement of spouse presence at extensions:** While the rule has always existed in practice, 2025–2026 has seen far fewer exceptions made for absent Thai spouses at Jomtien and other provincial offices. (youtube)
- **Border run era over:** The 2024–2025 enforcement of the 2 visa-exempt entries per calendar year limit effectively ended marriage-visa-by-border-run as a strategy. All marriage residents must now maintain proper annual extensions. (geosthai)
- **TM6 paper arrival card abolished:** The digital arrival card (TDAC) pre-registration requirement now applies to all arrivals including marriage visa holders. (visahq)
- **Visa code consolidation (August 2025):** The Non-O marriage category is preserved under the consolidated visa code structure. (thailand.prd)
- **Foreign marriage certificate legalisation scrutiny increased:** Several immigration offices and embassy posts have reported stricter enforcement of apostille/certification requirements for foreign marriage certificates — documents that were accepted informally in 2022–2023 are now routinely rejected without full legalisation. (issacompass)
