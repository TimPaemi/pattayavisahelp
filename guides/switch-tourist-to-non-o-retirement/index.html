How to switch from Tourist to Non-O Retirement

Convert your Tourist or visa-exempt entry to a 1-year Retirement Non-O without leaving Thailand. Step-by-step process at Chaeng Wattana.

Updated 10 May 2026·Pattaya Visa Help·Independent · No commissions
Transition guide · 2026 2 min read·Updated April 2026

Who this is for

Aged 50+. Currently in Thailand on Tourist TR visa or visa-exempt entry. Want to stay on a 1-year Retirement Non-O without flying home.

The 30-second answer

You can convert in-country at Chaeng Wattana Immigration Office in Bangkok (or some regional offices). You need ฿800,000 seasoned 2 months in a Thai bank account. Process takes 3-4 visits over 2 months. Fee: ฿2,000 application + ฿1,900 extension.

Step-by-step process

  1. Open Thai bank account while on Tourist visa. Banks have tightened recently — Bangkok Bank and Kasikorn (KBank) most flexible. Bring passport, address proof, sometimes need a Thai sponsor letter.
  2. Transfer ฿800,000+ from abroad using SWIFT. Get FET (Foreign Exchange Transaction) form from receiving bank.
  3. Wait 2 months minimum for the deposit to "season." Bank statement must show ฿800k+ continuously.
  4. Visit Chaeng Wattana Immigration Bangkok Building B, Section: Long Stay (foreigners 50+). Submit:
    • Passport (currently in Tourist or visa-exempt status)
    • TM86 application form
    • Bank book + bank letter (within 7 days of submission)
    • 2 passport photos
    • ฿2,000 fee
  5. Receive 90-day Non-O stamp same-day or next-visit.
  6. Within 60 days, return to immigration to apply for the 1-year extension. Fee ฿1,900. Now you have 1-year Retirement Non-O.

Common pitfalls

  • Trying at Pattaya/Jomtien immigration first — they may refer you to Chaeng Wattana Bangkok for first-time conversions. Bangkok handles new conversions; Pattaya handles routine extensions.
  • Insufficient bank seasoning — need 2 months. Don\'t submit at week 6.
  • Joint account — must be in YOUR name only.
  • Foreign currency deposit — convert to THB savings, not FCD.

Alternative: convert via Vientiane

Some retirees prefer to fly to Vientiane (Laos), apply for Non-O at the Thai Embassy there (1-2 day processing), then return on the new Non-O visa. Bypasses the Chaeng Wattana queue. Costs ~฿15k-฿25k extra (flight, hotel, visa fee). Read our embassy directory.
